## Verdict: 🟡 INVESTIGATE

Greenville has a strong demand floor — population up ~1.9%/yr, a tight 5–6% vacancy,
rising incomes, and a genuinely funded growth catalyst in the Isuzu plant. What holds
it back from a clean PURSUE is the middle of the curve: job growth has cooled to ~0.7%
in the most recent 12-month read, rent growth is moderate (2–3%), and the construction
pipeline is active enough to compete for renters during a hold. This is a **buy-the-
right-submarket-at-the-right-basis** market, not a rising-tide-lifts-all market.

**What would move it to PURSUE:** (1) a target submarket with vacancy under 5% and a
rent-growth trend above the metro, and (2) confirmation the Isuzu jobs and EDC pipeline
land on schedule (2027 operations).

## Key Signals
- **Strongest:** Isuzu North America committing a **$280M plant + 700 jobs** (ops 2027),
  on top of an economic-development pipeline targeting $100M and 1,000+ jobs over 5 years.
- **Biggest risk:** Job growth decelerated to **+0.7% (Oct 2023→Oct 2024)** — well off the
  multi-year pace. A value-add hold needs employment to keep absorbing rent increases.
- **Path of Progress:** Strong and funded — Isuzu plant, Gateway ($7.26M city investment,
  327+ units), and the 90-acre Bolden Street redevelopment on Laurens Road.

## Why Is Greenville Growing?

- **Reindustrialization of the Upstate.** Manufacturing (11% of jobs) anchored by BMW's
  regional supply chain, now adding Isuzu's $280M commercial-truck plant (700 jobs, 2027).
- **In-migration.** Greenville County added ~11,000 residents in a single year (2023→2024),
  with ~5,900 from net domestic migration — affordability relative to Charlotte and Atlanta
  is pulling households in.
- **Healthcare anchor.** Health care & social assistance is the #1 employment sector (15%
  of jobs), led by Prisma Health — a stabilizing, recession-resistant base.
- **Risk to the thesis:** The same cooling that shows in the 0.7% recent job print could
  persist if manufacturing capex slows before Isuzu ramps. Watch quarterly employment.
