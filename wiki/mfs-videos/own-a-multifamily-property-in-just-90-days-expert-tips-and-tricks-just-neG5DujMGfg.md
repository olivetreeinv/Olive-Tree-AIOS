## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches the complete process for acquiring multifamily properties (5+ units) within 90 days by leveraging other people's money (OPM). He breaks down the five pillars: finding deals, analyzing deals, raising capital, closing escrow, and asset management. The core strategy involves identifying off-market deals through broker relationships, structuring deals to minimize personal capital (demonstrated with an 18K down payment on a $2.15M deal), and raising investor equity to control larger properties.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Identify 3+ brokers per market, tour on-market properties, then ask brokers for off-market deals - this leverage builds relationships to access off-market inventory where real deals exist
2. Target 8-20 unit deals as entry point; avoid 50+ unit deals as first investment because you'll lack execution capability
3. Underwrite deals requiring 14-20% annual returns (IRR) and 6-10% cash-on-cash return - if deal doesn't meet this, don't proceed
4. Structure capital as 10% GP contribution + 90% raised investor equity; this keeps personal cash minimal while maintaining control and 40%+ profit split
5. General Partners earn acquisition fees (3% of purchase price) at close and 40%+ of deal profits while investors receive 14-20% annual returns
6. Lead deal sourcing with: deal → debt (70% bank loan) → equity (30% investor capital), not the reverse; money follows good deals
7. Use 'soft commitments' by changing your tagline to 'I buy and sell apartments with investors around the country' - this attracts capital before you have a deal ready
