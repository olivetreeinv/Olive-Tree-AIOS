## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches a comprehensive framework for buying 10-50 unit multifamily properties using minimal personal capital ($10-30K) combined with OPM (other people's money). He demonstrates how to source deals via brokers, analyze them using NOI and IRR metrics, structure syndications with investors, and execute using a five-pillar method: sourcing, analyzing, financing, closing, and managing.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use the 'direct + broker' strategy: leverage on-market deals to access 60% pre-market/off-market inventory that brokers hold
2. On a $1M deal, 10-unit property: bring 10% equity ($30K), get 70% bank loan ($700K), raise remaining 20% ($270K) from 5 investors via pitch deck
3. Every $1,000 monthly NOI increase adds $220,000 in property value (divide by cap rate)
4. Target deal metrics: 14-20% IRR, 8% cash-on-cash preferred return, 1.8-2.0x equity multiple minimum to raise capital
5. Acquisition fee: 2-3% of purchase price paid at close; asset management fee 1-2% of annual revenue; 30-40% of monthly profits; disposition fee 0.5-2% at exit
6. Start with 10-30 unit deals first (not 50+) to break in with mom-and-pop sellers and lenders willing to work with newer investors
7. Write a one-page vision letter in three sections (personal, professional, health/spirituality) dated one year forward, read daily—Justin crossed off 90% of his prior year goals
