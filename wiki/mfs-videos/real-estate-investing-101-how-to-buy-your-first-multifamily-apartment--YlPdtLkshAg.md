## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches the fundamentals of buying multifamily apartments (5+ units) as a value-add investment strategy. He covers deal sourcing through brokers, basic financial analysis using NOI and cap rates, and deal structuring with minimal out-of-pocket capital by raising investor money. While comprehensive, the content is foundational education rather than specific to Brian's 15-50 unit value-add focus in GA/TN/AL.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Access 60% pre-market off-market deals by building broker relationships on 10-30 unit on-market deals before scaling to 50+
2. Target 14-20%+ IRR, 6-10%+ cash-on-cash, 1.8x+ equity multiple as deal thresholds
3. NOI formula: Gross Potential Income minus economic loss (12-18% currently) plus other income minus property expenses = Net Operating Income
4. Cap rate = NOI / Property Value; every $1,000/month NOI increase = ~$220,000 value increase at 5.5% cap rate
5. Raise 90% equity from investors by bringing 10% yourself, getting 2-3% acquisition fee that returns most capital at close
6. For first deal, keep deal size 10-30 units to access easier financing, broker relationships, and mom-and-pop sellers
7. Make money 5-8 ways: acquisition fees (2-3%), asset management (1-2% revenue), 30-40% cash flow/profits, tax benefits (bonus depreciation), construction fees (5-8%), disposition fees (0.5-2%), and double-dipping as GP investor
