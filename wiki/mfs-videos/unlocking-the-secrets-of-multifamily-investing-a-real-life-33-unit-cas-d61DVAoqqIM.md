## Summary
Justin Brennan provides an in-depth walkthrough of analyzing a 33-unit Class C apartment complex (The Oasis) in Phoenix, Arizona listed at $7 million. The session covers how to read operating memorandums, stress-test financial projections, understand debt coverage ratios, and use proformas to determine if a deal makes sense regardless of asking price. Key insight: commercial multifamily financing is based on property financials (not borrower financials), which changes deal analysis fundamentally.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Five critical steps to buying apartments: finding deals, analyzing properly, raising capital (debt + equity), closing escrow, and asset management
2. 5+ units threshold: banks underwrite on property financials, not borrower financials; non-recourse loans where property (not you) pays mortgage
3. Properties valued as: NOI ÷ cap rate = property value; buy at higher cap rate, renovate/increase rents to lower cap rate, sell at lower cap rate for profit spread
4. Stress test using debt service coverage ratio (DCR) minimum 1.25x: NOI must be 125% of annual debt payments or bank won't finance
5. Expense ratio: target 30-31% of effective gross income (EGI) for Class C properties; verify property taxes and insurance with local brokers/assessors
6. Interest-only loan periods (2-3 years minimum): prioritizes investor cash flow over principal paydown since property sold in 3-5 year hold
7. Cost segregation study ($5-8k): accelerates building depreciation over 3-5 years instead of 27.5 years, creating paper losses that offset capital gains/ordinary income for tax professionals
8. Only buy in top 75 MSAs (Metropolitan Statistical Areas) with strong job/population growth; avoid tertiary markets that collapse during recessions
9. Current market metrics for Phoenix: 5-6% cap rate range, 5.75-6.5% interest rates, $100-200k per unit acquisition prices, target 2%+ cap rate spread between acquisition and disposition
