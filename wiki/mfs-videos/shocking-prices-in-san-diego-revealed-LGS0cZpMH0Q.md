## Summary
Justin Brennan interviews San Diego-based flipper/contractor Brian Daily about single-family flipping operations, market dynamics, and renovation best practices. While Brian discusses deal sourcing through agents and wholesalers, market analysis (supply/inventory trends), and construction management, the content focuses on single-family flipping (120-140 day hold periods, $85-100/sq ft renovation costs) rather than the 15-50 unit multifamily value-add deals that Brian Norton targets in GA/TN/AL. The video touches on multifamily briefly only as an aside.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Hard money lenders charge 10-11% interest rates plus 1-2 points; daily carry costs compound pressure on timeline
2. Use 'annualized return' metric instead of net/gross margin to account for time-value of money; target 30% annualized cash-on-cash minimum
3. Budget renovation costs at $85-100/sq ft for full interior work; expect cost inflation (was $30-35/sq ft when started, now 3x higher in San Diego)
4. Economic obsolescence (external factors: busy road, freeway proximity) reduces buyer pool by ~30% immediately; functional obsolescence (interior issues) is fixable but economic obsolescence kills demand
5. Complete detailed scope of work upfront before contractor mobilization to prevent change orders and cost overruns; stack subs and use just-in-time material delivery
6. Partner with experienced operators if new to flipping rather than learning execution live; find someone who's done 'hundreds of flips' and co-invest with them managing the deal/capital side
7. Require 12 months operating reserves before flipping; stress of illiquid capital without cash buffer severely impacts decision-making and sleep quality
8. Market timing is critical: appreciation covers mistakes for beginning operators; in flat/declining markets, execution must be near-perfect to avoid losses
