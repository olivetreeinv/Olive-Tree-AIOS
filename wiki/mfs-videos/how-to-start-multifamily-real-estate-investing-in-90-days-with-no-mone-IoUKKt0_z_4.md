## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches a foundational 90-day framework for entering multifamily real estate with minimal capital by leveraging syndication, OPM (other people's money), and the five-pillar acquisition method. While the content emphasizes mindset, community, and wealth-building philosophy, it includes deal-specific mechanics relevant to Brian's 15-50 unit target market, though deal analysis and sourcing tactics receive less depth than motivational framing.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Multifamily returns come from 5-8 revenue streams: acquisition fees (2-3% of purchase price), asset management fees (1-2% of revenue monthly), construction management fees (2-8% of hard costs), cash flow (30-40% of profits), disposition fees (0.5-2% of sale price), refinance pulls, and tax writeoffs
2. Good deals require: IRR 14-20%+, cash-on-cash return 6-10%+, equity multiple 1.8x or higher—all three metrics must be met or the deal is rejected
3. Only 40% of multifamily deals appear on public websites (LoopNet, CoStar, CoStarKy.com); 60%+ trade pre-market off-market through broker databases—leverage on-market tours to build broker relationships and access off-market pipeline
4. Acquire 10-30 unit deals first as a novice (not 50+) because you'll encounter mom-and-pop sellers and lenders willing to work with new operators; 50+ units require proven track record and sophisticated partners
5. Put down 5-10% of total equity requirement ($188k out-of-pocket on a $2.15M deal was typical); acquisition fees offset initial capital (e.g., 30k in, 20k fee out = 10k true cost)
6. Case study: 31-unit building purchased for $1.525M, renovated to $3.6M appraised value within 24 months, achieved 100% cash-out refinance, generated $590k profit on $188k out-of-pocket through refinance pull + asset fees + cash flow + disposition
7. The game requires partnerships and OPM—'the more hands you shake the more money you make'; solitary operators cannot scale beyond a few properties without learning to raise capital
