## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches the five pillars of multifamily investing: finding deals, analyzing deals, financing, closing, and managing. He emphasizes that deals must meet three key metrics (14-20% IRR, 6-10%+ cash-on-cash, 1.8x+ equity multiple) to successfully raise capital. For Brian's 15-50 unit focus in GA/TN/AL, the deal analysis framework directly addresses his underwriting drag by providing concrete thresholds for deal evaluation.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use brokers' on-market deals to access their pre-market inventory (60%+ of best deals not on websites); cold-call 3 brokers in chosen market to build relationships
2. For every $1,000 monthly NOI increase, property value rises $220,000 using formula: Value = NOI / Cap Rate
3. First deal must be 10-30 units (not 50+) to access mom-and-pop brokers who return calls and easier debt/capital raise; scale up after proving track record
4. Deal must meet ALL THREE metrics simultaneously: IRR 14-20%+, cash-on-cash 6-10%+, equity multiple 1.8x+ or you cannot raise capital
5. Acquisition fees are 3% of purchase price (paid at closing from deal costs), asset management 1% of gross revenue monthly, plus 40%+ profit split—income tied to solving multi-million dollar problems
6. Red/purple state preference: need 30-60 day eviction timeline vs. blue states' 14-year rent control challenges
7. Use deal analyzers (provided tools) to calculate IRR, cash-on-cash, equity multiple—don't manually calculate; this removes underwriting bottleneck
