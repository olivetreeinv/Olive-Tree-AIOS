## Key Takeaways
1. Write down anxious thoughts to validate whether fears are true or rooted in old stories; ask 'Do I have proof?' before accepting negative beliefs
2. Use a three-step state-shifting routine: (1) list three things you're grateful for, (2) ground barefoot on soil/grass facing light with hand over heart for five deep breaths, (3) listen to a high-vibe playlist of five anchor songs
3. Identify originating incidents of limiting beliefs through hypnotherapy or breath work, then reframe childhood trauma from adult consciousness to release behavioral patterns
4. Core needs: women need to feel provided for, protected, and emotionally safe; men need a supportive spouse, peace in home, and physical intimacy
5. Recognize that 95% of actions are controlled by subconscious beliefs; make the unconscious conscious through journaling to consciously choose different behaviors
6. Masculine-feminine polarity in relationships: when sexual polarity drops, emotional safety/intimacy disconnects and woman pulls away, triggering anxious attachment in man
7. Never rob someone of rock bottom—adversity and disruptive events create perspective shifts needed for transformation and healing
