## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches a comprehensive framework for buying 15-50 unit multifamily properties in 90 days, focusing on the three critical skills of finding deals through brokers, analyzing them using key metrics (NOI, cap rate, cash-on-cash returns), and structuring financing using commercial loans and OPM (other people's money). He demonstrates how to enter deals with minimal personal capital by leveraging acquisition fees and equity raises to reduce out-of-pocket requirements.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Find deals through brokers by leveraging on-market listings (40% of deals) to access pre-market opportunities (60% of deals) - only 40% of properties make it to websites, the rest go to pre-market investor groups first
2. Focus on 10-30 unit deals when starting - anything 50+ units requires established track record and lender relationships you won't have initially
3. Use 75% loan-to-cost financing: for $1.6M total project cost, secure $1.2M loan (75%) and raise $400K equity (25%)
4. Structure GP/LP deal splits: bring 10% of capital ($40K on $400K equity raise) and receive 30% ownership, while investors bring 90% and receive 70%
5. Calculate deal quality using three metrics: 14-20% annual returns, 6-10%+ cash-on-cash returns, 1.8x+ equity multiples
6. Net operating income (NOI) is the core value driver: every $1,000/month NOI increase adds $220,000 in property value (NOI ÷ cap rate = property value)
7. Collect 5-8 revenue streams as syndicator: acquisition fee (2-3% at close), asset management fee (1-2% of revenue), 30-40% of cash flow, construction management fee (5-8% of construction costs), disposition fee (0.5-2% at sale), tax write-offs, and capital raise fees (3%)
