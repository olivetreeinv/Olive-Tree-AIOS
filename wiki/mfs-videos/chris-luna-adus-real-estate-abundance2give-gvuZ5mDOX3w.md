## Summary
This is a podcast interview between Justin Brennan and Chris Luna discussing content creation, personal development, real estate investing philosophy, and community impact. While Chris Luna builds ADUs and does real estate investing, the conversation is primarily about mindset, social media strategy, personal growth through hypnotherapy, and giving-back initiatives rather than specific deal analysis, underwriting, or sourcing tactics relevant to Brian Norton's 15-50 unit multifamily business in GA/TN/AL.

## Key Takeaways
1. Post 365 times in 365 days on social media to build audience and develop communication skills
2. Repeat successful social media actions—when a post gets 100+ likes, it signals messaging resonated; recreate similar frameworks
3. Focus on Instagram as primary platform for nurturing audience; use LinkedIn and Twitter for funnel traffic back to main stage
4. Prioritize getting into 'right rooms' at conferences—pay for VIP/front access rather than general admission; 'more hands you shake the more money you make'
5. ADU construction in San Diego costs $200-250K all-in (hard costs + $25-30K permits/plans); can achieve 2-6X returns and command $1,500/room/month for student housing
6. Live and post from authentic places (living room, unfiltered); grant Cardone's Monday 12pm YouTube live streams were model for building audience
7. Identify your '7 mountains' (business, media, government, education, family, religion, sports/culture) that drive purpose beyond money; use business success to fund passion projects
8. When markets show fear (8% rates, uncertainty), execute aggressively—'be fearful when others are greedy, greedy when others are fearful'; now is time to learn the craft, build networks, prepare for next cycle
