## Summary
Justin Brennan discusses San Diego's unique investment landscape, including the San Diego State student housing market with ADU conversions generating 6.5% cap rates, and explains why San Diego's diverse economy (military, biotech, tech, pharmaceuticals) creates resilience versus boom-bust markets. He also covers multifamily syndication fundamentals and debt leverage for scaling, but the conversation is heavily weighted toward mindset, personal story, and political topics rather than specific deal mechanics.

## Key Takeaways
1. Cost segregation allows 25-30% of property value recognized as year-one loss against personal income, with improved recent rules allowing closer to 40%
2. San Diego State area student housing (4-6 bed units with ADUs) trading at 6.5% cap rates turn-key with third-party management
3. Every $1,000/month of NOI boost equals ~$220,000 property value increase using 5.5% cap rate formula
4. Debt enables 5x effectiveness vs. all-cash: $1.2M all-in cost yields $300K profit at 25% return; using leverage with $200K capital yields same $300K profit at 150% ROI plus scalability to multiple deals
5. Hard money lenders typically finance 90% of acquisition + 100% of renovation costs at 10-10.5% rates with interest reserves
6. Deal-by-deal syndication preferred over fund strategy until track record established; projected investor returns of 18-20% annually on multifamily value-add deals
