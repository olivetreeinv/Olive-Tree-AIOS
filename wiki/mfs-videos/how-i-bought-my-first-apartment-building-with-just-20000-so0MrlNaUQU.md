## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches how to acquire 10-30 unit multifamily deals with minimal out-of-pocket capital ($20,000) by leveraging OPM (Other People's Money), using debt service coverage ratio (DSCR) loans at 65-70% LTV, and structuring equity raises with passive investors. He breaks down a specific $1.5M/15-unit deal model where the sponsor invests 10% equity ($50K), receives an acquisition fee ($30K), and nets only $20K out-of-pocket while controlling the asset and earning 40% of profits.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. A typical 10-30 unit apartment produces $2,500-$4,500/month free cash flow; need 3-4 deals to generate $10-20K monthly passive income
2. Target deal price range: $50K-$150K per unit in non-coastal markets (Midwest, Southeast, Southwest) for first deals
3. Commercial apartment loans are 65-70% LTV; much easier than residential because banks underwrite to building cash flow (DSCR), not personal credit
4. 80% of deals sell during 2-3 week pre-market broker phase to investor database; only 20% reach public sites like LoopNet/CoStar—focus on direct broker relationships
5. Structure: Sponsor invests 10% equity, receives 40% of cash flow/profits plus acquisition fee (2-3% of purchase price), keeps 100% control
6. Cost segregation + bonus depreciation creates paper losses to offset W2 income; real estate professional status (700+ hours/year) allows passive loss deduction against W2 wages
7. Acquisition fees ($30K on $1.5M deal) + asset management fees ($1% monthly) + refinance proceeds create multiple income streams beyond monthly cash flow
8. Time requirement: 5-10 hours/week in beginning phase to source, analyze, and structure deals while maintaining W2 employment
