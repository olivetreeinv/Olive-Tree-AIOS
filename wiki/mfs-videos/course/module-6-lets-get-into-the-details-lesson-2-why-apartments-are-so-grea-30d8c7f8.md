## Summary
Justin Brennan explains four wealth-building benefits of multifamily investing: cash flow/mailbox money, capital events from sales/refinances, tax shelters via cost segregation, and inflation hedging. While foundational context, this lesson addresses investment philosophy rather than deal-specific sourcing or underwriting techniques that directly address Brian's stated drags.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Multifamily generates four wealth components: cash flow, capital events (sales/refinances), tax shelter, and inflation hedge
2. Cost segregation accelerates depreciation from 27.5 years to ~5 years, creating 1.2-1.3x negative depreciation write-offs by year five
3. Year one investors typically receive 86-87% negative K-statement depreciation benefit
4. Refinance events are tax-free cash-out opportunities
5. Real estate typically wins over 10-year holding periods through market cycles 9 out of 10 times
6. Rents increase with inflation, offsetting bank account holdings at 0.22% returns
