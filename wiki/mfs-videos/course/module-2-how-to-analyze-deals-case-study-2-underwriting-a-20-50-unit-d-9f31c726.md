## Summary
Justin Brennan walks through underwriting a 35-unit value-add multifamily deal in Decatur, Georgia using his pro forma model. He demonstrates how to extract rent roll and T12 financial data, populate unit types and rents, identify operational upside (moving from $487k to $585k NOI through collections and occupancy improvements), and calculate returns at various purchase prices ($4.0M-$4.5M range), ultimately showing how a GP investing $200k can realize $1.5M returns while LPs earn 20% annualized returns.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Download rent roll and T12 (last 12 months P&L) from broker after signing NDA; T12 shows month-by-month income and expenses
2. Count exact unit mix and market rents from rent roll; pull gross potential rent directly from rent roll total
3. Use T12 total column only (not monthly breakouts) for expense inputs; verify all numbers with 'trust and verify' principle
4. Expenses as % of EGI should run 'upper 30s to low 40s' for a stabilized 1975-era property depending on market (insurance/property taxes vary by region)
5. Debt service coverage ratio minimum 1.25 required by banks; if DSCR drops below 1.25, increase down payment from 35% to 40% to reduce loan amount
6. Properties like this are 'operational games'—move needle through collections, occupancy, and expense control, not renovation
7. A 10-unit deal is ideal first deal size: 'small enough but also large enough, economies of scale'
8. As GP bringing 10% ($200k on $2M equity raise), structure preferred return (6%) for LPs first, then split remaining cash flow 70% LP / 30% GP (promote)
