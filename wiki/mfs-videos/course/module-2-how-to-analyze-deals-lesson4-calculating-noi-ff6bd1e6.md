## Summary
Justin teaches the fundamental formulas for calculating Net Operating Income (NOI) and using the cap rate formula as a quick analysis tool. He demonstrates how knowing two of three variables (NOI, cap rate, price) allows investors to reverse-engineer deal feasibility and validate whether a seller's asking price is justified by market conditions.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. NOI = Gross Income + Other Income - Operating Expenses (fundamental formula)
2. Cap Rate = NOI ÷ Price (the three-piece puzzle formula)
3. If price is $30M and market cap rate is 4%, expect NOI around $1.2M ($30M × 0.04)
4. Use market cap rates from brokers as benchmarks to validate deals quickly
5. If calculated NOI doesn't match expected NOI based on price and cap rate, seller is asking too much
6. Example: $1.3M NOI ÷ $1.3M = 4.3% cap rate on $30M purchase
7. Reverse formula: Price = NOI ÷ Cap Rate (e.g., $1.3M ÷ 0.04 = $32.5M max price)
