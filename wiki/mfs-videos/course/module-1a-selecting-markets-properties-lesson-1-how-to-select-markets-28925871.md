## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches how to select multifamily markets for out-of-state investing, emphasizing the importance of following lender capital flows and using key metrics like population and job growth to identify high-opportunity MSAs. For investors scaling beyond expensive coastal markets, this lesson provides a framework for identifying target markets before property-level analysis.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Follow the money—identify markets where main lenders are concentrating capital and data research
2. Target markets with north of 1% annual population growth and north of 2% annual job growth
3. Use Marcus & Millichap, CBRE, and Burcadia annual reports to identify top 45-100 MSAs where institutional capital flows
4. Enter new markets 6-8 months in advance before deal hunting to set up team, logistics, operations, and relationships
5. Consider geographic logistics: central time zone markets like Kansas City offer 2.5 hour flights and easier operations than coasts
6. Start with data research on demographics websites, then move to 'boots on the ground' in suburbs and specific cities
7. Avoid high-priced coastal markets like California ($400-600K per door) for scaling—they're wealth preservation, not growth strategy
