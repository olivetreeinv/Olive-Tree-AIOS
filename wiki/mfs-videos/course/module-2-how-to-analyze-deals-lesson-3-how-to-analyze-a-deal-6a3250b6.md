## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches a systematic framework for analyzing multifamily deals by gathering and verifying specific financial documents (T12s, rent rolls, tenant estoppels, aged receivables), calculating key performance metrics (cash-on-cash returns, IRR, equity multiple, return on cost), and evaluating four qualitative factors (location, people/management, processes, and competitive comps) to quickly determine deal viability.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. For smaller properties (2-4 units): obtain current rents, market rents, post-renovation rents via comps
2. For larger properties (5+ units): require T12 (12-month P&L), rent roll, aged receivables, unit availability report, and lease velocity metrics (leases signed in last 30/90 days)
3. Verify rents with tenant estoppel certificates and lease copies; verify expenses with receipts, invoices, and utility bills
4. Target metrics for Class B/C assets: 7-10% cash-on-cash returns, 16-22% IRR, 18-25% annual rate of return, 1-2x equity multiple, 18-20%+ return on cost
5. Cap rate strategy for value-add deals: buy at 4 cap, force appreciation to 7 cap, sell at 4.5-5 cap; maintain minimum 2-point spread between forced cap rate and exit cap rate
6. Get boots on the ground to physically tour comps and understand the competitive landscape; don't rely solely on online data
7. Analyze the four critical factors: location (non-negotiable for tenant appeal), people (quality of tenants, property management, maintenance staff), processes (software systems, eviction handling, move-in/out procedures), and comps (direct competition analysis)
