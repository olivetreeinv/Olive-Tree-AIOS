## Summary
Justin outlines the hierarchy of capital sources for multifamily deals, starting with friends and family, then accredited investors, family offices, and institutional/private equity firms. He emphasizes that early-stage deals (15-50 units like Brian's) typically rely on friends/family and high-net-worth individuals, while larger deals access institutional capital. He also clarifies the lending distinction between 1-4 unit residential loans (borrower-focused underwriting) and 5+ unit commercial loans (property cash flow-focused underwriting).

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Start with friends and family capital—'nine times out of 10' this is your first round
2. Accredited investors must meet SEC criteria: certain income levels individually or jointly, or specific net worth thresholds
3. Family offices manage hundreds of millions to billions for ultra-wealthy families; relationships take time to build and require sophistication
4. Private equity firms charge fees, take equity percentages, and preferred returns; use them when you can't raise capital independently
5. Pitch decks must include property details, location, revenue, expenses, business plan, and projected returns
6. People invest in people, not just projects—emphasize trust, transparency, and honest communication about problems
7. At 5+ units you shift from residential loans (borrower-focused underwriting) to commercial loans (property cash flow-focused underwriting)
