## Summary
Justin Brennan explains partnership and syndication structures for multifamily deals, detailing how general partners and limited partners work together through Reg D SEC filings. He uses a $35M deal example showing ~60-65% debt financing, ~10% GP equity, and ~90% LP equity, with LPs receiving preferred returns, cash-on-cash projections, and exit profits while GPs manage operations and sign loans.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. $35M deal structure: $20-22M loan (60-65% LTC), $1.4M GP equity, $12-14M LP equity
2. Preferred return is minimum base return to LPs before any profit splits between GP and LP
3. Limited partners expect 2x equity multiple (e.g., $200K return on $100K invested) plus cost segregation depreciation bonuses not included in projections
4. General partners sign all loans, operate property/construction/asset management, take all risk; LPs are passive, get updates, and collect checks
5. Typical hold period is 3-5-7+ years with distributions throughout, then cash out via refinance or sale
6. Reg D filings are either 506C or 506B offerings
7. You run out of your own money at some point and need to scale through syndications, REITs, or crowdfunding
