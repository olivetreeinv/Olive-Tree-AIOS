## Summary
Justin Brennan explains the fundamentals of commercial multifamily financing, contrasting it with residential deals. He clarifies that lenders underwrite the property first and the borrower second, requiring minimum net worth equal to the loan amount and liquidity of 1-2% of the loan. He covers typical debt-to-equity ratios (70-30, 65-35, 60-40) and advocates graduating into larger deal sizes rather than jumping straight to massive acquisitions.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Commercial lenders underwrite the property first, then the borrower second—not individual credit like residential
2. Lender requirements: net worth ≥ loan amount + liquidity of 1-2% of loan amount
3. If you lack liquidity/net worth, bring a Key Principal (KP) to co-sign and meet requirements
4. Typical financing ratios: 70-30, 65-35, or 60-40 debt-to-equity
5. Bridge debt structure: acquisition + renovation dollars over 24-36 months, then refinance into fixed-rate (Freddie/Fannie) debt
6. Banks evaluate your track record and require experienced key principals on deals—your resume matters
7. Graduate into deal sizes progressively; smaller mistakes on smaller deals beats catastrophic failure on large deals
