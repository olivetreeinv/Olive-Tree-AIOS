## Summary
Justin explains the equity stack in multifamily deals, covering investor types (friends/family, accredited/non-accredited retail, institutional private equity, family offices, high net worth individuals), typical capital stack structures (70% debt/30% equity), and how to split equity between general partners (Class B) and limited partners (Class A shares) with example profit distributions.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Friends and family typically invest $25K-$100K per person in early deals
2. Institutional private equity minimum investments typically $5M-$10M
3. Standard debt-to-equity split is 70% loan to 30% equity
4. In a $7M loan / $3M equity deal, 90% of equity ($2.7M) comes from Class A limited partners, 10% ($300K) from Class B general partners
5. Typical profit/cash flow split is 70% to limited partners, 30% to general partners when LPs bring 90% of equity capital
6. Family offices and ultra-high-net-worth individuals require strong track record and resume; they conduct extensive underwriting and can write checks of $1M-$2M+ quickly once relationship is built
7. Capital stack structures vary by deal type; combo approach recommended early—mix of retail syndication plus institutional private equity
