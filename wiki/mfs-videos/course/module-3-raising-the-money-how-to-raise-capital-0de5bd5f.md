## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches the complete capital raising process for multifamily deals, covering both the marketing side (pitch decks, summary videos, investor outreach) and legal side (LLC operating agreements for friends/family deals vs. PPM with 506B/506C offerings for larger raises from unknown investors). He explains how to structure raises, identify investor sources, and work with securities attorneys to ensure legal compliance.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. On a $700,000 raise with $630,000 from LPs, expect to land 5-8 investors total, typically one or two high net worth individuals ($100k-$500k each) plus friends/family at $25k-$50k minimums
2. Pitch decks must answer the three questions every investor asks: How much do you need? When do you need it? When do I get paid back?
3. Friends and family deals with 3-6 known investors require only an LLC operating agreement drafted by an attorney; don't use LegalZoom templates without attorney backing
4. For raising from unknown investors, use a PPM (private placement memorandum) with four documents: offering, subscription agreement, operating agreement, and investor questionnaire—costs $14,000-$16,000 for first deal, ~50% less for subsequent deals
5. 506B offerings are non-public (marketed only to your network via email/phone/text) and can include non-accredited investors; 506C offerings allow public marketing but require accredited investors only
6. Include a 5-10 minute summary video on a backend portal where investors can review pitch deck, documents, and schedule phone calls before committing
7. Don't publicly market deals on social media or run ads without proper legal structure (506C with accredited investors only)
8. Use your entire network strategically: even broke friends may refer wealthy contacts—ask 'Who do you know?' rather than assuming they'll invest themselves
