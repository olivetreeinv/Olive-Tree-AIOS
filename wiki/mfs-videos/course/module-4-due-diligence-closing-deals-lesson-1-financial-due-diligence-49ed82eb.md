## Summary
Justin Brennan walks through the financial due diligence process for multifamily acquisitions in escrow, covering the critical documents and reports needed to validate property economics during the 20-30 day due diligence window. He emphasizes matching rent rolls to actual leases, analyzing T12 profit & loss statements, and segregating deferred maintenance from value-add improvements to avoid overpaying for a property.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Conduct financial, physical, and market due diligence within a 20-30 day escrow window before closing
2. Request rent roll (tenant list, unit types, square footage, market rents, current rents, vacancies) and audit every single lease to verify occupancy matches
3. Obtain T12 (12-month P&L broken down monthly) showing gross rents, concessions, loss to lease, vacancies, and all operating expenses; verify against bank statements and tax returns
4. Request 12 months of utility bills, all vendor/service contracts (landscaping, pest control, trash, pool maintenance), and aged receivables report to identify delinquent tenants
5. Get unit availability report showing current vacancies and lease move-out notices for next couple months
6. Separate and quantify deferred maintenance (HVAC, roof, windows, drainage, plumbing, electrical that don't add rent value) from improvement capex—very important for capitalizing costs
7. Request capex/repair expense verification from property management and verify RUBs (utility reimbursements) match other income; request 24 months of work orders showing repair history and response time
