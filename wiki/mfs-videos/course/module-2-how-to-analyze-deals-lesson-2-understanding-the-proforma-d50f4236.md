## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches the fundamental structure of a rental property proforma, breaking down the universal formula: Gross Potential Income → subtract economic losses (vacancy, bad debt, loss-to-lease, concessions) → add reimbursables and other income → equals EGI → subtract operating expenses → equals NOI → subtract debt service and CapEx → equals net cash. This framework applies across all property sizes from 2-unit to 500-unit multifamily.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Gross Potential Income less 8-10% total economic losses (1-2% bad debt/concessions, 3-7% physical vacancy) gets you to Effective Gross Income (EGI)
2. Add Reimbursables (RUBS): water, sewer, gas, trash, pest costs tenants pay back to offset landlord burden
3. Add Other Income sources: pet rent, administrative fees, late fees, parking fees, tech packages, valet trash fees
4. Operating expenses include: admin, advertising, insurance, contract services, repairs/maintenance, turnover make-ready, payroll, utilities, property taxes, property management fees
5. NOI (Net Operating Income) = EGI minus total operating expenses; represents cash return with zero debt
6. Final Net Cash = NOI minus mortgage payments minus capital expenditures (CapEx)
7. Proforma structure scales universally across all property types and sizes; complexity comes in detail execution but core formula remains constant
