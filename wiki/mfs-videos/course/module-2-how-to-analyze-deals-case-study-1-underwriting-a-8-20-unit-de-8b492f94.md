## Summary
Justin Brennan walks through underwriting a 7-unit turnkey property in Phoenix, demonstrating how to plug actual financials into a pro forma template to identify deal breakeven pricing. He shows that the asking price of $1.825M doesn't work financially, identifies the property would need to trade around $1.6M depending on interest rates, and emphasizes using on-market deal analysis to build broker relationships that lead to off-market opportunities.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Smaller multifamily (7-20 units) typically prices at $260k/door in Phoenix vs. $225k/door for larger 25-40 unit deals in the same market
2. Always add reserves at 5% of expenses and turnover costs (~$1k per unit turn) even if seller doesn't include them
3. Target minimum 1.25x debt service coverage ratio; loans under $1M get worse rates/terms than Freddie/Fannie loans above $1M
4. Location analysis: multifamily surrounded by single-family homes or residential zones is ideal; avoid junkyards, industrial, gas stations, and commercial mixed-use
5. Use realistic interest rate assumptions (6% vs. optimistic 3-4%); a 3% rate change makes massive DCR/return differences on small deals
6. Target 4-8% cash-on-cash returns and 12-15% IRR on pro forma metrics to have a viable deal
7. 'On to off' strategy: leverage on-market deal analysis and offers to build broker relationships that unlock off-market deals before they hit public listing sites
