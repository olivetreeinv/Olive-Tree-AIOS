## Summary
Justin teaches a systematic framework for identifying good multifamily properties through location assessment, people/processes evaluation, and competitive comp analysis. He emphasizes that location (neighborhood feel, single-family adjacency, path of progress) cannot be changed and is foundational, while people and processes can be improved through operational value-add.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Drive the property at multiple times (morning, midday, evening, weekday, weekend) to assess neighborhood dynamics
2. Target properties abutting single-family home neighborhoods, not isolated in commercial/industrial zones
3. Count bars on windows as a crime indicator during initial property assessment
4. Seek unit mix of 55%+ two-bedrooms (ideally more two-bedrooms than anything else, minimum 50-50 ratio)
5. Evaluate comps in person - 'get boots on the ground, walk the neighborhoods'
6. Property management staff, maintenance staff, and tenant base demographics make up 'the people' evaluation pillar
7. Expect to replace property management staff and update processes in 9 out of 10 acquisitions for operational value-add
