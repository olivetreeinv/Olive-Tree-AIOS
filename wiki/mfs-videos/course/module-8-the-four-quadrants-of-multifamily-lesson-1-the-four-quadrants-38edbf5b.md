## Summary
Justin Brennan explains the four quadrants of multifamily real estate (price, equity, NOI, and cash-on-cash return) and how they interdependently move together. He demonstrates through concrete examples how changes in one metric cascade through the others, providing a framework for evaluating deal quality and understanding the relationships between purchase price, down payment requirements, operating income, and cash returns.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. The four quadrants are: price, equity (down payment), NOI, and cash-on-cash (COC)
2. One quadrant can't move without affecting the others—they are interdependent
3. If price rises from $1M to $1.2M, equity requirement rises from $300K to $380K (hypothetically), reducing COC from 7% to 6%
4. NOI = gross revenues less vacancies less expenses; it changes only when revenues or expenses change
5. Price is calculated by dividing NOI by the market cap rate (e.g., $200K NOI ÷ 5% cap rate = $4M valuation)
6. When NOI drops from $200K to $150K at a 5% cap rate, property value drops from $4M to $3M
7. As price declines, equity requirement typically declines, which typically increases cash-on-cash return
