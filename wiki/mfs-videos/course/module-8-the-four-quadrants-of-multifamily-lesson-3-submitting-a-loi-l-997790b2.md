## Summary
Justin Brennan explains the commercial multifamily letter of intent (LOI) process, covering LOI structure, key terms (price, deposit, due diligence timeframe, closing timeline), and the typical competitive bidding process from initial submission through highest-and-best rounds to deal award.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. LOI is the commercial equivalent of a residential offer, typically prepared by investor group or listing broker
2. In larger multifamily deals, buyer's agents don't exist; groups submit directly to listing brokers
3. Deposits often go partially hard from day one in competitive markets (e.g., $500,000 deposit with $250,000 hard day one)
4. Due diligence timeframe, closing timeframe, and commission responsibility are critical LOI terms
5. Standard competitive process: 30-day lead time, call-for-offers deadline, highest-and-best rounds (typically 2-3 rounds), final phone call with seller before award
6. Always submit background information and resumes along with LOI to broker
7. If selected into highest-and-best, broker notifies you and requests submission of improved offer
