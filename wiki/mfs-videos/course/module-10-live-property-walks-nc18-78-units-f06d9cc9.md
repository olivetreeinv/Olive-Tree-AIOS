## Summary
Justin walks through a 78-unit San Diego value-add property mid-renovation, showing unit-level renovation scope (~$18k/unit in California for full gut), assembly-line renovation process (4-5 units at a time), and exterior rebranding. While detailed, the content is property-specific showmanship rather than systematically teaching Brian's deal-sourcing or underwriting workflows.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Full unit renovation at this property costs approximately $18,000 per unit in California (includes new doors, flooring, cabinetry, fixtures, windows, electrical, quartz countertops, paint, ceiling scraping)
2. Manage renovations as assembly line: do 4-5 units at a time to avoid 'stacking effect' where vacancies cascade (4 units vacant + 4 more behind = 8 vacant; then 12 vacant = massive problem)
3. Keep complex occupied during renovations; stagger vacancy to prevent back-to-back unit turnover
4. Maintain 30-day re-lease window per batch to prevent vacancy stacking
