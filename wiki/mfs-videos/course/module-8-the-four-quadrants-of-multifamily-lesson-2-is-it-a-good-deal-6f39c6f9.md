## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches a systematic framework for evaluating multifamily deals using six key metrics: cash on cash, net operating income (NOI), internal rate of return (IRR), equity multiple, price per door, and replacement cost. He provides specific performance thresholds for value-add Class B deals and emphasizes using sensitivity analysis to stress-test deals against market changes.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Cash on cash target: 7-10% upon stabilization for Class B value-add deals
2. Debt coverage ratio (DCR): minimum 1.2x or greater (NOI ÷ debt service)
3. IRR target: 18-25% for project-level returns
4. Equity multiple: minimum 2.0x for Class B (1.9-2.5x ideal), with Class A at 1.1x+ due to lower risk
5. Return on cost: 18% or more on renovation/improvement costs
6. Buy at or below replacement cost to ensure competitive advantage
7. Use sensitivity tables to model scenarios: price changes, interest rate increases, cap rate shifts, and stress-test against inflation/market downturns
8. Detailed financial analysis becomes critical for deals north of 20 units
