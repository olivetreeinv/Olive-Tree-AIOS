## Summary
Justin Brennan introduces foundational financial terminology essential for analyzing multifamily deals: NOI (net operating income), cap rate (capitalization rate), IRR (internal rate of return), and COC (cash-on-cash return). Understanding how these metrics interact is critical for quickly evaluating whether a deal makes financial sense and how to interpret the numbers on financial statements.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. NOI = all income less all expenses, showing returns before debt service/mortgages
2. Cap rate = NOI divided by purchase price, answering 'what's my annualized return with zero debt?'
3. IRR = time value of money: cash-on-cash returns + appreciation/sale proceeds annualized over the hold period
4. COC = actual physical cash returned monthly/quarterly/yearly on the investment
5. IRR includes both current cash return AND future profit from sale/refinance, while COC is only current annual cash
6. Download the attached glossary file for complete definitions and backstory of all financial terms
7. These four metrics (NOI, cap rate, IRR, COC) are the 'main ones you gotta focus on' for deal analysis
