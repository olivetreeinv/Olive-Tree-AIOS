## Summary
Justin Brennan provides a live walkthrough of a 121-unit multifamily property in Texas, demonstrating operational best practices for larger complexes including on-site management/maintenance structure, unit renovation processes (~$9,000 per unit), and critical water damage prevention protocols during due diligence and cold weather operations.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Inspect every building for water shutoffs and angle stops (under sinks) during due diligence tours
2. Prefer copper pressure lines over galvanized piping to reduce water damage risk; PEX acceptable for new construction
3. Position water shutoffs as close to individual units/buildings as possible to enable quick emergency response without disrupting multiple tenants
4. Allocate approximately $14,000 per unit for major renovations to move Class C assets toward B-minus quality
5. In cold climates during freeze risk, issue tenant notices to drip kitchen sink lines to prevent pipe freezing and explosions
6. Standard renovation process includes paint, flooring (90% new carpet), countertops, cabinets, appliances, fixtures, and new LVT or similar flooring materials
7. Model units should be staged like a house flip to showcase potential to prospects; use trifold brochures with floor plans, features, benefits, and pricing as takeaway marketing materials
