## Summary
Justin explains how to source debt financing for multifamily deals, covering the distinction between local commercial banks for smaller loans (under $3M), loan brokers for mid-size deals, and capital advisors for larger deals ($15-20M+). He details bridge debt vs. fixed-rate debt structures, with bridge debt used for value-add renovations and fixed-rate debt for stabilized assets.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Loans under $3 million typically come from local commercial banks in that specific market
2. For $10M+ acquisition loans, use loan brokers like CBRE or Bercadia, or direct relationships with Freddie/Fannie
3. Capital advisors enter at $15-20M+ loan sizes; they charge 3% fee on equity raised and 1% on debt sourced
4. You need 500+ units under ownership to work with capital advisors who will vouch for your track record
5. Bridge debt is used for value-add: acquisition portion + holdback for renovations, then refinance to fixed-rate debt after stabilization
6. Loan brokers can be found via LinkedIn and conferences; build relationships with local players before deal-hunting
7. Fixed-rate debt available from Freddie/Fannie or life insurance companies; bridge debt from debt funds and institutional lenders
