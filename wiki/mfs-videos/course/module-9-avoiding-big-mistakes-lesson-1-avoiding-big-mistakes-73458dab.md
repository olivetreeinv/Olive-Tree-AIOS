## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches critical mistakes to avoid when acquiring multifamily properties, emphasizing the importance of location over paper returns, proper due diligence, and scaling gradually rather than attempting zero-to-hero deals. He stresses managing key vendors (PMs, renovation teams) closely and avoiding undercapitalization to protect investor capital.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Never sacrifice location for high cap rates on paper—location determines 99.9% of rental bumps and resale value
2. Don't chase paper returns; strong proformas can hide weak fundamentals in bad locations
3. Start with 10-15 or 20-30 unit deals before scaling up, not zero-to-hero 100+ unit deals on your first transaction
4. Over-capitalize deals slightly rather than under-capitalize and trigger capital calls to investors
5. Property managers typically need lots of properties to survive, so actively manage the managers and stay on them
6. Test renovation teams on a couple units first; never pay full contract upfront, use max 25% deposit
7. Do detailed due diligence on every deal—the Texas 225-unit example lost millions due to missed pipe/drainage issues from skipped checklist items
