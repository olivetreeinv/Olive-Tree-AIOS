## 1. broker-search — Add the "On-to-Off" outreach playbook
**Why it matters:** Biggest sourcing gap. Current skill only *scans* for 2+ listing brokers — it does nothing with them. The single most-repeated lesson across 8+ videos is using on-market listings as bait to unlock the 60–80% of inventory that trades pre-market.

**Paste-ready (new section after the scan):**
```
## After the scan: On-to-Off outreach (turn listings into pre-market flow)

~60–80% of MF trades never hit a portal. The on-market listing is bait to reach the
broker who controls the pocket. Per qualifying broker (all outbound = draft, Brian approves):

1. Pick 3–4 of the broker's on-market listings in/near the buy box — even ones we'd never offer on.
2. Outreach: ask escrow/offer status, then book an in-person tour. "We're active buyers —
   looking for [X–Y units] at [price/door]."
3. Tour belly-to-belly, hand business card, look/act the part. Log contact + bump Tier.
4. Follow-up script: "Totally understand the gap on price. We're buyers in this market —
   any other deals, on or off market, coming up that fit [buy box]?" → ask for pre-market list.
5. Set Pre-market list = "Requested" + 2-week ping cadence.

Favor mom-and-pop brokers on sub-20–30-unit assets — you reach the decision-maker directly.
```

## 4. deal-analysis — Income realism (economic loss + other income)
**Why it matters:** Two of the most common proforma errors — guessing vacancy and omitting ancillary revenue — both distort NOI and price.

**Paste-ready:**
```
### Income build — don't guess
Gross Potential Rent → apply Economic Loss 8–10% total:

Then ADD other income (frequently missed → understated NOI):
   RUBS (standard at 50+ units), pet rent, admin/app fees, late fees, parking,
   tech package, valet trash.
```

## 5. deal-analysis — Expense completeness + back-into-offer-price
**Why it matters:** Sellers strip line items; anchoring to the ask wastes underwriting time. Solving price from target returns is the core skill.

**Paste-ready:**
```
### Expense completeness check
Sellers routinely omit: turnover (~$1k/turn), replacement reserves (~$250–500/unit or
5% EGI), full mgmt fee (~8%), maintenance (~$750/unit). Plug all in (moved one deal
$24k→$27k expenses).

### Back into the offer price
Don't anchor to the ask. Solve purchase price from target returns (CoC + IRR floors).
Output: "Pencils at $X vs $Y ask — here's where you need to buy it." Use as LOI basis
even when far below ask (opens the broker conversation; builds credibility).
```
**Confidence:** High · **Sources:** Case Study 1 (8b492f94), 8 UNIT (e93d2820), How To Analyze (6a3250b6)

## 7. deal-analysis — 30-sec cap-rate sanity check + value-add spread rule
**Why it matters:** Fast triage tool to reject overpriced asks before pulling docs; spread rule validates the business plan clears returns.

**Paste-ready (add to Step 0 area):**
```
### Cap-rate sanity check (pre-doc)
Two-of-three: known market cap × asking price → implied NOI. If implied NOI << claimed NOI,
seller is overpriced → re-price or pass.
Value-add spread rule: forced cap should beat exit cap by ≥2 pts (e.g., buy 5 → force 7 →
exit 5). If the spread isn't there, the plan won't hit return targets.
```
**Confidence:** Med · **Sources:** Calculating NOI (ff6bd1e6), Four Quadrants (38edbf5b), How To Analyze (6a3250b6)
