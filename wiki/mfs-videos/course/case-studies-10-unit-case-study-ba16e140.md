## Summary
Justin Brennan walks through the Menlo Park Townhomes 10-unit case study—a live Tucson deal in escrow with seller financing. He explains the five pillars of apartment acquisition (find, analyze, raise capital, close, manage), demonstrates how to transition from on-market to off-market deal sourcing by building broker relationships, and breaks down the pitch deck structure, return metrics (17% IRR, 9.74% cash-on-cash, 2.58x equity multiple), and GP/LP compensation (acquisition fees, disposition fees, asset management fees). He also covers four critical management reports for ongoing property performance.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Five pillars of multifamily investing apply uniformly across 2-unit to 100-unit deals: find, analyze, raise capital, close, manage
2. 60% of apartment deals are off-market; only 40% appear on LoopNet/Crexie, and 95% of listed deals are overpriced
3. Start on Crexie/LoopNet to identify active brokers, then ping them repeatedly ('ping, ping, ping') even on deals that don't work to build relationships and gain access to pre-market opportunities
4. Visit target markets in person for 2-3 days with 2-3 property tours lined up to establish credibility ('eyeball to eyeball') and convert brokers to active deal flow partners
5. Preferred return structure: investors receive 6% interest on capital first, then split profits (75% LP / 25% GP in this example) post-preferred return
6. GP double-dips: receives preferred return as an investor PLUS 25% promote bonus if investor returns are met, plus 2% acquisition fee ($30K on $1.34M purchase), plus 1% disposition fee on sale ($20K projected)
7. Four critical management reports to monitor: unit availability (occupancy pipeline), delinquency (collections), work orders (maintenance backlog), and lease renewal rate—review 90 days forward to predict property performance
