## Summary
Justin Brennan discusses the taxonomy of real estate asset classes, advocating for multifamily apartments (20-100+ units) and mobile homes as superior to single-family/condos due to economies of scale, cash flow, and tax incentives. While foundational philosophy aligns with Brian's target market (15-50 unit multifamily), the lesson focuses on asset-class selection rather than deal analysis or sourcing—Brian's stated drag areas.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Avoid 'onesie twosies' (single-family/condos); consolidate into 20-50 unit or 100+ unit properties for economies of scale
2. Party begins at 5+ units; real game starts at 100+ units where true economies of scale emerge
3. Be 'inch wide and a mile deep'—specialize deeply in 1-3 asset classes rather than spreading thin across many
4. Multifamily apartments superior to office/commercial because tenants have fundamental need (food, water, shelter); office buildings go vacant in recessions/COVID
5. Current US rental mix ~30% renters vs 63% homeowners, trending toward European model with more renters over next 10 years
6. Mobile homes are phenomenal second-best asset class; both multifamily and mobile homes provide reliable cash flow, growth, tax incentives, and inflation hedges
7. Progress through asset classes: start single-family/condos, graduate to 2-4 units, then 5-20 units, then 20-50 units, then 100+ units
