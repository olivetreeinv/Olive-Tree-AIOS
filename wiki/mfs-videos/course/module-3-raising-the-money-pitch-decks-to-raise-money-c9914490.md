## Summary
Justin Brennan walks through the complete structure and sections of a multifamily pitch deck used to raise investor capital. He covers the 20-30 page document's key components including executive summary, financial metrics, sources and uses, market data, unit comps, and pro forma P&Ls, explaining where to source information and offering templated solutions via Canva or PowerPoint.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Pitch deck should be 20-30 pages packaged to send to potential investors
2. Cap stack should typically stay 70-30 or 80-20 debt-to-equity split
3. Set minimum investment threshold (example given: $50K)
4. Manually enter projected cash flows into pitch deck rather than screenshot Excel for professional appearance
5. Use Fiverr to hire someone for $200-300 after doing first deck yourself
6. Pull market data from offering memorandum (OM) or public websites listed in vendor systems PDF
7. Unit comps should be broken down by bedroom type (1BR, 2BR, 3BR) and sourced from broker or Rent-O-Meter/Zillow
