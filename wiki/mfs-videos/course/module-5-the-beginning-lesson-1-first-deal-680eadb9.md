## Summary
Justin shares his personal journey to the first multifamily deal, emphasizing partnership strategy to access capital and lending ability while leveraging operational expertise. He advocates finding complementary partners based on skill gaps rather than trying to do everything alone, and demonstrates this with his $100k condo first deal in 2010 that achieved 13% cash-on-cash and 25% annualized return.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Start small with your first deal—Justin's first was a $100,000 condo with $25,000 down payment
2. Achieve 13% cash-on-cash return and 25% annualized return targets on value-add deals
3. Partner based on complementary skill sets: if you have operations/deal-sourcing, find a capital/lending partner; if you have capital, find an operations partner
4. 50/50 partnership split can work effectively when both parties bring non-negotiable value
5. Build momentum through graduated property sizes: single-family → 2-4 units → 5-10 units → larger complexes
6. Use broker commissions as sweat equity to roll into deals
7. Focus on your best skills and hire/partner for weaknesses rather than trying to master everything
