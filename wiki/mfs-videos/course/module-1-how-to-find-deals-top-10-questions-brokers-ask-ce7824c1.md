## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches newbie multifamily investors how to effectively communicate with commercial real estate brokers by preparing scripted responses to the top 10 questions brokers typically ask. He emphasizes first impressions, credibility signals, and positioning oneself as capable of closing deals in the 10-50 unit value-add space. The video provides specific talking points on property criteria, markets, pricing, financing structure, team composition, and investment strategy.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Target 10-50 units on first two deals; don't jump to 50+ units as a newbie—'walk before you run'
2. Answer property type question: 'I'm focused on small to midsize multifamily deals, typically 10 to 50 units value add potential'
3. On financing: 'I'll be using a mix. Conventional financing, typically 65% to 70% leverage. And I'll be syndicating the equity with other investors and investment partners'
4. On equity contribution: bring 10% of equity yourself, raise the remaining 90%
5. On deal review speed: 'I can typically get back to you in 24 to 48 hours after receiving the package' (offering memorandum/OM)
6. Request T12 (12-month P&L), rent roll, and marketing package from brokers
7. Target deal metrics: '6% to 10% cash on cash return and a 14% to 20% or more IRR over 3-5 years'
8. Use even on-market deals as conversation lures to build broker relationships for pre-market and off-market deal flow
