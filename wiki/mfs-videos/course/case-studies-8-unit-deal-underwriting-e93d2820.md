## Summary
Justin Brennan demonstrates how to underwrite an 8-unit multifamily deal in San Antonio, Texas, showing how to extract data from offering memorandum (OM), input financials into a pro forma analyzer, and determine true property value. He illustrates that the asking price of $995,000 doesn't work financially, but reducing the price to $700,000 improves metrics, and explains how to use on-market deals to build broker relationships and access off-market opportunities.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use Crexie.com to identify on-market brokers, then leverage those brokers to access off-market deals before they hit the market
2. Download the offering memorandum (OM) from listing sites to extract rent roll, unit mix, square footage, and current financials
3. Input data into Excel pro forma analyzer: purchase price, down payment (35%), repair budget, closing costs (5%), interest rates (7%), unit count, rents (current and future), and all expenses
4. Calculate property taxes using county-specific formulas (Justin example: San Antonio formula results in approximately 2% of property value)
5. Set expense ratio target near 50% and debt service coverage ratio (DSCR) minimum at 1.2 or greater for lender approval
6. Build market infrastructure during 2-3 day market visit: meet 3-4 brokers, connect with property management companies, identify construction crews, learn good/bad neighborhoods, and development trends
7. When analyzing an 8-unit deal, assume $750-$1,200 per unit annually for maintenance/repair, $80/month per unit for utilities, 8% management fee, and $2,000 annual reserves for unexpected CapEx
