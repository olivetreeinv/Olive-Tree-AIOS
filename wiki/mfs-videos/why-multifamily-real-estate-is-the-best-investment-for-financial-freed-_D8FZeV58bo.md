## Summary
Justin Brennan delivers a motivational overview of multifamily investing as a wealth-building vehicle, emphasizing the importance of community, partnerships, and the five-pillar method (finding, analyzing, financing, closing, managing deals). While the presentation covers foundational deal mechanics and return structures, it focuses heavily on mindset, tax strategy, and sales messaging rather than concrete underwriting tactics relevant to Brian's specific deal-sourcing and underwriting challenges.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Focus on 10-30 unit deals for first acquisitions to build resume and broker relationships before scaling to 50+ units
2. Leverage on-market broker listings to gain access to pre-market deals; brokers do 5x outreach compared to direct-to-seller efforts
3. Target 14-20% annual returns, 6-10% cash-on-cash, and 1.8x+ equity multiples as deal quality thresholds
4. Net Operating Income (NOI) is the key value lever: raising NOI by $1,000/month = ~$220,000 in property value at 5.5% cap rate
5. Raise capital in order: Deal first (must pencil), then Pitch Deck (showing projected returns), then Process (execution plan)—not money-first
6. Structure deals with GP bringing 10% capital for 30-40% equity split, LP investors bringing 90% for 70% equity plus 8% preferred return
7. Get paid 5-8 ways: acquisition fees (2-3%), asset management (1-2% of revenue), cash flow split, refinance profits, disposition fees (0.5-2%), construction mgmt (5-8%), finder fees
