## Summary
Justin Brennan explains multifamily investing fundamentals for Brian Norton's AIOS, covering why multifamily is superior to single-family (5-8 revenue streams vs 1-3), how to find deals through brokers, analyze using NOI and cap rates, raise capital via OPM, and structure deals with acquisition fees and asset management fees. Real case studies show how to deploy minimal personal capital ($18-45K) while controlling larger properties through syndication.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Target 10-30 unit deals under 50 units for easier broker/seller relationships as a beginner
2. Aim for 14-20% annual returns, 6-10% cash-on-cash, and 1.8x+ equity multiples to qualify as good deal
3. Use 70% loan-to-cost financing (DSCR loans) to minimize equity needed; remaining gap filled by OPM from 5-7 investors
4. Leverage on-market broker deals to build rapport and gain access to 60%+ pre-market off-market deals before website listing
5. Every $1,000/month NOI increase adds $220,000+ in property value (NOI ÷ cap rate formula)
6. Generate revenue 5-8 ways: acquisition fee (2-3%), monthly asset management (1-2%), 30-40% cash flow, 30-40% profits, tax write-offs, refi gains, disposition fee (0.5-2%), finder fee (1%)
7. With $45K down payment, receive $26K acquisition fee back at close = $19K net out-of-pocket to control 23-unit deal
