## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches how to acquire 10-30 unit apartment buildings with minimal capital ($20k-$50k out of pocket) by leveraging OPM (other people's money), syndication structures, and broker relationships. He debunks limiting beliefs about capital requirements, expertise needs, and deal sourcing, demonstrating specific deal structures where investors bring 10% of equity but receive 40% ownership plus acquisition fees.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Target 10-30 unit deals for first investment (not 50+ units) - mom and pop sellers/brokers are more receptive to new operators
2. Bring only 10% of equity to get 40% ownership plus 2-3% acquisition fee on purchase price
3. First deal example: $1.5M property with $100k renovations + $50k closing costs = $1.65M total; 70% LTV loan = $500k down payment, but investor brings only $50k (10%) and nets $30k acquisition fee within 1 week of close, reducing out-of-pocket to $20k
4. Dedicate 5-10 hours/week to find deals while maintaining day job
5. Find deals two ways only: direct-to-broker (80% of deals sell pre-market in 2-week window before listing online) or direct-to-owner (via software like RealEstateRadar.ai)
6. Online listings (LoopNet, CoStar, Craigslist) show only ~40% of market - the 'leftovers' that don't make financial sense
7. Call brokers directly after touring 3 comparable properties in target market to get on their pre-market deal distribution list
8. Good deals attract money - focus on deal quality first (debt second, equity third); if deal makes financial sense financially (15-18%+ annual returns), capital raises itself
9. Typical investor returns 15-18%+ per year on capital - this is threshold that attracts private money from stock market/401k alternatives
10. 31-unit case study: $19k out-of-pocket grew to $590k profit over 5 years (32x return) through acquisition fee ($45k), asset management fees ($120k), and sale profits ($470k)
