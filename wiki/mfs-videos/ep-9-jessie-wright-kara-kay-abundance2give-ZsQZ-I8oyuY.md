## Summary
This is a long-form interview podcast featuring two San Diego-based entrepreneurs (Kara Kay and Jessie Wright) discussing personal values, business mindset, networking, and charitable giving. While the hosts mention multifamily real estate briefly (interest rate environment, AITD financing strategies, market dynamics), the conversation is primarily focused on mindset, authenticity, core values, and personal storytelling rather than actionable deal analysis or sourcing strategies relevant to Brian's 15-50 unit value-add business.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Network aggressively through in-person events and build mile-deep relationships with 10 quality people rather than 82 surface connections
2. Ask 'how can I serve you?' instead of 'can you teach me?' to create reciprocal value with people above your level
3. Under-promise and over-deliver: don't announce what you plan to do, only reveal results once execution is complete
4. All-Inclusive Trustee Deed (AITD) strategies allow sellers to wrap existing first mortgages with seller financing at higher rates in high-rate environments, but require attorney documentation and maintaining both insurance policies to avoid lender notification
5. Stay true to your gut and core values in business decisions rather than collecting opinions from others
6. Identify and act on opportunities quickly once you've set frequency/intention—the universe presents doors but you must recognize and walk through them
7. San Diego market dynamics: still supply-constrained despite rate increases; 70-80% of apartment value-add deals used short-term bridge debt that's now stressed as rates doubled from 3-4% to 7-8%
