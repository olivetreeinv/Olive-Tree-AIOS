## Key Takeaways
1. Target 10-30 unit deals for your first acquisition, not 50+ units (60% of deals never hit the market, only 40% visible on loopnet/CoStar)
2. Net Operating Income (NOI) is the key valuation metric: $100/month rent increase per unit on a 10-unit building adds $220,000 in value
3. Structure deals with 10% of your capital, 90% from others (OPM); acquire fees typically pay back initial investment at closing
4. Good deal metrics: IRR 14-20%+, cash-on-cash 6-10%+, equity multiple 1.8x+, all occurring simultaneously
5. Syndicators make money five ways: acquisition fees, asset management fees (paid monthly), profit splits (30-40%), refinance cash-out gains, disposition fees, and general partnership equity
6. Build broker relationships by touring 2-3 on-market properties with different brokers, then ask for pre-market deal access with language like 'we're going to buy here—do you have inventory not yet on market?'
7. The 70/30 financing split is standard: commercial lenders prefer NOI-based underwriting over personal financials for multifamily properties
