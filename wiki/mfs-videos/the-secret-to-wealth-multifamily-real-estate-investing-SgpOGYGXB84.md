## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches Brian Norton's AIOS community the fundamentals of multifamily real estate investing, covering deal sourcing through brokers, financial underwriting metrics (IRR 14-20%, cash-on-cash 6-10%, equity multiple 1.8+), and the general partner structure for value-add apartment acquisitions. The framework applies directly to Brian's stated drag areas of deal sourcing and underwriting.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use broker-led sourcing: call 3 brokers on 3 on-market properties in target market in one day to gain access to 60% off-market pre-market inventory (only 40% of deals trade on public sites like LoopNet)
2. Only consider deals meeting ALL THREE metrics simultaneously: IRR 14-20%+, cash-on-cash return 6-10%+, equity multiple 1.8+; if metrics don't align, move to next deal immediately
3. 10-30 unit deals are optimal entry point—avoid 50+ unit deals on first deals; lenders require resume, higher liquidity; scaling path: 27 units → 31 units → 88 units → 121 units → 200 units
4. General partner structure: bring 10% of capital, control 30-40% of profits via acquisition fees (2-3% at close), asset management fees (1% monthly), monthly cash flow, and disposition fees on exit
5. Raising capital requires three things: (1) deal meeting financial metrics, (2) professional pitch deck showing investment terms and projected returns, (3) documented legal process; credit score 650+ sufficient if average US credit (650) meets threshold
6. Deploy OPM through multiple sources: private lenders, seller financing, friends/family; with $188k capital deployed on $2.15M Kansas City deal, pulled 100% capital back in 24-month refinance, earned $590k net profit (3.1x return)
7. Target midwest/sunbelt markets with value-add thesis (renovations increasing NOI); use 70% loan-to-cost financing (easier than residential since lenders underwrite property financials, not personal credit)
