## Summary
Justin Brennan conducts an extensive walkthrough and mentorship of an 80-unit value-add multifamily property (NC18) in San Diego that he acquired for $17M with $3M renovation budget. He covers renovation execution, unit finishes, operational management, tenant transition strategies, and capital structure, demonstrating how to achieve nearly 2x rent growth while managing construction, property management, and investor relations.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use LVT (luxury vinyl tile) instead of LVP (luxury vinyl plank) for easier unit-level repairs and lower cost
2. Order renovation supplies as 'kits' from suppliers like HD Supply to get 20-25% discounts and improve construction efficiency at 30+ unit scale
3. Budget $20-25K per unit for full renovations in California; asbestos abatement alone costs ~$4K per unit
4. Charge rent concessions as one-time marketing costs (capitalize them) rather than recurring monthly concessions to preserve NOI and building valuation
5. Keep property managers on-site for 80+ unit buildings; off-site managers work for smaller properties but must be within 10-15 minutes
6. Close property acquisitions between the 10th-15th of month to collect 90%+ of rents before software/PM transitions
7. Implement utility reimbursement programs (RUBS or software like Utility Ranger) to recoup 65-75% of utility costs from tenants
8. Maintain 65-70% loan-to-value leverage with fixed-rate debt to survive 20-30% vacancy scenarios; never use centralized chiller systems for HVAC
