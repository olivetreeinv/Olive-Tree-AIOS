## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches a five-step apartment buying framework focusing on deal-sourcing and underwriting. He emphasizes that success in multifamily investing requires finding good deals first (through broker relationships and on-to-off-market strategies), analyzing them against specific return thresholds (14-20% IRR, 6-10% cash-on-cash, 1.8x equity multiple), and structuring deals with proper general partner and limited partner arrangements. He provides a detailed case study showing how a $188,000 investment in a 31-unit deal generated nearly $600,000 in returns across multiple fee sources.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Target 10-30 unit deals for your first multifamily investment, not 50+ units—this is where 'the numbers make sense' and you can actually execute
2. Use broker relationships instead of direct-to-seller cold outreach: 'for every phone call you're making a broker and his team are making five'
3. The deal sequence is 'deal, debt, equity'—find a good deal first, then financing, then raise equity capital; never chase money without a deal
4. Acquisition fees are typically 1-3% of purchase price; asset management fees are 1-2% of monthly revenue; disposition fees are 1-2% of sale price
5. On a $1.6M deal with $510K total equity needed, bring in 10% ($50K) out of pocket and raise the remaining 90% ($460K) from investors
6. Required deal metrics: 14-20% IRR minimum, 6-10%+ cash-on-cash return, 1.8x+ equity multiple—if deals don't meet these thresholds at first glance, 'keep moving'
7. Get on broker pre-market email lists by building rapport: tour 3 on-market properties with 3 different brokers, then ask 'do you have pre-market deals—we're going to buy here in [market]' to access deals before they're overpriced on websites
