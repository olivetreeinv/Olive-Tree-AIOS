## Summary
Justin Brennan discusses his transition from residential real estate agent to multifamily investor, emphasizing the importance of using a real estate license as a catalyst for building wealth through property syndication. He shares his personal story of starting with a $100k condo in 2010 and scaling to larger multifamily deals, while also covering broader financial literacy topics including cryptocurrency, NFTs, and the future of real estate tokenization.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Start with a partner who has capital/signing power while you provide sweat equity and deal management—commissions roll to the deals you operate
2. All-in business expenses should not exceed 19% of gross income to preserve capital for investing
3. Learn to underwrite deals properly starting with 2-4 unit properties before scaling to larger multifamily
4. You must be willing to work two full jobs (residential sales 9-5, deal sourcing/syndication 7pm-midnight) to transition from agent to investor
5. Keep only 6 months of cash reserves in fiat; convert excess to tangible assets (real estate, metals, crypto, commodities)
6. Real estate will be tokenized and fractionalized via NFTs—allows liquidity without refinancing or selling, and enables fractional ownership starting at $10 per share
7. Goodwill + Free Offer × Frequency = Revenue (Grant Cardone's customer acquisition formula)
