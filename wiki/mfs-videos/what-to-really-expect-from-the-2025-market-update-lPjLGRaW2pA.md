## Summary
Justin Brennan provides a 2025 market update and detailed walkthrough of multifamily acquisition mechanics. Market conditions show sticky interest rates (10-year treasury at 4.6%, mortgage rates in high sixes, commercial loans in low sixes to low sevens), declining property values due to rising cap rates, and distressed deals from variable-rate debt failures. He teaches the Five Pillars framework: finding deals (via brokers to access pre-market inventory), analyzing (using NOI and key metrics like 15-20% IRR, 6-10% CoC, 1.8x+ equity multiple), financing (70% leverage max, general partner brings 10% equity), closing, and managing properties.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Find deals through on-market brokers to access pre-market inventory (60% of deals never listed publicly)
2. Analyze deals requiring all three metrics: 15-20% IRR, 6-10% cash-on-cash return, 1.8x+ equity multiple—not just one or two
3. Structure as general partner with 10% of down payment while raising 90% from limited partners; get 30-40% deal ownership plus acquisition fee (1-3% of purchase price)
4. On $1M deal needing $300K down: GP brings $30K, gets $20-30K acquisition fee at close, leaving ~$0-10K true out-of-pocket
5. Every $1,000/month NOI increase = ~$220,000 property value increase (based on cap rate formula)
6. Target properties: 10-50 units in middle U.S. markets (GA/TN/AL included); avoid over-leveraging beyond 75% LTV
7. Use 70% loan-to-value financing maximum; residential financing available for 10-27 unit buildings with minimal experience if property size is 'bite-sizable'
