## Summary
Justin Brennan walks through a live case study of a mentee's 16-unit multifamily deal in Phoenix (at best-and-final offer stage for $3M purchase). The session covers offer strategy, debt assumption vs. refinancing analysis, capital raise structuring, and how to position the deal competitively while maintaining underwriting discipline.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Competition on deals is good—if you're the only offer, question if it's actually a good deal; assume existing 2.6M loan at 6.63% fixed with 48 months interest-only beats new 2.3M loan at 6.25% due to better cash flow
2. Use 10% of total equity requirement as your initial GP contribution (~$56k on $557k total equity)—this keeps your returns strong while raising the rest from LPs
3. Submit 50k earnest money deposit (roughly 1.5-2% on $3M purchase) and request 60-day close with optional 30-day extension for additional 10k deposit
4. Don't increase offer price speculatively; instead, model conservative 2024-2025 rents, then project 5-6% annual growth starting 2026 when rent growth is expected to materialize
5. Call broker before final LOI submission to confirm: how many competing offers exist, where pricing is trading, and whether you can stick to your underwriting without moving
6. Arizona/Nevada markets are attractive for low property taxes (~$450/unit insurance vs $2500-3000 in Florida, $750-1500 in Texas) and no income tax—focus on markets with job/population growth
7. Tax benefit packaging: emphasize to investors that cost segregation study (~$6k) can generate $80-95k in year-one K1 write-offs on $100k invested, effectively offering 12-15% cash return plus tax shelter
