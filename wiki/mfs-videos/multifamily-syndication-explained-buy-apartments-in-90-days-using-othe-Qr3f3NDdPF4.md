## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches how to buy 10-30 unit multifamily apartments with $20,000 out-of-pocket capital by leveraging OPM (other people's money) through syndication, obtaining 65-70% bank financing, and capturing acquisition fees (2-3%) that effectively reduce initial capital requirements. The core strategy involves positioning yourself as the General Partner bringing 10% of capital while raising 90% from investors, earning 30-40% equity plus acquisition fees and asset management fees.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Buy 15-unit deals at $50k-$150k per unit in non-coastal markets (Midwest, South, East Coast but not CA/NY/coastal)
2. Acquire property for $1.5M + $100k renovations + $50k closing costs = $1.65M all-in, finance 70% ($1.15M loan), need $500k down payment
3. GP brings 10% of down payment (~$50k), receives 30-40% of profits plus 2-3% acquisition fee (~$30k), resulting in $20k true out-of-pocket
4. $1.25 trillion in commercial loans due 2025-2026 creates stressed seller opportunities for discounted deals
5. Find deals via direct-to-broker relationships (pre-market/off-market) and direct-to-owner software targeting 150-160M properties
6. Class B properties (1980-2005 vintage with some amenities) outperform Class A and C during recessions and normal markets due to consistent occupancy
7. Bank underwrites property financials/cashflow, NOT personal credit/income—600+ credit score sufficient; property must substantiate the loan
