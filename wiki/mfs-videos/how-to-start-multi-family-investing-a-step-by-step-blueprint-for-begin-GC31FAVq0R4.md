## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches beginners how to start multifamily investing, targeting 10-30 unit boutique properties as first deals. He covers deal sourcing via brokers, underwriting metrics (14-20% IRR, 6-10% CoC, 1.8x equity multiple), and capital raising structures using general/limited partner models. He demonstrates how to raise capital through pitch decks and relationships rather than direct seller sourcing.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Target 10-30 unit 'boutique' properties for first deal, not 50+ units—easier to finance, raise capital, and manage as a beginner
2. Use 'on-market to off-market' method: tour listed properties to build broker relationships and access pre-market deals not on websites
3. Deal must produce IRR 14-20%+, cash-on-cash return 6-10%+, and equity multiple 1.8x or higher—all three metrics required or don't do the deal
4. General partner brings 10% of capital but receives 30-40% of deal profits; limited partners bring 90% and receive 70%
5. Acquisition fees, asset management fees (monthly), and cash distributions provide 5-8 different payment streams throughout deal lifecycle
6. Close escrow in 60-90 days; have 14-day lag after acceptance plus 3 months to raise capital—plenty of time if deal is bite-sized
7. Physical out-of-pocket can be minimal ($18K example on $1.5M deal) via acquisition fees and refinancing; leverage OPM to scale capital efficiency
