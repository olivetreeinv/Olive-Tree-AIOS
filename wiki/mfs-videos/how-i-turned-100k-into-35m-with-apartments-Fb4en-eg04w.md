## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches how to acquire 10-30 unit multifamily deals with minimal out-of-pocket capital ($20K) by leveraging other people's money (OPM) through structured partnerships. He breaks down deal mechanics, including acquisition fees, limited partner/general partner splits (90/10 capital, 60/40 equity), and how to access off-market deals via brokers and direct-to-owner software. The video emphasizes skill-building in deal-finding, underwriting, financing, and property management as the path to solving large financial problems and earning disproportionate returns.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. 15-unit deal structure: $1.5M purchase + $100K renovations + $50K closing costs = $1.65M all-in; 70% loan ($1.15M); GP brings 10% ($165K), gets 40% equity + acquisition fee (2-3% = $30-50K); LP brings 90%, gets 60% equity
2. Net out-of-pocket: $20K after acquisition fee ($50K in - $30K out = $20K net)
3. Revenue multiples: 3-4 deals in 10-30 unit range produce $10-20K/month passive income, enabling exit from W-2 job
4. Time commitment: 1-2 hours/day, 5-10 hours/week sufficient to execute (deal sourcing, analysis, offers)
5. 31-unit case study: $19K out-of-pocket → $590K profit after 24-month hold (24-month BRRR refinance, $355K tax-free cash, $2K/month asset mgmt fee, $470K net proceeds from sale)
6. Deal sourcing: 80% of multifamily market sells pre-market to preferred broker lists (Crexi/LoopNet = bottom 20% only); use direct-to-broker (build relationships on-market) or direct-to-owner (Real Estate Radar software with 25-30M apartment listings, skip-trace, AI likelihood-to-sell)
7. Tax strategy: Debt is tax-free money (not earned income); bonus depreciation + cost segregation can eliminate taxes for years ('zero taxes for last 8 years' example)
