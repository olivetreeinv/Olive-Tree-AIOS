## Summary
Brian Coons discusses ADU development and market dynamics in San Diego, emphasizing understanding municipal codes to identify value before the market catches on. The conversation covers construction costs ($250-350/sq ft), market saturation in North Park, and secondary markets like Linda Vista showing better cash flow. While tangentially related to Brian Norton's 15-50 unit value-add focus, the content is heavily weighted toward single-family ADU conversion rather than traditional multifamily underwriting.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Municipal code changes create arbitrage opportunities before mass adoption catches on
2. Construction costs now run $250-350 per square foot for R2 (apartment) buildings including site prep and materials/labor
3. Land value = only what you can legally build on it; back into land residual from exit assumptions
4. Smaller unit sizes (200 sq ft studios) increase per-unit costs due to kitchen/bathroom density; larger units are cheaper to build per SF
5. North Park saturation is real; secondary markets 5 minutes away (City Heights, Linda Vista, areas above the 8) show less density competition
6. Highest and best use requires understanding both zoning capability AND market demand—you can technically build 70-80 units in studios on a 7,000 sq ft lot but renters prefer larger units at $200+ premium
7. Development timelines are extending due to permit delays and construction cost inflation; projects are squeezing or breaking as interest rates and build costs diverge from original underwriting
