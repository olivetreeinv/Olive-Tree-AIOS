## Summary
Justin Brennan coaches John through underwriting a 16-unit multifamily property in South Dakota using the deal analyzer, demonstrating how to verify broker assumptions, back into realistic numbers that meet investor return metrics (15% IRR, 1.8x equity multiple, 6-10% cash-on-cash), and negotiate with brokers. He also reviews Michael's pitch deck for a 30-unit Louisville deal to show the capital-raise phase workflow.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use the income approach formula (NOI ÷ cap rate = value) to verify asking prices; don't trust broker offering memorandums
2. Back into purchase price from required metrics: 15% IRR minimum, 1.8x+ equity multiple, 6-10% cash-on-cash return, 1.25+ debt service coverage ratio
3. Set maintenance/repairs reserves at $300/unit/month (~$4,800/year for 16-unit) to avoid under-capitalization and future capital calls to investors
4. For value-add renovations, require $200-$300/month rent bump per unit to justify $5,000-$10,000/unit capex spend (return on cost analysis)
5. Use 8-10% vacancy & credit loss assumption (not broker's 5%) plus realistic operating expense ratio of 48-50% for conservative underwriting
6. Verify broker NOI against actual 2-year bank statements and P&L, not potential income; request proof of collected revenue
7. Use fully amortized debt service (not interest-only) for bank underwriting, even if seller offers IO terms initially
8. Pitch deck must answer 90% of investor questions: for $25K/$50K/$100K investment, what returns and timeline; include market comparables, team, cash flow projections, preferred return structure
