## Summary
Justin Brennan walks through underwriting a 28-unit, 1956-built Class C apartment complex in Texas pre-market deal, analyzing physical components, market positioning, financial projections, and key risk factors like chiller systems and plumbing infrastructure with Gupta who toured the property.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Inspect all plumbing laterals with cameras down every line—budget $3-5K for a 26-unit for full sewer line inspection; use findings as leverage against seller for price reduction or credit
2. Run expense ratio at minimum 10-15% vacancy/credit loss for properties 1980 or older in GA/TN/AL markets (45-55% total operating expense ratio); only drop to 5-7% in tight coastal markets
3. Verify copper wiring not aluminum—pull receptacles to confirm; aluminum creates fire risk and insurance cost increases
4. Chiller systems are deal-killers if single unit serves all 28 apartments; if it fails in summer heat, property becomes uninhabitable; avoid or require backup parts on-site and trained maintenance staff
5. Check utility bills for last 24 months in detail; don't rely on broker estimates—this is where most underwriting errors hide on older buildings
6. Aerial inspection: property should be surrounded by single-family homes and other apartments, NOT commercial/industrial; drive streets counting window bars—any bars indicate problem neighborhood
7. Pre-market deals from reputable brokers (TMG, MMG) come with pre-market advantage; use 25-question seller disclosure form as addendum before executing contract to create leverage for issues found during due diligence
8. On $2.2M deal with $67K equity piece at 10% down: acquisition fee of 2-3% ($45-67K) plus $22K earnest money plus $5-8K inspections = fully covers initial capital requirement, then reimburse from closing costs
