## Summary
Justin Brennan walks through a real live deal that his mentee Michael is pursuing in Indianapolis—a 33-unit value-add apartment complex. The deal doesn't pencil at the asking price of $3.65M, but works at ~$3.1M with a 6.2% cap rate. Justin reviews underwriting fundamentals, value-add strategies (washer/dryer connections, amenity upgrades), and broker relationship dynamics while showing raw footage from on-site property tours.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Washer-dryer connections cost $3,700-$5,000 per unit and generate ~$100/month rent bump—highest ROI value-add in older buildings
2. Frame laundry closets in kitchen nooks next to drainage/water lines and electrical panel; lose nook space but rent justifies it
3. Never pay asking price if numbers don't work; at $3.65M this deal yields only 1.1% cash-on-cash return, uninvestable
4. Only boost rents with major value-add (kitchens/baths) if you can increase rent >$200/month; otherwise it's not worth the capex spend
5. Kid play areas cost minimum $15-25K installed by certified vendors for liability compliance; dog parks are cheaper with basic 4-foot chain-link fencing
6. When touring: visit 1-2 units per type (1BR, 2BR, 3BR), takes 30-45 minutes total; don't tour all units until you're in escrow doing due diligence
7. Don't believe broker's verbal comp analysis—do your own homework on rental comps, location, amenities vs. nearby competition (Auburn Place had pool/gym here)
8. Current market cap rate floor is 6.0%+ Midwest/South; anything sub-6% is not investable given today's rate environment (10-year Treasury ~4.2-4.3%)
9. Smaller loans ($1-2M range) get 6.75% rates from local banks; larger loans ($3M+) qualify for agency debt (Fannie/Freddie) at 5.5-6.1%
10. Location aerial review: check for commercial/industrial buffer, train tracks, liquor stores, gas stations, crime zones, curb appeal, single-family residential nearby
11. Pre-market access requires existing deal history with broker; new investors can get week head-start via Facebook group + website vs. 2-3 week private list
12. Equity multiple = annual returns ÷ cash equity invested; at 30% down with 7030 split, investors should see ~14%+ annual return + 2x equity multiple
