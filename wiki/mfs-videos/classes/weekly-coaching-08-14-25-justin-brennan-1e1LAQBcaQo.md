## Summary
Justin Brennan coaches Brian Norton's AIOS community on writing Letters of Intent (LOI) for commercial multifamily acquisitions, including deal timelines, negotiation tactics, and underwriting discipline. He walks through a live LOI template, explains the offer submission process, and emphasizes how brokers use inflated assumptions to make deals appear better than they are.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. LOIs are non-binding, one-to-two page documents sent before purchase agreements; send them aggressively and frequently (aim for ~10-12 LOIs to land one deal)
2. Due diligence period is typically 30 days from PSA execution with full refundability of earnest money deposit during this window—use it to back out for any reason without disclosure
3. Economic loss assumption should be minimum 10% (vacancy + loss to lease + bad debt + concessions), not the 5% brokers quote; only high-coastal markets (Miami, San Diego, NYC) justify 5-7%
4. Cost of capital is the only legitimate way to outbid competitors; if all use traditional financing at same rates, revenue and expenses are identical, so higher bids require cheaper money or lower required returns
5. Closing timeline: 60 days standard + 30-day extension option; push back on 45-day closings unless you have a 30-day extension; extension deposits (few thousand dollars) are typically non-refundable after due diligence ends
6. Submit LOI via email only (no hard copies); immediately follow up with broker by phone/text using rapport-building language: 'I used your numbers, show me where I'm wrong'
7. Contact lenders and insurance brokers during offer phase for soft quotes to tighten underwriting; use vendor list in mentorship portal (Incorp for Wyoming LLC setup at $125/year, then get agent of service)
