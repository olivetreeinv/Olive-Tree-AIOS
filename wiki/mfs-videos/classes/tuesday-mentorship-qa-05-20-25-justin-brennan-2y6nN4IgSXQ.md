## Summary
Justin Brennan walks through a pitch deck for a 33-unit Kansas City apartment deal closing in 30 days, explaining how to structure and present investor opportunities at stages 3-4 (after deal acquisition and underwriting). The mentees (Hayden and Matt) are raising ~$730k with a 6% preferred return, 16-18% projected returns, and $25k minimum investment, demonstrating how pitch decks answer 90% of investor questions.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Pitch deck is created at stages 3-4 (after offer accepted, in due diligence/escrow) to raise capital from your investor database
2. Minimum investment threshold: $25,000 in this deal structure
3. Preferred return: 6% baseline guaranteed return, included in total 16-18% projected return
4. 80/20 equity split: investors receive 80% of profits, GP takes 20%
5. Equity multiple 1.99-2.25x means $100k investment returns $199k-$225k total
6. GP puts in 10% skin-in-game on smaller deals (33-50 units); reduces to ~5% on larger $30M+ deals
7. If raised at least 50% of equity by end of 30-day due diligence period when money goes hard, keep going; if raised nothing/very little, consider canceling to protect deposit
8. Soft commitments: build investor database before deals close using CRM; gather contact info from conversations (backyard barbecues, retail encounters, etc.)
9. Create placeholder pitch deck as marketing tool even before first deal to warm prospects and build rapport
10. Legal documents (PPM, contracts) prepared when entering escrow; use attorney templates from portal then connect with PPM lawyers for final review/naming
11. Solicit both accredited and non-accredited investors via 506(b) offering; 506(c) requires accredited-only but allows public online advertising
12. Start raising money simultaneously with deal sourcing via soft commitments, not after deal is found
13. Pitch deck template pulls 80% content from broker OM (marketing package) via copy-paste; use ChatGPT for market statistics/charts/graphs
14. Can backfill capital after closing if needed: close deal with partial equity raise then continue raising remaining capital post-close
