## Summary
Justin Brennan coaches multifamily investors on deal underwriting fundamentals, focusing on analyzing a 10-unit Idaho property and a 9-unit Kansas City deal. He teaches how to work with incomplete financials, apply reasonable expense ratios by property age, use cap rates to determine valuations, structure offers, and understand financing mechanics including bridge debt and debt service coverage ratios.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use 40-50% expense ratio for properties built 1958-1960 when seller lacks documented expenses; verify with utility invoices and tax/insurance bills
2. Apply 6% cap rate minimum for Midwest deals outside coastal zones; cap rate must approximate interest rate (currently 5.8%-7.2%) or deal won't cash flow
3. For 10-unit deal: $1,000 current rent + $150 market rent bump = $57K to $65,550 NOI annually, raising valuation from $950K to $1.1M using 6% cap rate
4. Debt service coverage ratio must be minimum 1.25x; mortgage payment cannot exceed NOI divided by 1.25 (e.g., $57K NOI allows max $45,600 annual debt)
5. Bridge debt financing: short-term (2-year) variable rate loans covering purchase + renovation, designed to refi into fixed-rate debt after property stabilizes at higher rents
6. Only three deal underwriting levers move numbers: (1) revenue/rent growth, (2) expense reduction, (3) cheaper capital; if speculatively relying on hope-and-pray rent assumptions, pass on deal
7. Syndication fee structure: for 10-unit at $1.1M purchase, add $50K renovation + $70K closing costs = $1.2M total cost; acquire 2-3% ($20-30K) as acquisition fee from capital raise
8. Plug all scenarios into deal analyzer—static napkin math confirms loan coverage but doesn't prove 14-20% IRR or 6-10% cash-on-cash needed to attract OPM investors
