## Key Takeaways
1. Call brokers directly off Crexy with brief intro: 'This is Justin Brennan with Brennan Pulley Capital Group, calling on your listing at [address]'—establish credibility without over-explaining
2. Ask brokers open-ended questions about market conditions (jobs, anchors, university presence) to build rapport and let them talk; empowers broker to like you
3. For smaller deals (10-50 units) without P&L statements, request bank statements + physical leases, then cross-match deposits against lease amounts to reconstruct revenue and expenses
4. When lacking track record, position as: 'I've done single family, now entering 10-30 unit space because we've pre-positioned market relationships (brokers, PMs, lenders, construction) to close efficiently'
5. Schedule back-to-back property showings to create FOMO/urgency; overlap investor tours by 15 minutes so prospects see other buyers evaluating simultaneously
6. Always emphasize to investors: 'We do not risk your money until close—funds sit in property-specific escrow account and are returned if deal doesn't close'
7. Pitch to investors focuses on three questions: When do you need money? What are projected returns (frame as 15-20% annually)? When do you get distributions (quarterly)
