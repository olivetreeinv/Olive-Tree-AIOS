## Summary
Justin Brennan mentors on analyzing a 27-unit value-add multifamily deal in Colleen, Texas. The group walks through underwriting adjustments including rent comps verification, expense reconciliation, DCR improvement strategies, and capital structure optimization using a deal analyzer tool.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use AI/ChatGPT to verify property taxes by address: input purchase price and get assessed value projections (expect ~90% of purchase price initial assessment)
2. 1970s multifamily buildings should operate at 45-55% expense ratio; anything above 55% signals bad operations or cost overruns requiring detailed investigation
3. Maintain 3% annual rent growth assumptions in proformas; brokers tend to overestimate—use 10% economic loss (vacancy/bad debt/concessions) minimum in Texas markets, not 5-8%
4. Separate bank accounts by function: operating account (daily rents/expenses), CapEx account (renovations/big ticket items), entity account (distributions)—prevents forensic accounting problems
5. For DCR below acceptable threshold, options are: increase down payment (35-45%), reduce purchase price, cut expenses, or refinance at year 3 to improve cash flow profile
6. Always include 'other income' line items even if broker OM shows zero: late fees, admin fees, application fees, pet rent, parking fees, utility reimbursements typically add $5k+ annually on 27-unit property
7. Submit LOI even when numbers are close but not perfect; use LOI process to build broker relationships and negotiate expense details rather than rejecting deals outright
