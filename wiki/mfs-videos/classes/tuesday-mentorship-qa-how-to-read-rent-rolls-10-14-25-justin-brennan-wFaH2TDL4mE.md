## Key Takeaways
1. Use rent roll data to verify occupancy claims—request tenant tickler/move-in-move-out reports for last 24 months to validate stated vacancy rates
2. Underwrite to 10% gross economic loss minimum (vacancy + lost lease + bad debt + concessions combined) for mid-1970s properties; brokers often hide behind artificial 5% figures
3. Always request delinquency and bad debt reports; if seller won't provide, ask for bank statements of single-property entity to forensically audit income/expenses
4. For 18-unit deals, expect local commercial bank financing (6+ years in sixes range), not agency debt; agency financing requires $1M+ loan amount minimum
5. When rent-bumping assumptions exist, implement utility reimburseable (RUBS) programs at $30-40/unit/month tiered by bedroom—example: $30 for 1BR, $40 for 2BR
6. Verify insurance and tax assumptions with seller; request proof rather than accepting stated figures, especially for acquired properties where tax basis changes
7. Structure LOI offer at $950-975K for this deal type, not at asking price; brokers expect negotiation but respect data-driven counteroffers showing true underwriting
8. Bonus depreciation provides roughly 1:1 year-one write-off ratio on investment; for $50K invested, expect ~$40-50K negative K1 statement to offset investor W2/1099 income
