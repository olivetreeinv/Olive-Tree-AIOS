## Summary
Justin Brennan conducts a live mentorship Q&A where he deep-dives into Christian's 48-unit value-add multifamily deal in Portage, Michigan (asking price $4.495M, negotiable down to ~$4.2M). He walks through deal analyzer mechanics, underwriting adjustments, refinance strategies to return investor capital, and negotiation tactics. A second deal analyzer for a 15-unit in Bennett, Texas is also reviewed for expense verification and pricing benchmarks.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Run stress tests at 45% expense ratio minimum for 1980s properties with partial renovations to be conservative
2. Section 8 housing programs pay a total housing allowance that includes utilities—if you charge higher rent, you must cover utilities, so the tenant's total benefit remains constant; verify this with housing authority before underwriting
3. Negotiate LLC purchase instead of property sale to potentially avoid property tax reassessment, but require seller indemnity for historical liabilities and ensure it's a single-purpose entity; budget for possible legal fees if school districts challenge the structure
4. Insurance replacement cost should be based on rebuild cost (unchanged day-before vs day-after purchase), not sale price; only increase coverage if currently undercovered
5. Refinance strategy: invest $1.7M total capital ($180K of yours, $1.5M raised), bump rents ~$100/month via renovations, refinance in years 3-4 to return ~40-50% of investor capital tax-free while maintaining ownership and cash flow; repeat every 3-5 years until full payout by year 10
6. Three levers to improve deal economics: increase revenue (rents), decrease expenses, or reduce purchase price; interest rates are market-dependent, so focus on what you control
7. Get soft insurance quotes early (use community vendor network) and double-check property tax assessments, repairs/maintenance baseline (~$700-800/unit for 1980s properties), and utility bills directly from seller; confirm all expense assumptions before increasing offer price
