## Summary
Justin Brennan conducts live broker calls in Ohio (Toledo, Columbus) to demonstrate deal sourcing and relationship-building with agents. He has an in-depth conversation with Beth Rose, a foreclosure and auction specialist, discussing a 40-unit multifamily property on auction, financing options, reserve pricing, and bidding strategy. The call illustrates how to build broker relationships, ask the right questions, and qualify deals via auctions.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Ask brokers 'What is the whisper price or price guidance?' when list price is unavailable—Beth Rose indicated $50-55K per door ($2-2.2M for 40 units)
2. Auctions require due diligence upfront (inspections, financials) before bidding; no contingencies post-win, so verify everything in advance
3. Bid to financials, not emotion—determine absolute max price using deal analyzer, stress-test to your required IRR (teens for syndicated deals), and hold the line
4. Register to bid by providing proof of funds for 10% down + lender letter for 65-70% loan + evidence of investor access; brokers vet before auction goes live
5. On foreclosures, call broker day-of-auction for updated market intel and reserve guidance; last-hour bidding typically sees highest activity and emotion
6. Build broker relationships before deals arise: visit market in person, meet property managers & lenders they recommend, check their app/website for pipeline deal flow
7. For first auction/market entry, physically tour property and meet broker to build trust; once rapport established, future deals can be handled more efficiently remotely
