## Summary
Justin Brennan guides mentorship students through the multifamily acquisition roadmap, focusing on two case studies: Julian navigating financial due diligence on a $1.25M Michigan deal with reconciliation issues between seller's claimed $130k NOI versus his verified ~$76-78k, and King who just went under contract on a Fort Worth, TX property. The coaching covers critical underwriting discipline, seller transparency requirements, and the importance of verifying all income sources and expense line items before committing capital.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Maintenance & repair should run at $700-800 per unit annually for typical properties; if seller claims $7k total for 20+ units, investigate deeply
2. Economic loss (vacancy) should be calculated at 10% of gross rental income as a conservative standard
3. For $205k annual rent with 10% vacancy loss ($20k) plus other income (~$17k), effective gross income = $202k; expenses at 50% = $100k NOI, not the 64% expense ratio claimed
4. Always reconcile seller's claimed NOI line-item by line-item: rent roll × occupancy minus vacancy, plus documented other income (RUBs, application fees with bank statement proof)
5. Bad debt write-offs don't increase usable income—they're already deducted in economic loss; $23k bad debt means $23k additional vacancy impact
6. Never accept 'cash-based system' as explanation for unverified income; all rental income must flow through single-purpose entity bank account (required by lenders anyway)
7. If numbers don't pencil at seller's asking price after rigorous due diligence, present clear choice: show verified documentation of additional income/lower expenses, or accept lower purchase price (e.g., $900k vs $1.25M)
8. Do 'ugly duckling in decent area' value-adds—worn property surrounded by single-family homes with pride of ownership, churches, new construction nearby shows path of progress
9. Solar screens (~$0.15-0.18/sq ft application) improve curb appeal and create uniform exterior without visible unit interiors
10. Tar and seal parking lots ($0.15-0.18/sq ft) refreshes appearance without full replacement; restripe and it looks new
11. Sample/mock pitch deck sent to warm database of 25+ people establishes multifamily investor identity before deals exist, builds soft commitments and trust foundation
12. When under contract with 7-14 day PSA negotiation window, don't let brokers rush; use Texas commercial contract with addendums for smaller deals (King's situation)
13. Landing page + pitch deck + CRM database setup BEFORE deal procurement allows immediate capital raise infrastructure when LOI is accepted
