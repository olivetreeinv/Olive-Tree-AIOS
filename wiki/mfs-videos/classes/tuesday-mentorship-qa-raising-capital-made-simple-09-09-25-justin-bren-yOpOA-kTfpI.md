## Summary
Justin discusses capital raising fundamentals for multifamily syndications, focusing on soft commitments, pitch decks, and investor relationship-building. He covers preliminary marketing setup (websites, LLCs, branding) to establish credibility before securing deals, and explains how to collect investor commitments before signing purchase agreements.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Soft commitments are collected during pre-deal phase through conversations—investor says 'when you have a deal, send me info' and you add them to CRM
2. Create a mock/sample pitch deck highlighting tax benefits (bonus depreciation, K-1 offset) to send to soft-committed investors before you have an actual deal
3. LOI is 'worthless, literally worth the paper it's on'—not legally binding; write 20+ LOIs in 60 days if needed to get first deal
4. Show brokers 10% of your equity requirement in bank statement or LOI (e.g., $1M property = show $10K-30K down payment proof)—you syndicate the rest
5. For 30-50 unit deals, target: CAP rate above interest rate environment (currently 6%+), cash-on-cash 6-10% minimum, IRR 16-18%+ ideally
6. Use A2P registration (federal law) for CRM text messaging; requires LLC with EIN, website, trust center setup in CRM to comply
7. Partnership on 200+ unit deals: mentee becomes co-GP, receives portion of acquisition fee, gets track record for future larger deals
