## Summary
Justin Brennan conducts a detailed coaching session analyzing Rory's 12-unit multifamily deal in Missouri listed at $635,000. The session covers deal sourcing through broker relationships, underwriting mechanics using the deal analyzer tool, syndication structuring with preferred returns and equity splits, and advanced tax strategies including bonus depreciation, cost segregation studies, and capital gains management through strategic quarterly exits.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use broker relationship building and off-market deal sourcing via websites (LoopNet, CoStar) to identify brokers, not just properties, and nurture relationships through personal touches like gifts
2. Structure syndications with 6% preferred return to investors until 100% return of capital, then 70/30 split (70% to LPs, 30% to GP/operator)
3. Run 20% down payment with 80% investor capital on smaller deals to control deal while only deploying $60K-$80K out of pocket after acquisition fees (typically 3%)
4. Project 8% economic loss factor on 12-unit complexes due to natural vacancy rates of 4-5% in workforce housing areas near schools
5. Bonus depreciate approximately 67% of purchase price (e.g., $400K on $600K property) with majority coming year one, generating dollar-for-dollar tax write-offs against taxable income
6. Achieve 25-28% annualized returns on operator capital when combining preferred return allocation (6%), equity split (30%), asset management fees (1% of revenue), and construction management fees (8% of capex)
7. Execute strategic property sales in Q1 to capture 9-month runway for reinvestment of capital gains into new deals, effectively creating a backdoor 1031 exchange to defer recapture taxes
