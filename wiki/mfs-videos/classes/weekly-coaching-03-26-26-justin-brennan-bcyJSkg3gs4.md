## Summary
Justin conducts a live broker call with Zachary in Charlotte, NC regarding a 16-unit value-add property and discusses deal sourcing strategy, local lender options, and property manager recommendations. While directly relevant to Brian's deal sourcing challenge, the session focuses primarily on relationship-building mechanics rather than deal-specific analysis or underwriting frameworks.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Call brokers on specific deals first to establish relationships, then ask 'do you have any others you're working on BOVs for' to uncover pre-market opportunities
2. Face-to-face market visits convert phone relationships into trusted local connections - schedule in-person meetings when traveling to a market
3. For 10-30 unit deals under $3.2M, target lenders offering bridge-to-permanent products (bridge at acquisition + renovation, roll to permanent at 85% occupancy with no rollover fees)
4. Local property managers that traditionally handled single-family but want to enter multifamily offer better terms and service than firms specializing exclusively in multifamily
5. When asking brokers for recommendations, use tactful framing: 'what are some property managers your clients use' rather than 'who do you recommend' to get honest intel without putting them on the spot
