## Summary
Justin Brennan analyzes an off-market bank-owned 44-unit deal in McAllen, Texas priced at $2.7M with 37% down payment ($220K). He walks through underwriting methodology including expense ratio benchmarking, rent growth assumptions, renovation cost per unit ($5-6K), refinance scenarios, and investor capital account tracking, demonstrating how to verify income/expenses and structure returns.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Target 18% or greater return on cost (ROC) for renovation metrics to justify capex spend
2. Use 5-year fixed loans on stable value-add deals to enable refinancing flexibility without prepayment penalties, enabling rate drops 3+ years out
3. Benchmark expense ratios: 35-45% for 1980-2010 properties, targeting mid-40s for 1993-era assets; Texas properties run higher due to insurance/taxes
4. Conduct lease audits verifying names match between leases and rent roll; require bank statements if seller cannot produce T12 financials with supporting docs
5. Establish three separate checking accounts within property LLC: operating (property manager access only), capital improvement, and entity/investor account (owner-controlled)
6. Structure equity splits (70/30 GP-LP preferred over 80/20) and 6% preferred returns (not 8%) to optimize investor distributions
7. Never allow property manager to establish or control bank accounts; this creates leverage risk if terminating management
