## Summary
Justin Brennan conducts a mentorship session covering deal analysis tools, partnership structures via GP allocations, and due diligence processes. He walks through a 29-unit Oklahoma City value-add deal with Gideon, discussing underwriting mechanics, loan structure, and refinance strategies to return capital to investors within 24-36 months.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use the 0-50 unit deal analyzer (updated this morning with a toggle for renovation dollars in loan vs. separate equity raise)
2. Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) must exceed 1.25 minimum; aim for 1.32+ on tight deals
3. Expense ratios for mid-1970s properties should run 40-50%+ (not the 30s brokers often claim)
4. On value-add deals, structure initial down payment at 40-45% to meet DSCR, then refinance within 18-24 months to recover 50-60% of capital and reset equity
5. Investor preferred returns calculated annually (e.g., 6% per year) paid before capital return; document the threshold (e.g., 'once I achieve 15% annualized return, I can pay you out')
6. Section 8 fair market value rents post-renovation can jump significantly (1-bed $900→$1,100, 2-bed $1,000→$1,200) but lenders underwrite current financials, not future hope
7. GP allocation: deal-finder 5%, due diligence 10%, boots-on-ground 10%, asset manager 10%, loan signer 20%, risk capital 15%, capital raising 30% (pro-rata split within that bucket)
