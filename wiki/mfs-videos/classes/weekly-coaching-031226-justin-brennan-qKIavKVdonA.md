## Summary
Justin Brennan demonstrates live broker outreach by calling on a 20-unit fractured condo apartment complex in Clearwater, Florida. He models how to initiate contact using a specific property as the conversation lure, build rapport with the broker (Sebastian), and pivot toward establishing a relationship for future deal flow. The call reveals market conditions in Florida and underwriting principles, though no actual deal analysis occurs.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use a specific property as the 'lure' when calling brokers—don't open with generic requests for deal flow
2. Build rapport by having a natural conversation rather than following a rigid script; let brokers speak to gather information
3. Brokers underestimate expenses to prop up NOI; standard expense ratios: pre-1979 properties 45-55%, 1980-2005 properties 39-45%, post-2005 properties 30s depending on state
4. Two types of sellers in current market: those who bought pre-2020 with good basis, or those in deep debt trouble; sellers from 2022-2024 buying window are likely distressed
5. Leverage 65-70% on deals with mid-six interest rates; local credit unions and regional banks prefer smaller deal sizes (15-50 units) over agency lenders for initial purchases
6. 'Loss to lease' means the difference between current in-place rents and market rents on a specific property (e.g., $150-200 spread), not market comps
7. Follow broker phone calls with in-person market trips to build personal relationships ('belly to belly')—this cements you in broker's top 5 for future deal flow
