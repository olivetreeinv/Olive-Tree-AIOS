## Summary
Justin Brennan coaches Enoch through underwriting a 40-unit apartment deal in Montgomery, Alabama priced at $3.25M with an assumable loan. The core issue is insufficient revenue due to the unit mix (one-bedroom and studio apartments) making the deal difficult to pencil at current market rates, despite strong exterior renovations and an attractive assumable loan rate of 5.59%.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Assumable loans: adjust down payment percentage until the loan amount matches the assumed debt amount (in this case $1.8M at 5.59%)
2. Cap rates must align with interest rates in current market conditions—don't buy properties at cap rates significantly below lending rates (avoid 5.4% cap when rates are 6%+)
3. Use Rentometer.com and Zillow for high-level rent verification, then get boots on the ground in the market to confirm rent levels by neighborhood
4. Price per unit is misleading on unit-mix deals—focus on total revenue generation; one-bedroom units at $825 rent differently than two-bedroom at potentially $1,050+
5. Target exit cap rate of 6.5% for Montgomery, Alabama market (6.5%-7% range)
6. Operating expense ratio for 1980s+ properties should be 40-50% of gross revenue
7. Deal structure: on 40-unit deals, assumable loans may require 38-40% down payment vs. traditional 30-35% financing
8. LOI template format: price per unit, 1% earnest deposit going non-refundable post-due diligence, 30-day due diligence with extension option, 60-day close with 30-day option to extend
