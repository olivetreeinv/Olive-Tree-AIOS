## Summary
Justin Brennan mentors multifamily syndication operators on attracting investors through pitch decks and capital raises. He reviews Samir's 12-unit San Antonio deal pitch deck (showing 19.77% IRR, 2.7x equity multiple, 8% preferred return), explains investor fund flow mechanics, and teaches strategy for overcoming tax events to fuel deal raises.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Pitch deck must show minimum investment (e.g., $25k) with projected returns and capital stack; include both $25k and $100k investor examples on separate pages
2. Sell properties in Q1 (Jan-Feb-Mar) to give 9-10 months for bonus depreciation to offset capital gains; avoid 1031 exchanges for smaller deals under $1M
3. Establish property-specific LLC with dedicated Bank of America account; investors wire funds only after signed PPM, no commingling of funds
4. For first deals in 10-30 unit range, raise $300k-$600k total equity (syndicate ~90% from partners, retain ~10%); start with 25k commitments to build momentum
5. Position yourself as strategic partner on website/brokers; be transparent with lenders about being new/smaller; brokers prefer bite-sized 10-30 unit deals for newer operators
6. Visit property 3 times: Sunday afternoon, evening when tenants home, and one random time to assess neighborhood conditions and identify red flags
7. For 12-unit deal raising ~$400k, expect 4-7 total investors; no SEC involvement if raising from known relationships only; operating agreement sufficient vs. full PPM for friend/family rounds
