## Key Takeaways
1. Capital tracking spreadsheet set up once, runs itself for life of deal with quarterly investor statements
2. Preferred return paid first before GP split; GP double-dips as both investor (Class A) and general partner receiving asset management fees
3. Rent concessions must be one-time lump sum (not monthly recurring) on second full month of lease to preserve P&L—capitalize as marketing expense below NOI line
4. Stagger lease expirations across 10-14 months to maintain 70%+ tenant retention; target ~9-10 units turning annually on 30-unit building
5. Bookkeeper cost ~$250/quarter to input property financials into QuickBooks; CPA ~$2K annually for tax returns; use App Folio or Rent Tech for PM software
6. Always use your own LOI template for offers rather than wholesaler/broker documents; back into price based on financials, don't accept asking prices
7. Wholesalers stack assignment fees on top of purchase price and often don't know deal financials; request unpriced deals and 'whisper price' guidance but verify independently
