## Summary
Justin Brennan coaches Brian Norton's AIOS mentees on setting up administrative/business infrastructure (LLC, website, CRM, logo) as a prerequisite for attracting lenders, brokers, and capital partners. He emphasizes syndication structures where new investors bring 10% equity and raise 90%, using sweat equity (deal-finding, underwriting, management) to offset lack of capital. He also discusses networking, fundraising psychology, and positioning multifamily investing as solving major wealth-building problems for investors.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Set up LLC, website, CRM with ~25 names, and logo within first 3 weeks to look professional to lenders/brokers/investors
2. New deal sponsors bring 10% down payment capital and syndicate remaining 90% from limited partners
3. Average first-deal investor size should be $25k-$50k; Justin's first deal raised $800k from ~7 investors across $25k-$175k checks
4. First deal target returns: 14-20% IRR to investors; 2-3x equity multiple; ~7% cash-on-cash return minimum
5. Use indirect social media posting for 506(b) offerings (stories, feeds) without pitching terms; reserve paid ads/webinars for 506(c)
6. 60-day standard closing timeline (with 30-day extension option in LOI) allows time for due diligence, inspections, lending, and capital raising
7. Pitch deals to database by sending pitch deck, then follow up in few days asking 'Who do you know?' instead of directly selling
8. Use capital accounts tracking spreadsheet (template) to manage and track investor contributions quarter-by-quarter; email statements to investors each quarter
