## Summary
Justin Brennan conducts a year-end mentorship Q&A covering letter of intent (LOI) fundamentals using Miguel's 50-unit San Antonio deal as a case study, addresses market conditions and deal evaluation across multiple properties, and discusses underwriting challenges including broker number manipulation and location risk assessment.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Write 2-3 LOIs per week; they're not legally binding so treat them as practice repetitions to build comfort
2. LOI deposit is typically around 1% of purchase price; can negotiate non-refundable status after due diligence period (e.g., 45 days due diligence, 75 days to close)
3. Extension clauses should require additional escrow deposit (e.g., $5,000) for 30-day extensions beyond initial closing timeline
4. Verify broker underwriting numbers: flag artificially low maintenance/repairs and zero turnover on older buildings (e.g., 1969 building should have realistic M&R)
5. For agency debt financing, loan amount must be $1M minimum; below that threshold expect 6.25-6.75% rates from local/regional banks
6. Use 8% property tax estimates in underwriting for verification; request Google Maps research on immediate surrounding area for location red flags
7. Red flags: 24-hour gas stations/Circle K next door (transient traffic), boarded windows (security screens indicate area issues), highway noise, closed restaurants (economic indicators)
8. Debt service coverage ratio and timing matter more than perfect year-one cash flow on value-add deals; acceptable to run negative first year if rents are achievable
