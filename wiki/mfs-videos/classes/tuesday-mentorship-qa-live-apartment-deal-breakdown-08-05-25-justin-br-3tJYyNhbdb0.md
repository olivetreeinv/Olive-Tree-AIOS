## Summary
Justin conducts a live mentorship session breaking down Robbie's 10-unit (5 duplex) value-add deal in Nampa, Idaho purchased at $1.173M with 25% down. The deal features significant upside from rent increases ($150-200/unit without improvements) and potential lot consolidation enabling new unit construction worth ~$800K in additional value. Discussion covers deal sourcing (off-market through personal network), underwriting methodology, financing structure, and how to evaluate properties when incomplete broker data is provided.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Request T12 profit & loss statement and rent roll as minimum critical documents when broker flyers lack complete data
2. Use ChatGPT with property address to pull population growth, job growth, median household income projections and new developments to validate market opportunity
3. Rent increases of $150-200/month can be achieved on undermarket mom-and-pop properties without any unit renovations
4. Hire civil engineer locally (cost $250-300K) to combine multiple lots into one, which can add ~$800K in value through additional unit construction potential
5. Insurance costs may be artificially low on multi-property portfolios due to blanket cross-collateralized policies—must ask broker this question and underwrite to individual property insurance costs post-acquisition
6. Deal analyzer spreadsheet: only blue-highlighted cells are editable/have no formula; all non-blue cells are formula-based and auto-populate when inputs change
7. Use 6-10% cash-on-cash return range with aligned equity multiples and IRR as the decision threshold—if property fits these ranges, write the LOI
