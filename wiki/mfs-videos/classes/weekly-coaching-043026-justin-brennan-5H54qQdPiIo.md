## Summary
Justin Brennan analyzes Christian's 28-unit Indola Avenue deal using updated Version 10 analyzer, identifies fundamental issues with seller's $2.4M asking price, and guides Christian through proper underwriting including realistic expense assumptions, renovation budgeting, and capital structure to achieve 14-15% IRR at ~$1.45-1.55M offer price. Additionally, Justin demonstrates broker outreach by calling Cincinnati broker JD on a 33-unit value-add opportunity, discussing property management, financing options, and Ohio tax strategies (drop-and-swap LLC structures).

## Key Takeaways
1. For 20-unit multifamily, eliminate onsite payroll entirely—use offsite professional management company; any internal payroll means 'they're fired'
2. Budget minimum $8,000 per unit for renovation if rents are below-market; do not include reno costs in loan amount, keep in separate reno budget
3. Always run 10% credit loss/vacancy assumption regardless of seller claims (ignore 2% figures sellers present)
4. Debt coverage ratio (DCR) is the binding constraint for loan approval; when property doesn't pencil, increase down payment to 35-40% rather than lowering offer price further
5. Use properties as 'lure-in' tools with brokers even if deal won't work—send LOI at 30%+ discount to establish rapport, get on offmarket lists, and build relationships for future deal flow
6. Ohio tax strategy: use 'drop and swap' LLC structure where seller creates new entity, transfers property pre-close, then sells LLC to buyer—hides sale price from county assessor and prevents automatic tax reassessment
7. Target underwriting standards for 10-50 unit Class B/C deals: 1960-2000 vintage, light-to-medium value-add only, assume 6-8% preferred returns with 70/30 GP/LP split to attract capital
