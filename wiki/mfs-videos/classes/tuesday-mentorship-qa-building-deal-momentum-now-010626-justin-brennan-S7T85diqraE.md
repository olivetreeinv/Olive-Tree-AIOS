## Key Takeaways
1. Investors receive 60-70% of deal returns split amongst them; pay out quarterly from cash flow
2. Cost segregation study completed within 1-2 months of closing; if doing renovations, inform cost seg company to include them in depreciation
3. Bonus depreciation squeezes 39-year commercial property depreciation into 5 years, with majority received in year one
4. If you've raised at least 50% of required capital by end of due diligence period, proceed with deal close
5. Close with down payment + closing costs only (typically needed at closing); can raise remaining capital including renovation budget post-close within 30 days
6. Replacement reserves: $250-300 per unit per year built into annual operating expenses; add extra 10-15% on renovation budget as starting reserve capital to avoid under-capitalization
7. Light renovations: $3,500-5,500 per unit; medium renovations: $7,000-10,000 per unit; heavy renovations: $14,000-25,000 per unit
8. Seller financing typically 1-1.1% closing costs; match term to first loan (ideally 5 years); seller retains any accumulated reserve account at sale exit
