## Summary
Justin Brennan reviews a 23-unit value-add deal in Lancing, Michigan under contract at ~$1.2M, discussing underwriting fundamentals, cap rate compression, forced appreciation through rent increases, roof condition negotiations, and the refinance strategy to return investor capital within 5 years. He also addresses underwriting adjustments between capex and deferred maintenance, uses Real Estate Radar for off-market deal sourcing, and explains how execution creates deal flow through direct-to-owner outreach.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Cap rate compression math: $100/month rent increase on 12-unit = $233k value add at same cap rate
2. Refinance strategy: Year 5 refi at 70% LTV of compressed-cap-rate value can return all investor capital while owner retains 100% equity
3. Roof inspection critical: $100k potential cost—negotiate shared responsibility by showing inspection report; you'll pay it now or higher when selling in 5 years
4. Keep $50-150k per unit price range for bite-sized loan amounts (<$1M) and capital raises (<$1M) on first/second deals
5. Deferred maintenance vs capex: Ask 'did it increase rent?' If no (roof, sewer, appliances, flooring turnover), it's deferred maintenance operational expense, not capex
6. Real Estate Radar filters: Loan maturity dates narrowed to current year = 1,300 Ohio sub-50-unit properties; owners must refinance or sell—skip value estimates, focus on skip-tracing direct-to-owner outreach to avoid brokers
7. Section 8 properties require market-specific cap rate reality: Akron, OH at 21 cap rate realistic; no natural appreciation in upper Midwest—only forced value-add via operations/renovations
