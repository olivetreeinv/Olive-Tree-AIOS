## Key Takeaways
1. Require T12 and rent roll before writing LOI: 'Send me over the T12 and rent roll and I'll go ahead and analyze and get you an LOI based off the financials'
2. Use 50% expense ratio as baseline for underwriting Midwest 1960s properties: 'You're in a 1962 property in the Midwest, upper Midwest. You're going to be right around 50-55% expense ratio'
3. Interior renovation budgets: '7 to 15,000 a unit for interior renos, light to heavy. And then your exterior maybe five or so thousand exterior per unit'
4. Never get attached to asking price; let financials dictate offer: 'I never get attached to what they ask. I watch what the property's telling me'
5. Use Goal Seek in Excel to back into purchase price based on cap rate or IRR targets
6. Ask brokers for 3 vendor referrals: PM companies, local commercial lenders/CMBs, and renovation/construction contractors
7. Deferred maintenance items (roof replacement) warrant price reduction or seller credit, not capital raise: 'that's a deferred maintenance item that you'd probably be asking for all or portion of credit for from the seller'
8. Verify lease documents: sample audit to confirm rent roll names/terms match actual lease agreements
