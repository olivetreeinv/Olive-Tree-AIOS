## Summary
Justin Brennan mentors Brian Norton's multifamily team on deal sourcing and underwriting tactics. Key discussions include LOI submission strategy, proof of funds requirements, lease audit procedures, seller representations and warranties protection, and identifying red flags like sudden lease spikes and occupancy vs. delinquency mismatches. The session emphasizes brokers' due diligence on buyers and the importance of professional presentation through websites and buyer questionnaires.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Submit LOI with financing structure: 30% down payment from equity partners, your 10% of that amount, soft quote from lender for 70% loan, syndicated equity to fill remaining gap
2. Provide proof of funds as 10% of down payment (e.g., $50K on $500K down payment) separately via email with soft lender quote when brokers request it
3. Flag new lease volume: if 90+ days shows excessive new leases on small properties, request tenant applications and screening details—red flag indicates fake occupancy without paying tenants
4. Analyze delinquency reports against occupancy claims—property at 96% occupancy with 15% delinquency means economically vacant, not paying rent
5. Include representations and warranties section in purchase agreement to protect against fraudulent seller disclosures and require seller backup documentation
6. Never submit offers on auction deals for first investment—requires all-cash, non-contingent offer, and $25K+ refundable registration deposit
7. Build professional business presence before first deal: website, company logo, bio/headshot, capital partner structure publicly visible so brokers verify legitimacy during 11th-hour buyer questionnaire stage
