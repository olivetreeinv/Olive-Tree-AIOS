## Summary
Justin Brennan coaches Brian Norton on a 10-unit value-add deal in Virginia Highlands, Atlanta, priced at $2M. The core lesson: modern market conditions (6-6.5% cap rates, 6.5% interest rates) require buying based on current cash flow breakeven or better, not future appreciation hopes. Brian must offer ~$1.35M (6% current cap rate) instead of the $2M ask, and use this as a broker negotiation framework.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Current cap rate must be 6-6.5% minimum based on today's interest rates—back into purchase price using current NOI, not proforma
2. Full HVAC renovation may cost $15-20K/unit; consider mini-splits to save on ducting labor costs
3. Comp rents in Virginia Highlands are $1,395-$1,500, not the $1,650-$2,100 broker claims—use actual market rents, not hope-and-pray numbers
4. Tell overpricing brokers: 'No bank will loan me based on hope-and-pray numbers. That was 2021-2022. Today it's about cash flow and financials.'
5. If broker won't accept your real numbers offer, ask them to co-invest equity—their refusal proves the deal doesn't work
6. You cannot carry negative cash flow on current numbers into a 24-month renovation; must break even or cash flow on day-one
7. Send underwritten proforma to broker with the line: 'Tell me where I'm wrong' rather than just walking away—keeps door open for future deals
