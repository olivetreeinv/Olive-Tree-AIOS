## Summary
Justin Brennan mentors Brian Norton's AIOS cohort on capital foundation and deal sourcing. Core teaching: execute a mock deal (pitch deck, landing page, summary video) to warm investor database before securing real deals, using a professional presentation to establish credibility and generate soft commitments. Also covers deal underwriting mechanics including expense normalization, broker relationship-building, and avoiding fractured condo deals without HOA control.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Create mock deal with pitch deck + landing page + 15-min summary video, send to 30-person database even without real deal to warm capital
2. Send email asking 'Who do you know that may have interest?' rather than asking contacts directly for money—expect 11 of 30 to respond, 7 to ask why you didn't ask them, 5 to schedule calls
3. Set up 3 checking accounts at same bank for operating, capex, and entity accounts to prevent expense commingling and cleaner financials
4. Normalize property expenses using industry benchmarks (e.g., 1996 building in fair condition = 35-45% expense ratio, 1975 Midwest = 48-50%) when brokers bundle capex into R&M
5. Use cap rate minimum of 0.5 points above interest rate (e.g., if 6.5% rate, buy at 6%+ cap minimum) to avoid negative leverage
6. Current commercial loan rates: $2M+ gets high-5s (5.8-5.9%), agency debt $1.5M+ gets mid-5s, local banks $1M-$2M gets 6.25-6.9%
7. Only consider fractured condo deals if you control majority HOA seats; without control you're vulnerable to assessment hikes that ruin economics
8. Keep deal landing page link in phone notes for text sharing at networking events—signals professionalism without asking for money directly
