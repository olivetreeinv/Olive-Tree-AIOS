## Summary
Justin Brennan coaches Julian, a newer investor working on his first multifamily deal in Michigan, on deal underwriting fundamentals including cap rates, debt coverage ratios, interest rate environments, and how to structure financing with renovation budgets. The session covers why broker proformas often show unrealistic returns through negative leverage, how to identify distressed sellers, and the importance of keeping first deals under $1M in total equity raise.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Cap rate must be within ~0.5% of interest rate for deals to work—if buying at 5.25% cap with 6.5% rates, you have negative leverage and must put down 40-50%, killing returns
2. Debt Coverage Ratio must equal 1.25x minimum: if mortgage payment is $1,000, NOI must be $1,250 to cover debt plus cash flow
3. Construction/renovation loans cost 8% vs. permanent loans at 6-6.75%; keep renovations under $100K and raise separately as equity to avoid higher rates
4. Rule of thumb: always ask seller 'when did you buy?'—2017-2018 buyers have good basis; 2021-2024 buyers selling now are distressed and may have zero/negative equity
5. First deal equity raise should stay under $800K-$1M maximum unless you have established HNW investor relationships; Julian's 16-unit at $975K total property price requiring $281-458K equity is sized right
6. Light to medium value-add only: target 1970s or newer, avoid heavy renovation (>$5-10K per unit)—properties needing 'bombs' rebuilt are too risky for first deals
7. Interest rates 6-6.5% expected in current market; calculate using deal analyzer—if cap rate + 0.5% gap from interest rate, metrics will show poor cash-on-cash (5.5%) and IRR (11%)
