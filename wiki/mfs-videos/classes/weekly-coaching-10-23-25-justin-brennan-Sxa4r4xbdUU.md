## Summary
Justin Brennan coaches mentees on setting up professional infrastructure (website, CRM, LLC, pitch deck, landing pages) to attract capital before securing deals. He demonstrates how establishing credibility through online presence and team positioning enables easier fundraising when deals arrive, using his 27-unit deal ($900K raised) as proof of concept.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Set up LLC, website, landing page, and pitch deck 1-3 months BEFORE getting a deal—look the part before you have the part
2. Trust is the foundation: investors ask 'Do I trust you'll return my $25K, $50K?' before evaluating the deal itself
3. Build a CRM database of 25+ soft-committed contacts; from 25 contacts, expect ~11 responses and ~7 actual investors (27-unit deal: 7 of 11 invested ~$400K total)
4. Minimum investor commitment threshold: ~$25K per investor; position returns at 15-20% annually to attract capital
5. Use Loom (free screen-recording tool) to create summary videos by walking through pitch deck—share via email, text, YouTube without mass marketing compliance issues
6. Website should include: About Us with your photo, Investment Strategy, Opportunities pages, Contact form linking to Calendly, downloadable pitch deck behind form submission
7. Connect all tools pre-deal: website → CRM contacts → pitch deck → summary video → Calendly scheduling = seamless investor flow when deal launches
