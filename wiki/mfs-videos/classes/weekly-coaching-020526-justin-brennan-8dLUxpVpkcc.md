## Summary
Justin Brennan conducts live broker calls with a multifamily agent (Len Johnson) in Huntsville, Alabama to demonstrate deal sourcing and broker relationship-building. He emphasizes identifying market-maker brokers, asking strategic questions about property renovations and rent bumps, and building relationships before entering contract.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Identify market makers by looking for broker logos appearing repeatedly across 10-30 unit deals in target markets—these brokers are doing volume and can provide pre-market deal flow
2. Ask brokers specific questions about renovation costs, rent bumps (e.g., 'What kind of bumps are you getting from classic to renovated units?'), and remaining value-add upside
3. Request soft quotes from lenders early by saying 'based on X information, what loan could you offer with these contingencies'—lenders rarely refuse soft quotes
4. Build broker relationships by edifying them, letting them speak, and stating your buybox clearly: '10-30 unit, 50K-150K per door, sunbelt/midwest, decent cap rate that meshes with current rates'
5. Set up your market team (brokers, property managers, lenders, contractors) before writing offers so due diligence and closing run smoothly
6. Tour properties only after serious buyer earnest money is deposited to avoid wasting broker time on tire-kickers
7. Ask brokers about off-market deals coming 1-2 weeks before they list on CoStar or public sites by building relationship first
