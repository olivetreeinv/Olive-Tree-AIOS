## Summary
Justin Brennan coaches Kurt through underwriting a 14-unit off-market deal in Nampa, Idaho being offered at $2.65M with seller financing at 5.5% interest. The deal struggles to pencil at current pricing due to low fundamentals and Idaho's tax-avoidance-driven market dynamics, with fundamental issues in expense assumptions (management fees underestimated, utilities, insurance, maintenance) and overall returns that don't support multifamily investment criteria.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Ask broker directly: 'How many apartments do you own and manage?' to verify real management costs are 8% all-in (5-6% fee + lease commissions, turnover costs)
2. Triple-check R&M costs on 1980 buildings: should be $600+ per unit, not $430; verify what CapEx has been deferred
3. Demand to see broker's non-operational expense line (below NOI line on P&L) where operators hide true costs; if broker 'smirks' they know they're hiding expenses
4. Metering setup matters: understand individual electric/gas meters vs. master meter to properly allocate utility reimbursement at 60-65% recovery rate
5. Utilities passed back: landlord currently pays all utilities but should implement RUBS (ratio utility billing system) to charge tenants back; verify this with broker
6. On 1980 duplex properties (7 duplexes = 14 units): multiple roofs and systems increase maintenance vs. single building; expect higher expense ratios
7. Idaho market buying on fundamentals at 5.4 cap rate signals 1031 exchange tax-avoidance buyers, not operational buyers; ask broker if deals are marketed to 1031 buyers vs. fundamentals players
