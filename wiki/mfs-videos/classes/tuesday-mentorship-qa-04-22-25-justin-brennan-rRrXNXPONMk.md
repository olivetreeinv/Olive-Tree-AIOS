## Summary
Justin Brennan mentors Tony and Ryan through underwriting challenges on a 12-unit Kansas City deal in escrow. Key issues: RUBs (reimbursable utilities) were embedded in the rent roll causing double-counting, inflating NOI; lender requirements (1.27 DCR, 16%+ IRR for investors) forced a price renegotiation from $1.215M to $1.05M-$1.15M; property management fee assumptions and parking deficiencies reduced returns.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. RUBs must be separated from rent in underwriting—ask brokers explicitly: 'Are RUBs included in this rent roll?'
2. Underwrite to third-party PM fees of 7-8% blended (including leasing costs), not owner-quoted discounts like 5%
3. Lender DCR minimums are typically 1.27; secure 6.25-6.75% rates; non-recourse loans are standard but negotiate partial recourse caps (e.g., recourse only up to $300K of $680K loan)
4. Back into purchase price using investor return targets: need north of 16% IRR for limited partners; 14-15% IRR minimum is insufficient to attract capital
5. Detach emotionally from deal; use broker's own numbers in analysis so seller can't blame underwriting; if numbers don't work, renegotiate or walk
6. Visit out-of-market properties in advance of offers to validate rents, parking, neighborhood quality, and comps with local brokers and PM companies
7. Year 1 cash-on-cash can be tight (5-6%) if rent growth is projected year 2—verify improvement trajectory and operator's execution ability
8. Knock on resident doors to validate pain points (e.g., lack of off-street parking) rather than relying on seller-aligned property managers
