## Summary
Justin Brennan walks through updates to the Simple Deal Analyzer tool used for 0-50 unit multifamily deals, focusing on how to calculate and present investor returns in pitch decks. He explains waterfall calculations, cash-out refinance scenarios, the tax benefits of leverage, and how to provide two standardized investment examples ($25K and $100K) to potential capital partners.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Provide only two investor examples on pitch decks: one at the minimum investment ($25,000) and one at six figures ($100,000), as statistically a ~$600K raise will have 4-5 investors at $25-50K and 2-3 at six figures
2. Keep hurdle one and two identical with 0% GP return and 100% LP return to avoid formula errors that incorrectly show equity paydowns
3. When doing a cash-out refi, each investor receives their pro-rata share based on their capital contribution percentage (e.g., if they invested $25K of $513K total, they get 5% of any refinance proceeds)
4. Operate 60-unit multifamily buildings off-site with a good PM within 10 miles; budget 6% management fee but model conservatively at 8% in analyzers to account for leasing commissions
5. Target a 30-40% return of capital within 36 months from refinance events as a realistic expectation; only attempt full payoff if market conditions align favorably
6. Use ChatGPT with specific prompts to compare markets: request population growth, job growth, and median household income for major cities over three years past and projected three years forward in a ranked table
7. Build broker relationships by consistent follow-up (couple touches per week) to access pre-market deals not listed online; fly in-person to meet brokers, local lenders, and property managers rather than relying solely on digital communication
