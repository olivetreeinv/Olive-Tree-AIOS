## Summary
Justin Brennan mentors multifamily investors on market reconnaissance, deal underwriting, and relationship-building with brokers/property managers. Key focus: physical market visits (3-day trips) are essential for connecting with deal sources and understanding neighborhood dynamics before analyzing properties online. Discusses deal structures including assumable loans, seller financing, and mezzanine debt strategies.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Conduct a mandatory 3-day 'reconnaissance trip' to target market: Sunday arrival, Monday-Tuesday broker/property tours, Wednesday departure
2. Set up meetings with 2-3 brokers, 2 property managers, and lenders during market visit—location (office, coffee, property) matters less than face-to-face connection
3. Target 15-30 unit deals in bank-owned/stressed portfolios using the deal analyzer spreadsheet to plug in financials before submitting offers
4. For older buildings (pre-1970s): avoid if plumbing, electrical, roof, HVAC haven't been updated—high maintenance costs kill deals unless major systems already replaced
5. Deferred maintenance = improvements that don't raise rents; avoid these in underwriting
6. Assumable loans (subject-to): only viable if term is favorable (e.g., 3.78% interest-only for 7 years)—layer seller financing on top if needed to close gap
7. Mezzanine debt (9-15% interest, second position) only works on value-add deals you can refinance within 24 months post-renovation at 65-70% LTV with stabilized rents
