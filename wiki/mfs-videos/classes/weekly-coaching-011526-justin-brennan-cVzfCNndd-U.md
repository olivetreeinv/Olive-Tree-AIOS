## Summary
Justin Brennan coaches Brian Norton's AIOS community on the complete deal acquisition and funding process for multifamily investments, emphasizing market selection, broker relationships, team building, and proof of funds requirements. A detailed case study with Don Casper on a 5-unit value-add property in Orange, CA demonstrates how vacancy status and financing structure materially impact deal underwriting and purchase price.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Select 3 states, narrow to 1 using ChatGPT analysis of population growth, job growth, and median household income
2. Use Crexy/LoopNet to identify broker logos appearing across listings, then use AI prompts to find broker contact details and names
3. Phone brokers with a specific on-market property as a 'lure' to build relationships; 2-5 minute conversations can unlock off-market deal flow
4. Proof of funds strategy: get 70% pre-qualification loan letter from lender (typically 70% of purchase price), show 10% equity from personal capital, demonstrate ability to raise remaining 20% via syndication and CRM soft commitments
5. For vacant properties requiring hard money (12%+ interest + points + 8-10 month carry), back into purchase price from post-renovation exit value using 5.25-5.5% cap rate on pro-forma NOI
6. Hard money costs add $140K-$200K to deal carry on $1.4M loan over 8-10 months; renovation + financing costs must be underwritten before committing to price
7. For occupied properties, convert tenants to month-to-month leases (vs. full vacancy) to enable DCR financing at cheaper rates and preserve revenue during renovation phase
