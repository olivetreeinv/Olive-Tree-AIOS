## Key Takeaways
1. Make three broker appointments in your target market as your first reconnaissance mission, even if you fall flat on your face
2. Count bars on windows while driving the streets—high bar count indicates poor neighborhood quality and is a reliable risk indicator
3. For neighborhood due diligence, speak directly to local business owners (mom-and-pop restaurants) and neighbors to understand area dynamics and safety
4. Use ChatGPT to pull market data in 15 minutes instead of a full day: ask for population growth, job growth, median household income, rent growth trends, vacancy rates, and new construction supply vs. demand
5. Check 10-year flood history by asking neighbors and researching if ravines/flood channels have actually overflowed—if no overflow in 10 years, acceptable risk with flood insurance
6. On the Deal Analyzer 0-50 unit model, if double-dipping as GP, zero out GP contribution at top and include 100% of capital in LP section so numbers accurately reflect what LPs actually receive
7. Healthy LP annual returns are 12-15%, with anything north of 14% being good; equity multiplier of 1.8x or more is decent (though these are projections and can be outperformed)
