## Summary
Justin Brennan walks through the complete A-to-Z process of finding, analyzing, underwriting, and closing multifamily deals, with emphasis on the find-analyze-LOI cycle that must repeat 20-30 times before landing a deal. He details his own first 27-unit deal journey in Kansas City (2019) and explains the financing structure, capital raising mechanics via syndication, and how to build investor soft commitments through pitch decks and CRM management.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Repeat the find-analyze-LOI cycle relentlessly; you may need 20-30 properties before getting one accepted deal
2. Write as many LOIs as possible (even 400/day) since they are non-binding; an LOI is 'literally worth the paper it's written on'
3. Target deals at $50,000-$150,000 per unit in markets like GA/TN/AL/OK to minimize out-of-pocket capital (goal: under $50k personal investment)
4. Use Craigslist/Cruxy only to identify brokers in your target market, not to find actual deals
5. Good deals attract both debt and equity automatically; 14-20% annual return, 6-10% cash-on-cash, 1.8x+ equity multiple are the thresholds
6. Raise equity by creating FOMO: email prospects asking 'who do you know' rather than 'are you interested,' triggering self-selection into the deal
7. Set LOIs with 60-day escrow: 30-day due diligence period (refundable), 30-day closing, plus optional 30-day extension (90 days total max)
8. Minimum investor check is $25,000; typical first deal raises $600k-$900k from 5-8 investors ($25k, $50k, $150k, $200k check sizes)
9. Never allow money to go hard on day one to get contract; maintain 30-day refundable due diligence window for inspections and financials
10. Use income approach (NOI-based) not comparable approach to value commercial properties; two identical buildings with different NOIs have different values
