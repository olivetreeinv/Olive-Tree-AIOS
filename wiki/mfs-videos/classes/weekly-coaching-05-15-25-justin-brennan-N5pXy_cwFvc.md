## Summary
Justin Brennan conducts a live walkthrough of how to analyze a multifamily deal using a real Dallas property example from Crexy. He demonstrates using the broker's offering memorandum, extracting data into a deal analyzer, adjusting conservative assumptions, backing into maximum offer price, writing an LOI, and preparing a pitch deck for investors.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use deal analyzer to input unit mix, rents, and expenses directly from broker OM (offering memorandum)
2. Separate contract services (pool, landscaping, pest control, cleaning) from repairs/maintenance line items—brokers combine them to hide true costs
3. Remove management fees from general administrative expenses and input as separate 6% line item
4. Add reserve/replacement line of $250-$300 per unit per year for capital expenditures—brokers omit this entirely
5. Set minimum 10% economic loss/vacancy assumption, never believe broker's 5%—"there's no way you're operating less than 10% in Texas"
6. Target expense ratio of 45-55% for buildings built 1950-1980; 35-45% for 1980-2000; 30s or lower for post-2000 buildings
7. Debt coverage ratio (DCR) must be 1.25 or greater for lender approval—this is the critical constraint
8. Back into max offer price using required metrics: 14-20% IRR, 6-10% cash-on-cash, 1.8x equity multiple minimum
9. Use Chat GPT to verify property taxes by county and market value—can save 30-40 minutes of research
10. Write LOI at 1% earnest money deposit (non-binding, refundable until due diligence), shoot out multiple offers—"you could spit these out 400 a day"
11. Leverage broker relationships by offering professional deal analysis and saying 'show me where I'm wrong'—positions you for pre-market deal flow
