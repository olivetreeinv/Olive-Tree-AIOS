The session addresses practical underwriting questions and entity formation strategy relevant to value-add multifamily acquisitions.

## Key Takeaways
1. Segregate deferred maintenance from capital improvements during inspections—deferred maintenance is negotiable for price reduction, improvements generate additional rent
2. For unit-by-unit inspections on 12-15 unit properties: allocate 1-2 inspectors for 1 day, plus separate professional plumber with camera for sewer scope
3. Big ticket items requiring negotiation: mold, health/safety issues, foundations, structure, roofing, water heaters—don't nitpick minor appliances on older buildings (1970s+)
4. Closing costs should total ~6% of purchase price and cover acquisition fee (2-3%), title, escrow, and 1% lender fee
5. For non-syndicated deals, create state-specific LLC (assignable contract required) in last 10 days of due diligence once financials and inspections validate deal viability
6. DSCR loans attribute to entity EIN only, not personal credit—lenders pull personal credit as fraud check, not for qualification; balance sheet equal to loan amount required if entity looks weak
7. Pitch deck investor example calculations: for $100,000 investor, divide their investment by total equity raised to get percentage (e.g., $100K ÷ $1M total = 10%), then apply cash-on-cash and total return percentages to that investor's specific amount
8. Minimum investment threshold for 506(b) syndication offerings: $25,000 (accredited/non-accredited mix)—initial raise typically $25K, $50K, $75K, $100K tranches rather than single $100K minimum
