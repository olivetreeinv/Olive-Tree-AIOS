## Summary
Justin Brennan mentors multifamily investors on capital raising strategy, emphasizing pitch deck creation as the foundation for investor outreach. Luis presents his 12-unit San Antonio deal (under contract at $954K, 20% IRR) and learns critical steps: building a pitch deck with 17-20% IRR targets, compiling a 25-30 person investor list, creating a simple company website, and recording a walkthrough video to answer 95% of investor questions before direct conversation.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Target 17-20% IRR minimum to justify pulling capital from liquid assets like 401Ks and stocks
2. Build pitch deck with executive summary, capitalization stack, projected returns per investment amount ($25K-$100K increments), unit mix, property location, market metrics, and financial projections
3. Create simple company website (template-based, one home page + about section minimum) listing company name, address, partnership faces
4. Compile list of 25-30 potential investors including friends, family, and high-net-worth individuals; 'don't worry if they don't have money, people know people'
5. Record 30-minute Loom video walking through pitch deck on landing page to answer: Where does money go? What are returns? When do I get paid back? What safety measures protect the asset?
6. Use Canva template for pitch decks (more efficient than PowerPoint) and eventually delegate to VA at $3.99/hour after first 1-2 decks built personally
7. Send seller questionnaire and representations/warranties addendum to contract 7-10 days before execution—brokers will pressure to sign immediately, resist this timeline
