## Summary
Justin Brennan mentors Bruce through a complex seller-financed 12-unit deal structure, demonstrating how to use preferred equity, capital stacking, and creative financing to make an underperforming property work. The discussion covers deal underwriting, refinance strategy, and investor return calculations for a small multifamily property requiring significant rent increases through renovations.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use 35-40% down payment on value-add deals to achieve minimum 1.25 debt coverage ratio and ensure bankability for future refinance
2. Banks will only underwrite to current rents unless you prove rent increase through lease renewal alone (no renovations); renovation deals get underwritten on today's financials
3. Structure seller financing as 'preferred equity' at 7% simple interest (accruing, not paid monthly) to reduce investor capital requirements while keeping seller in deal
4. Create three separate checking accounts under one bank account: operations, renovation/capex, and entity distributions to avoid co-mingling funds and track cash flow
5. Projected investor returns should target: 13-18% annual returns, 1.9-2.0x equity multiple, 8-9% average cash-on-cash return, 3-5 year hold timeline
6. Never speculate or force deals; prioritize 'safety first' by de-leveraging with higher down payments so you can hold property through market downturns without forced sale
7. In 24-month cash-out refi: if you increase NOI from $56k to $90k and refinance at 70% LTV on $1.5M value, you can return investor capital while keeping property and future cash flow
