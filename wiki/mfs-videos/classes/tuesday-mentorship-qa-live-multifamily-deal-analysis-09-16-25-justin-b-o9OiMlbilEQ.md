## Summary
Justin Brennan conducts a live deal analysis of a 42-unit multifamily property in Logansport, Indiana with mentee Michael, focusing on market cap rates, underwriting assumptions, and tertiary market considerations. The discussion covers realistic exit cap rates (7-7.5% vs. seller's 7.75%), expense ratios, debt service coverage ratios, and due diligence priorities for small-market deals.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Run tertiary market deals at 6.5% minimum exit cap rate for safety, even if market data shows 7-8%
2. Stress test assumptions: use realistic 5% credit loss rather than seller's optimistic numbers
3. Verify historical cap rates using ChatGPT data aggregation across multiple real estate websites
4. Check for pipeline of new construction units in development department over next 2 years to assess downward pressure
5. Tertiary market lending rates typically run high fives to middle-low sixes (currently improving toward sub-5%)
6. For small towns, prioritize job market diversity: need 6+ strong employers, not single-employer dependency like 'one Walmart'
7. Due diligence must include historical occupancy verification and confirmation market hasn't changed since seller's T12 projections
