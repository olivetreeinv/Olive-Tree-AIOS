## Key Takeaways
1. Use the five-pillar method: Find deals → Analyze deals → Write offers → Finance (debt + equity) → Close escrow and manage. Focus on 10-30 unit deals first before scaling larger.
2. Never overly leverage properties; overleveraging is the only way to lose in real estate. Buy conservatively and keep cash reserves; market conditions will test your deals.
3. The 3-day reconnaissance mission: Fly in Sunday afternoon, tour 3 properties Monday morning, meet 2-3 property managers and lenders Monday-Tuesday, fly out Tuesday afternoon. This establishes your core team before deal flow.
4. Deal structure: Form Wyoming holding company (LLC, $125, 15 minutes); each property gets its own LLC in the property's state. Holding company owns each property LLC as sole member. This isolates liability and protects assets.
5. On your first 10-30 unit deal, plan for 8-10% total management costs (blended fee + commissions + leasing). Use 10% vacancy/economic loss in proformas to account for physical vacancy, loss-to-lease, concessions, and bad debt.
6. For raising capital: Soft-commit 25-50 investors before deal; when you have LOI, pre-market with pitch deck; get earnest money in escrow (fully refundable during due diligence); close in 60-90 days. Distributions to LPs are 60-70% of cash profits for 90% of capital with 6-8% preferred return.
7. Ask brokers 22 pointed pre-contract questions and get written answers before going into contract; these answers become an addendum and protect you if seller lied during due diligence.
