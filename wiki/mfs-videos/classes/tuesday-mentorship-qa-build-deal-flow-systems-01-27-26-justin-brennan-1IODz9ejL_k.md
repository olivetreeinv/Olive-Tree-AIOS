## Summary
Justin Brennan conducts a live mentorship session analyzing two multifamily deals: a 9-unit property in North Carolina that needs price reduction to pencil (backing in from $1.7M to ~$1.35-1.4M), and a distressed 50-unit in Huntsville, Texas at 52% occupancy requiring bridge financing strategy. The session covers underwriting mechanics, valuation backing-in, and stressed asset acquisition approaches using bridge debt with interest reserves.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Back into deals when metrics don't work: if asking $1.7M but deal only supports $1.35-1.4M, make aggressive LOI with follow-up phone call to broker saying 'I want to talk about the numbers' rather than radio silence
2. Target 15%+ IRR on value-add deals; in North Carolina 9-unit example, IRR was too rough and deal needed $300K-350K price reduction to work
3. For 2002-built property, use 30-40% expense ratio guidance; newer properties should be 30-35%, older properties closer to 40%
4. On distressed properties (50%+ vacancy), use bridge debt at ~9% interest rate for 2-3 years with interest reserve built into loan to carry until stabilization, then refinance
5. Maintain 2% gap between cap rate you build property to and market cap rate at exit (e.g., build to 8-9% cap, sell at 7% market cap)
6. Closing costs on smaller deals (9-30 units) should be 6%; drops to 4% on 80+ unit properties
7. Don't put money hard on deposits in current market (post-2022); keep all deposit money refundable through due diligence period
8. If missing expense data from wholesaler, use property age to estimate expense ratio range, then make aggressive offer and tell broker 'I need actual data to increase offer'
