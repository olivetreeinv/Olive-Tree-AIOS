## Summary
Justin Brennan mentors Rory Lee on pitching a 28-unit townhome syndication deal (Millwood Townhomes in Kansas City) valued at $3.3M with a $1.025M equity raise. The session covers deal structure, underwriting validation, renovation strategy, and capital raising mechanics for a value-add multifamily property.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use LVT (luxury vinyl tile) glue-down flooring instead of LVP (luxury vinyl plank click-together) for easier unit-by-unit repairs without disrupting entire floors
2. Do not provide washers/dryers directly; instead use third-party appliance rental companies (like Appliance Warehouse) that handle maintenance to avoid burdening maintenance staff
3. Avoid demolishing old tile when installing new flooring; overlay with thin cement subfloor layer then glue down LVT to save time and cost
4. Keep operating expense ratios conservative at 35-40% for 1980s-era properties; validate assumptions in deal analyzer against property age guidelines
5. Design simple cosmetic renovations (paint, signage, lighting, stainless appliances, countertop refinishing) to drive rent growth without major capital expenditure
6. Exit cap rate projections should be equal to or higher than entry cap rate; conservatively underwrite at 6.6% exit when entering at 6.1% cap rate
7. Building local relationships with lenders, contractors, and brokers before deal sourcing saves ~1% loan origination fee versus using brokers to place loans
