## Key Takeaways
1. Write aggressive LOIs even if significantly below asking; follow with professional text to broker: 'Hey [broker], I just sent you an LOI on [property]. It's aggressive, but I'm going off the numbers I have. I'd love to chat more about it' to build rapport and access pre-market deals
2. 80% of deals are in pre-market phase before websites; use online listings (Krexy, Loopnet) as lure to build broker relationships, not primary sourcing tools
3. Target 65-70% LTV in underwriting despite lenders approving higher, because DSCR requirements (typically 1.2-1.35x) naturally back into that range given current market conditions
4. Local/regional banks and credit unions are sweet spot for 10-50 unit deals; offer 5.5-7.25% rates, 5-10 year terms, more flexibility than CMBS ($2M minimum, rigid, expensive attorneys)
5. Bridge loans for distressed assets: 9-12% rate, 12-18 month term, 2 points (1% in/1% out), minimum 6-month interest requirement; use when you can buy 30% below value
6. Workforce housing (B/C class) outperforming Class A in 2026; national rents up 25% since 2020 but affordability at multi-decade lows driving demand to secondary markets
7. New sponsors: start with duplexes/triplexes or partner with experienced operator taking minority equity; alternatively structure as co-GP with experienced partner who gets larger split to earn experience
