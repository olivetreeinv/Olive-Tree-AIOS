## Summary
Justin Brennan mentors multifamily investors on raising capital efficiently by building infrastructure before securing deals. He emphasizes creating mock pitch decks, landing pages, and warming up capital sources through a structured process—including lender soft quotes and investor conversations—so that when a deal closes, financing is already partially committed rather than chased reactively.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Create pitch decks and landing pages during mock deal phase to signal professionalism and warm investor database before actual deals
2. Set up 3 bank accounts for LLC: operating (day-to-day), entity (investor money), and capex (renovations) to track expenses cleanly
3. Execute pitch deck, landing page, and summary video within 3-4 days of signed LOI, before formal contract, to begin capital raising immediately
4. Investors primarily care about 3 things: How much money needed, what returns (equity multiple, preferred return, cash-on-cash), when do they get money back
5. For capital raisers without GP status in regulated deals, allocate percentage of GP based on capital raised target (e.g., raise 30% of total = 30% of GP pool)
6. Use Real AI (realai.com) connected to real-time data sources for market analysis, rent comps, and business plan generation
7. Submit PPM legal documents in advance (~$13K upfront) so closing is faster; deals reimburse closing costs including legal prep
8. Red coloring on pitch deck slides psychologically signals negativity—use green or black instead
