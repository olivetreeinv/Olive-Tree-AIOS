## Summary
Justin Brennan conducts a detailed walkthrough of a simple deal analyzer tool for multifamily properties, demonstrating how to input property data, calculate key metrics (debt service coverage ratio, cash-on-cash return, IRR, equity multiple), and work backwards from target returns to determine fair offer prices. The session includes practical examples of underwriting a deal to make sense, broker outreach strategy, and market reconnaissance methodology.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) must exceed 1.25x to be bankable
2. Target cash-on-cash return of 6-10% or more; IRR of 14-20%+ per year; equity multiple of 2.0x or higher (minimum 1.8x acceptable)
3. Down payment assumption: start with 30% as commercial standard (range 30-35%)
4. Repair budget: 7,500-8,500 per unit for turnkey/nice properties; 10,000-14,000 per unit for heavy renovations
5. Closing costs baseline: 6% (includes legal, title, escrow, acquisition fees, financing fees)
6. Interest rate baseline: 6.5% current assumption for modeling
7. Maintenance and repairs expense: ~700 per unit per year; turnover costs: ~250 per unit per year; property tax reserve replacement: 250-300 per unit per year
8. Management fee: 6-8% all-inclusive; vacancy/credit loss assumption: 10% as starting point
9. Use Crexy to identify broker names/logos (market makers), not to find deals directly; 4-week broker relationship building precedes property tours
10. Reconnaissance mission: fly in Sunday, tour properties Monday with 3 separate brokers belly-to-belly for rapport; tour 3 properties minimum
11. Property tour timing: Sunday shows all residents (good/bad/ugly); weekday evening commute also reveals neighborhood truth
12. Letter of Intent template available in portal; verbal offers acceptable before written LOI submission
13. When offer rejected, use as leverage to get on pre-market email list: 'I'm a buyer in this market, I'd love to buy through you'
14. Chat GPT usage for market research: ask for population growth (want >1%), job growth (want >1-2%), median household income (want >$60k+) for city comparisons over last 3 years
15. When deal doesn't work: either increase revenue (rents), decrease expenses, or reduce purchase price; work backwards from target returns to acceptable offer price
