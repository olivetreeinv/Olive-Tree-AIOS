## Summary
Justin Brennan conducts a comprehensive multifamily deal analysis coaching session, walking through three specific deals (16-unit Cincinnati townhomes, 22-unit Cincinnati property, and 39-unit Memphis bank-owned deal) to teach Brian Norton's student group proper underwriting methodology, expense ratio targets, and renovation cost ROI calculations. The session emphasizes identifying red flags in proformas and the importance of market-specific underwriting assumptions.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Return on renovation cost must be 18% or greater—if below 18%, either increase rents achievable or decrease renovation budget
2. Target expense ratio for stabilized multifamily is 49-50% even for older 1960s properties; anything 60%+ indicates operational problems
3. For Midwest renovations, budget $10,000-$14,000 per unit; validate property tax numbers through county auditor websites and RAI tool rather than accepting seller proforma figures
4. Insurance varies by state: Ohio ~$600-$700/unit annually, Texas ~$1,200-$1,400/unit, Florida ~$3,000/unit—get soft quotes from state-specific brokers
5. Utilities with RUBs/reimbursables should generate ~60% recovery; if utilities show $37,000 expense, expect to recover ~$22,000 through reimbursements
6. Refinance deals year 3-4 only if interest rate environment allows you to pull enough proceeds and improve cash flows; forced appreciation alone doesn't work if interest rates rise (cap rates follow interest rates)
7. On broker calls, move conversation from their listing to pre-market deals by asking about buy box, value-add tolerance, and opportunities 'in the initial stages' before Crexy posting
