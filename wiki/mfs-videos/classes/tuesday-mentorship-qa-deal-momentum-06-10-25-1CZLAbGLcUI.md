## Summary
Justin Brennan conducts a mentorship Q&A covering deal sourcing challenges, underwriting techniques, and capital raising strategies. Key discussions include analyzing off-market deals, negotiating broker fees, understanding competitor capital structures, and setting realistic price-per-unit targets for first-time operators. A live example features a 33-unit Missouri deal needing $80-100K in final capital to close.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Submit 15-20 LOIs before expecting one to land; don't get attached to any single deal
2. Earnest money should be 1-2% due within 72 hours of executed contract, not 5% over 20 days
3. Expense ratios: 1970-1990s properties target 40-50%, post-1990 can achieve 30s; verify brokers aren't hiding expenses in capex
4. Interest rate should not exceed current cap rate; for example, 6.5% cap rate cannot support 7.35% debt without significant down payment
5. Price-per-unit sweet spot is $50,000-$150,000 to keep capital raises bitesizable ($300K-$700K range)
6. Call brokers on lost deals asking: 'What's their capital structure, down payment %, debt rate, and terms?' to understand competitive positioning
7. For co-GP partnerships, allocate percentages by responsibility: deal finder 10%, due diligence 10%, boots-on-ground 10%, asset manager 15%, loan signer 20%, earnest money/risk capital 10%, capital raiser 30%
