## Summary
Justin Brennan conducts live multifamily deal analysis on a 27-unit Phoenix property, demonstrating how to use the updated deal analyzer to work backwards from asking price to determine investability. He shows why the property doesn't pencil for syndication at current market prices and explains it's only suitable for a 1031 exchange buyer, then calls two brokers to source additional deals and build relationships.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Ask brokers for 'whisper price' or 'price guidance' on unpriced listings to get realistic seller expectations
2. Use the 1% rule: monthly gross rent should be at least 1% of purchase price (e.g., $24k monthly rent on $1.6M purchase = 1.5%)
3. Target minimum 18% return on cost (ROC) for renovation projects to justify the capital deployment
4. Require 40-50% down payment on stabilized deals to achieve acceptable debt coverage ratio (target 1.3x DSCR) and investor returns
5. The three critical metrics that must be 'green' are: cash-on-cash return, equity multiple, and IRR (other metrics are nice but not defining)
6. When a deal doesn't work financially, don't lower price below lender's loan amount—use non-workable deals to build broker relationships and access pre-market deal flow
7. Call brokers 100 miles away from your target market first to practice broker calls before calling in your actual operating territory
