## Summary
Justin Brennan mentors multifamily investors through detailed analysis of three deals: a 10-unit Vegas apartment requiring higher down payment to improve DSCR, a 20-unit Dallas property with high expense ratios that can be repositioned through operational fixes, and discussion of a Georgia off-market bank deal. The session emphasizes underwriting discipline, proper capital structure, and presenting credible buyer profiles to brokers.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. DSCR issues on stabilized deals require 40-45% down payment instead of 30% to achieve 1.2x minimum threshold
2. Raise rents conservatively: Vegas 1BR from $1,200 to $1,300, 2BR to $1,500 based on comparable market data, not speculation
3. Management fees should be 8% of gross rents, general admin ~$12,000/year, not the 15-20% operators often budget
4. Bridge debt strategy only makes sense if property generates significant value-add; interest-only at higher rates requires substantial rent/operational improvement to justify
5. For stabilized deals, underwrite at 6-6.5% fixed-rate interest; don't accept variable-rate loans on stable cash-flowing assets with recourse provisions
6. Preferred return of 6% with 85/15 GP/LP split works better than 8% preferred when cash-on-cash returns are compressed by higher rates
7. Earnest money/escrow deposits: hold firm at 25% rather than accepting broker requests to increase to 50% unless deal fundamentals warrant it
