## Summary
Justin Brennan leads a mentorship session on setting up professional deal landing pages and pitch decks to attract investors. He demonstrates the technical backend of creating customized single-property landing pages within the CRM system, including embedding pitch decks and summary videos. The discussion covers capital raising strategy through investor relationship building rather than direct solicitation.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Create a mock deal with landing page, pitch deck, and 5-10 minute summary video using Loom before pursuing real deals to practice the process
2. Use a single landing page (not full website) specific to each deal with unique URL saved on phone for quick sharing at networking events
3. Include embedded pitch deck, summary video, and property details on landing page so investors can self-educate before initial conversation
4. Never ask investors for money directly; instead invite them to opportunities and ask if they know anyone interested to shift psychology from sales to invitation
5. Send deal link immediately when interest is expressed ('money loves speed') rather than waiting until tomorrow
6. Use seller financing creatively (20% down, interest-only for 5 years at 5.5% fixed) as alternative to bank loans to improve deal economics
7. Keep renovation budget separate from loan financing when under $400k; only finance reno through debt when deal exceeds $400k threshold
