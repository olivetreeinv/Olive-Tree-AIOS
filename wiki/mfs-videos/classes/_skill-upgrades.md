## Broker outreach — use their listing as the opener

When contacting any qualifying broker, lead with THEIR active listing, not a cold pitch.
The listing is the bait — it removes the "what do I say" friction and earns the real ask.

Script flow:
1. Reference the specific listing ("your 28-unit on ___").

   "We're looking for 10–50 unit, B/C class, light-to-medium value-add,
    mid-teens IRR on property-level returns."
5. Capture the off-market ask: "What's the best email to keep me on your
    pre-market list?" → set Pre-market list = follow-up flagged.

Why the metrics matter: a precise buy box reads as "this person knows what
they're talking about" → trust → broker volunteers off-market deals.

### Step: Re-underwrite broker NOI (assume it's inflated)

Broker NOI is built to maximize price. Don't argue revenue or reno cost —
attack the expense + loss lines, which is where they hide value:

  • Vacancy / credit loss — brokers squeeze toward 0%. Reset to market/submarket
    actuals; never underwrite below your buy-box floor.

    flag historic/special-status properties separately (tax exposure risk).
  • Economic loss / reserves — add back if missing.

Output the delta: "Broker NOI $X → adjusted NOI $Y (−$Z). Re-priced value: $___."

## 3. deal-analysis — Replacement-Cost vs. Acquisition Price Check
**Why it matters:** Buying well below rebuild cost is a structural margin-of-safety signal that pure cash-flow metrics miss — fast way to flag exceptional deals and defensibility.
**Candidate #4.**

**Paste-ready — add as a Scorecard line + Callout:**
```markdown
### Scorecard add: Price-to-Replacement-Cost

Estimate replacement cost (rebuild $/unit or $/SF for the market) and compare:

  Ratio = Acquisition Price ÷ Replacement Cost

  • < 0.50  → A  (exceptional — "couldn't rebuild for 2x"; flag in Callouts)
  • 0.50–0.75 → B
  • 0.75–1.0  → C
  • > 1.0   → D  (paying above rebuild cost — justify or pass)

Callout example: "Buying at $1.7M vs ~$5M replacement (0.34x) — structural value,
hard to compete with on new supply."
```
