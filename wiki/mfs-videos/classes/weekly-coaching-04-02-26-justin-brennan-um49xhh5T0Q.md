## Key Takeaways
1. Use unit-type methodology for larger deals: group similar units (e.g., '5 two-bed/one-bath units at $1,850 avg rent') rather than listing each unit individually to streamline analysis
2. For small 5-10 unit self-managed properties, operating expense ratio can run in the low 30% range (self-management saves on PM fees)
3. Vacancy assumption: assume at least 10% vacancy or one month of rent loss per year; on a 5-unit with $2K/month rent, budget ~$2,000 annual turnover/lease-up costs
4. Target refinance in year 3-4 at 70% LTV with 3 years interest-only to maximize cash-on-cash returns and pull proceeds for investor buyouts
5. IRR takeout threshold: set preferred return between 16-18% annualized; once cumulative returns + principal repayment hit this level, investors exit and sponsor owns property outright
6. Acquisition fee: ~2% of purchase price; asset management fee: 1-2% annually; refinance fee: 0.5-1% of new loan amount; disposition fee: 0.5% of sale price
7. 'Buy 3, sell 1' portfolio strategy: continuously acquire deals while periodically liquidating to recycle capital; use tax-free refinance proceeds to fund new acquisitions without triggering capital gains
