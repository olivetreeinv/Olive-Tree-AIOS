## Key Takeaways
1. Don't place email-based concession requests; move to phone call with broker to understand seller's true position and constraints
2. Understand the seller's debt situation first—variable vs. fixed rate, loan amount, and interest payments drive their desperation level
3. 10-year Treasury below 4% is critical threshold; if it reaches 3.5-4%, commercial mortgage rates can drop to low 5%, compressing cap rates and enabling deals to transact
4. For deal underwriting: use conservative 6% exit cap rate, assume 2% annual rent growth and 2% annual operating expense increases
5. Run sensitivity analysis on deal analyzer—shift cap rate from 6% to 5.5% and watch IRR jump from 20% to 21%+ to show investors upside potential without overleveraging
6. Structure debt as max 5-year fixed term with interest-only period to avoid prepayment penalties if market conditions improve and you want to exit/refinance early
7. If seller is stressed by variable-rate debt and can't service it, they have limited runway—emphasize time pressure: 'It's not going to get better for her'
8. 30-day inspection due diligence period acts as financing contingency in commercial deals; you don't need explicit financing contingency language to preserve exit rights
