## Summary
Justin Brennan leads a mentorship Q&A focused on troubleshooting the updated deal analyzer spreadsheet tool. The session covers closing costs allocation, equity raise calculations, sources and uses mechanics, and applies the analyzer to a real 32-unit multifamily deal in Kansas City, demonstrating how to identify which deal fundamentals (price, revenue, expenses) need adjustment to achieve target returns.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. When raising capital, overestimate the equity needed—if numbers show $500k needed, shoot for $600k instead to avoid under-capitalization
2. Closing costs are typically split 75% equity / 25% loan coverage; adjust loan-to-value assumptions accordingly
3. Total equity to raise includes purchase price portion + renovation dollars + uncovered closing costs, shown in 'sources and uses' section
4. For interest reserves when property won't cash flow during renovation, calculate annual debt service (e.g., $16,200/month × 12 months = ~$195k) and add as line item to total project cost
5. Deal fundamentals analysis: once revenue, expenses, and interest rates are locked in, only three levers remain—property price, rental rates, or down payment percentage
6. Cap rate arbitrage matters: if buying at 6.83 cap and selling at 6 cap, need minimum 2-point spread differential to create value
7. Bridge loans justified when DCR doesn't pencil on stabilized basis; allows rent growth before permanent financing
8. For refinance timing: year 3-4 refi may work only if significant value-add justifies new valuation; stable 2020 product better held 5-6 years without refi
