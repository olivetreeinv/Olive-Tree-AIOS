## Summary
Justin Brennan walks through Brian Norton's actual 48-unit portfolio deal (four properties in Atlanta) with live deal analyzer training, focusing on how to input broker data (OM, T12, rent roll) and calculate key underwriting metrics. The discussion covers unit economics, renovation value-adds, proforma rent validation through market comps, and threshold metrics for deal evaluation.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use three key metrics to evaluate deals: Cash-on-Cash 6-10%+, IRR 14-20%+, Equity Multiple 1.8x+; if all three align, other metrics will follow
2. Input only blue cells in deal analyzer; white cells auto-calculate—do not modify until proficient
3. Proforma = future rent achievable within 6-12 months post-close; current = actual last 12 months; validate proforma by touring 3 comparable properties in market
4. Expense ratio typically 45-48% for value-add multifamily; management fee headline 5-6% but effective 8% when including leasing/lease-up costs at 2%
5. For sub-$1M loan amounts, expect ~7% interest rate from local commercial banks; portfolioizing 2+ properties can lower to ~6.25%
6. Unit mix analysis: use market research via ChatGPT or AI by property address to determine optimal bedroom configuration rather than assuming 2-bed superiority
7. Deal analyzer for 0-50 units is starter tool; advanced analyzer ($300-5K build cost) used only after first two deals closed
