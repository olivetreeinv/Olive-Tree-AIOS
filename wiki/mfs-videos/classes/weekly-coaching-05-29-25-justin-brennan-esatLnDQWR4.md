## Summary
Justin reviews Mike's 28-unit multifamily deal in North Charleston, SC priced at $4.2M ($150K/unit). The deal analysis focuses on underwriting accuracy, expense ratios, financing strategy, and investor pitch mechanics rather than Brian's specific GA/TN/AL sourcing and underwriting challenges.

## Key Takeaways
1. Use ChatGPT to calculate property taxes in 45 seconds vs. 45 minutes (ask county name, property type, address, assessment ratio, mill rate)
2. Run vacancy/economic loss at minimum 10% on proforma, not broker's inflated 5-8% numbers
3. Keep expense ratios realistic at 46-47% for 1970s vintage properties, not brokers' optimistic 41-42%
4. Negotiate interest rate down to 6% or better with lenders—this moves the deal price more than tweaking occupancy
5. Target 15% annual investor return + 7%+ cash-on-cash preferred return + $1 tax write-off per $1 invested to attract capital
6. Deal portals (funnel pages) showcase property with pitch deck, summary video, property highlights, and investor contact form
7. For capital raise: create pitch deck, host webinar Q&A (records to 20-min summary video), email both to prospect list with landing page link
