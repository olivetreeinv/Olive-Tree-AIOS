## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches how to build a capital foundation before closing deals by establishing business identity, creating a CRM database of 30 contacts, and executing a mock deal (pitch deck, landing page, Loom video, email campaign) to warm up investors and shift how people perceive you as a multifamily investor rather than just your primary profession.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Change email signature from 'Realtor Justin' to 'Realtor and Multifamily Investor' with multifamily website link to shift market perception
2. Build CRM with minimum 30 people you know (not necessarily wealthy) and use bulk CSV import with name/email/phone
3. Create mock deal marketing package: pitch deck + 5-10 minute Loom video summary + dedicated landing page + email to database asking 'who do you know' not asking for money
4. Mock deal email template: subject 'quick look [unit count] unit deal,' tone 'I'm closing on it with or without you,' include returns/highlights/video link/pitch deck, ask referrals not investments
5. Send mock deal email asking 'Can you send this to anyone you may know?' rather than 'Do you have $50k to invest?'
6. Pull mock deal after 3 weeks with message: 'Found issues during escrow, pulling away, thanks for interest, will send next deal'
7. Follow SEC guidance: avoid posting specific deal terms on social media; acceptable to send deal details via email to known contacts or warm introductions (506b compliant)
