## Summary
Justin Brennan demonstrates the deal landing page backend system for creating mock deals during the capital foundation stage (Stage 6). He shows how to edit deal funnels, set URLs, and create professional landing pages with pitch decks and summary videos. The coaching also covers broker calling strategy, market selection (focusing on OKC), deal analysis fundamentals, and using return-on-cost metrics to evaluate value-add feasibility. While the content includes technical backend setup, the deal-analysis components directly support Brian's underwriting and deal-sourcing challenges.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Return on cost should be 18% or greater to justify value-add spending; use the new analyzer metric to determine if repairs make financial sense
2. Pre-market deals (80% of market) come through broker databases of 25-50 investors before hitting CoStar/LoopNet; focus on building broker relationships to access pre-market flow, not website listings
3. Market trips are critical to move broker relationships from phone to face-to-face; visit brokers, property managers, lenders, and construction crews in person to signal seriousness and access deal flow
4. Landing page URL structure: domain.com/dealname (e.g., rococapitalpartners.com/1234EStreet); must edit both desktop AND mobile versions separately or changes won't display on phone
5. For first deals, use local commercial banks and credit unions (via HB Capital or MVP Capital) rather than Fannie Mae/Freddy Mac, which require track record
6. Call brokers using listed properties as 'lures' even if deal doesn't work financially; goal is pre-market relationship access, not the specific deal
7. Email signature must include website URL and multifamily investor title so brokers can click through, verify credibility, read your story, and see your advisory board/team
