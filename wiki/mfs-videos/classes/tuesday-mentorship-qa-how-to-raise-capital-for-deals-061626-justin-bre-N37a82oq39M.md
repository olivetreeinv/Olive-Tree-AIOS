## Summary
Justin Brennan mentors multifamily investors on raising capital for deals, covering the deal pipeline from LOI writing through closing, capital raising strategies, and deal underwriting fundamentals. The session emphasizes building infrastructure (websites, CRM, landing pages) to signal professionalism to investors and lenders, and demonstrates how rent increases directly impact property value and NOI.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Write 10 to 20 LOIs to lock in a good deal because it takes that volume to find a motivated seller willing to sell at financial fundamentals
2. LOIs are not legally binding and should be written aggressively as a lure to start broker conversations, not as a final offer
3. Bump rents $50-100/month per unit and you add $180,000-260,000 in property value on a 25-unit deal with a 6% cap rate
4. Use 5-year or 7-year fixed-rate, low-leverage debt (65-70% LTV) to maximize appreciation as rents grow at 3% annually
5. If you're halfway through capital raise by end of due diligence with commitments secured, you proceed with the deal—timing is flexible with loan extensions
6. Professional website and landing page signal credibility to brokers and investors, directly influencing deal acceptance (Doug's example)
7. Prepayment penalties step down (3% year 1, 2% year 2, 1% year 3 on 5-year loans), allowing refinance without penalty after year 3 to capture rate drops
