## Summary
Justin walks through analyzing a 47-unit Kansas City multifamily deal using his deal analyzer, correcting underwriting assumptions (vacancy rates, expense ratios, cap rates) and demonstrating how to adjust pricing to hit target IRR metrics. He also explains cost segregation strategy as a capital-raising tool to attract high-net-worth investors seeking tax benefits.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use Crexy/Loopnet to find brokers (representing ~30% of deals), not to find deals directly
2. Target minimum 6-10% cash-on-cash and 15%+ IRR on a 3-5 year hold
3. Expense ratios on 1926-era buildings should be 48-50% even with renovations
4. Typical vacancy/credit loss assumption should be 10-15% for Kansas City market
5. Cost segregation on $2M property generates ~$800K in year-one bonus depreciation, creating 1.3X paper loss multiplier for investors
6. Real estate professional status (15 hrs/week) allows unlimited writeoff of ordinary income + capital gains vs. capital gains only
7. Hold properties 5+ years to minimize depreciation recapture tax hit on sale; refinancing avoids recapture entirely
8. Cost segregation cost ($5-7K on 25-unit) is paid by the deal, not out-of-pocket
