## Summary
Justin analyzes a 32-unit Cincinnati value-add deal for Chetna, demonstrating how to underwrite multifamily properties by identifying key expense levers and challenging broker assumptions. He also covers property manager evaluation criteria for Brian's 39-unit Memphis bank-owned deal, and discusses tax strategies like entity structuring to avoid reassessments in Ohio.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Pull four critical levers on any property: leasing/leads, work order handling (quick turnarounds for tenant satisfaction), rent collections, and lease renewals 90 days before expiration—not 60 days
2. Property manager hiring guide should focus on: marketing processes, software used, unit showing processes, handling leasing inquiries, turnovers, and renewals
3. Expense ratio targets: 40-50% for properties 1980 and older; expect brokers to squeeze these low—fine-tune to realistic 45-49% range
4. Expense and tax numbers are the primary levers to pull on a proforma when revenue (rents) and financing are fixed
5. Request all major financial documents and surveys as 'Exhibit B' in contracts—frame as wish list and let seller say no rather than assuming unavailability
6. Never start tenant renewals only 60 days before lease expiration; they're already looking—initiate outreach 90 days prior
7. Entity transfer strategy: if sellers move property into new LLC with 12-24 month maturity before sale, reduces red flags from school districts and tax assessors challenging reassessment
