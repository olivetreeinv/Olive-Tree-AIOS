## Summary
Justin Brennan mentors Tony on structuring a 39-unit value-add deal in Cleveland (7049 Superior Street, 44103) purchased for $546K with $2.6M construction loan at 13% interest-only. The deal involves full gut renovation, seller financing payoff by August 29th, projected $5M+ ARV, and refinance strategy to return all equity within 18 months while building significant profit equity.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Write three LOIs per week for one month to qualify for quarterly on-site coaching visit to your market
2. Structure: property LLC in state of location (e.g., Texas LLC), holding company in Wyoming/Delaware ($80/year maintenance), investors held in holding company at ~60% with GP retaining 40% plus LP share ('double dipping')
3. Construction loan interest reserve should cover 18 months not 12 months; at $2.6M loan at 13%, build in ~$500K interest reserve to avoid feeding deal from pocket if you extend
4. Plan 18-month timeline: 9-12 months construction + 6 months lease-up/stabilization minimum before refinance; construction loans typically 12-24 months with 6-month optional extension (~0.5-1 point)
5. Start pre-leasing at 3-6 months into construction using Fiverr renderings posted to apartments.com to achieve 40-50% pre-leased at certificate of occupancy
6. Include $50K+ marketing budget for lease-up phase; treat heavy renovation projects like new construction from marketing standpoint—'blast at 100 miles an hour' with signage, banners, flags, not just Zillow drip
7. Fence entire property with 5-6 foot iron fencing, gate all parking, install lighting to privatize and secure—model after similar developments that prove what works in market; security is 'numero uno'
8. ARV appraisal post-renovation determines takeout loan amount; if 70% LTV on $5M ARV = $3.5M loan pays off $3.556M all-in cost, creating $1.5M+ profit equity in 24 months (~78% annualized return)
9. Verify property for opportunity zone status by contacting city directly—surrounding areas may qualify but specific address must be confirmed
10. Don't use small regional banks; use national banks (Bank of America, Wells Fargo, US Bank) with branch presence nationwide for ease of deposits, tech integration with QuickBooks, and out-of-state access
11. LLC agent of service costs ~$120/year (e.g., Incorp); necessary when forming LLC in state where you don't physically reside
12. Wyoming/Delaware LLCs can hide ownership via trust structure (e.g., 123trust.com trustee) so creditors cannot identify owners; harder for creditors to pierce veil vs other states
13. Don't create foreign entity registration in property state if holding LLC already in Wyoming/Delaware—creates double costs and dual agent/attorney management; single state LLC for property + Wyoming holding company is more efficient
