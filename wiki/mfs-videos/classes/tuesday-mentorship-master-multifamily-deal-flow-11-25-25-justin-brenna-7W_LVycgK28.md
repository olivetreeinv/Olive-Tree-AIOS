## Summary
Justin Brennan guides mentorship group through analyzing multifamily deal financials, focusing on identifying red flags in T12 statements and expense line items. The discussion centers on proper expense categorization, capitalized items versus operational expenses, and benchmarking utilities, maintenance, repairs, and turnover costs for accurate deal underwriting.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. T12 should show Gross Potential Income, then deduct Loss to Lease, Vacancy, Bad Debt, and Concessions to reach Effective Gross Income (not just collected rent)
2. Four critical expense line items to monitor: Turnover ($1,500 per unit when it occurs), Repairs & Maintenance ($700-800/unit), Utilities (varies by market), Property Taxes (research county assessment cycles)
3. Total R&M + Turnover should benchmark around $1,000 per unit per year; flag as red flag if significantly higher
4. Turnover calculation: 30% of units × $1,500 per turned unit; if retaining 70-80% of tenants annually you're performing well
5. Utilities at $166/unit/month (~$2,000/year) is abnormally high for 36-unit property; investigate if owner is illegally paying tenant electricity
6. Water/Sewer reimbursement typically recovers 60-65% of costs through tenant charges; remainder is landlord expense
7. Extract capex items (roof replacement, HVAC condenser replacement, furnace replacement) from operational expenses; monthly plumbing over $1,600+ suggests systemic line issues requiring investigation
8. Property taxes: Calculate based on post-purchase county assessment; budget for higher tax rates for first 6 months of ownership until stabilization
