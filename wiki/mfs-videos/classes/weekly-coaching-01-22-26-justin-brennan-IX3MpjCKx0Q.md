## Summary
Justin Brennan coaches on closing process fundamentals including PSA review, financial due diligence items (lease audits, delinquency lists, bank statements), and property manager hiring/evaluation. He then analyzes two deals: a 122-unit in San Antonio (recommends against due to Texas costs under 150 units) and a 16-unit duplex deal in Missouri (workable at right price with creative financing strategy).

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Send 22 due diligence questions from acquisition checklist to broker/seller BEFORE signing PSA
2. Focus on 'Sellers Representations and Warranties' section in PSA for accountability if seller misrepresents
3. Lease audit critical: match tenant names on leases to rent roll exactly; mismatches create legal eviction problems
4. Require property bank statements for last 3-6-12 months; collections should match delinquency claims (e.g., if 95% collection on $10K/month rent = ~$9,500 in deposits)
5. Don't accept reserve account funds in closing; only prrated rents, deposits, and property expenses transfer
6. Incentivize property managers: pay 2x more for renewals at 90-120 days out than new leases to hit 70-80% annual retention
7. Request 4 key weekly reports to predict property status 90 days out: delinquency list, work orders, unit availability, expiring leases
8. Property threshold: avoid Texas deals under 150 units due to high insurance/property taxes/payroll eating margins (recommend 15% economic loss assumption not 8%)
9. Test multiple PMs during first 2 weeks in contract with lease audit exercise; if they don't know what lease audit is, 'run'
10. For duplexes with sale upside: model selling 2 units within 24 months, add net profit to cash flow to boost IRR; requires local bank release schedule on debt
