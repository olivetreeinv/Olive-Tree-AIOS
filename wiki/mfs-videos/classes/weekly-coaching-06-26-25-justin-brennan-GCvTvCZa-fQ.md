## Summary
Justin Brennan coaches Angelica through analyzing a 24-unit multifamily property in Miami worth $4.75M, focusing on proper underwriting methodology, expense estimation using ChatGPT for tax calculations, and creative seller financing structures. He demonstrates how to extract unit mix and rent data from rent rolls, project future rents based on market comparables, and structure a deal where sellers become lenders to avoid massive tax events while generating monthly income.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Extract unit mix from rent rolls by asking 'what are your unit types' and count each type separately (e.g., 15 x 1BR @ $1,500 avg, 9 x 2BR @ $2,100 avg)
2. Use ChatGPT for property tax estimation: input property address + purchase price and ask for 'projected property taxes annually based on assessed value' - then adjust for 90% of purchase price if needed
3. For expense ratios on class-B/C properties aged 1950s in Florida: target 40-48% expense ratio to EGI (effective gross income)
4. Management fees should be 8% of gross potential rents for professional third-party management
5. Maintenance and repairs for older properties: budget at least $500-750/month for reasonable estimates
6. Contract services (landscaping, etc.) typically minimum $500/month - verify actual contracts with broker
7. Seller financing strategy: if sellers have no debt, offer $600K down payment + $28K/month for 5-year note at 6.25% interest, allowing them to avoid tax event, receive lump sum, get monthly income, and avoid property management
8. Use cap rate formula: NOI ÷ purchase price = cap rate; ensure cap rate > loan interest rate (e.g., 6.71% cap > 6.25% loan = positive spread)
9. Acquisition fee calculation: 2% of purchase price ($4.75M deal = $95K fee); as GP you can structure deal where acquisition fee covers your equity requirement
