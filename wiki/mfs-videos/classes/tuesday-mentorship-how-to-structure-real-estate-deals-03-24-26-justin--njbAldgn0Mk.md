## Summary
Justin Brennan walks through updates to an automated multifamily deal analyzer tool, focusing on refinancing mechanics, sources & uses breakdown, waterfall distributions, and GP equity splits. He addresses deal structuring questions from mentees, including one participant (Doug) in best-and-final negotiations on a Georgia acquisition. Content is relevant to underwriting and deal modeling but not specific to sourcing or analyzing actual deals.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Lender typically covers 80-90% of renovation budget plus ~25% of closing costs; model this in sources & uses to show equity vs. loan breakdown
2. Refinance in year 3 example: 70% LTV, 2% closing cost, can generate ~$344k profit after paying preferred returns to investors
3. Once investors hit 15-20% annualized IRR (internal rate of return), they can be taken out of deal entirely via refinance proceeds; industry standard is 15%+ IRR to attract capital
4. Exit cap rate is a 'big guess' based on interest rate trends—buy high cap rate, sell low cap rate by increasing NOI and compressing caps as rates fall
5. Earnest money, inspections, appraisals all count toward GP's total capital commitment; all reimbursed at closing but capital stays in deal
6. PPM (private placement memorandum) essential even for friends & family deals; costs $10-13k first time, ~$5k thereafter, reimbursed via closing costs
7. Loan guarantor provision in GP split should sunset if guarantor changes at refinance; language protects against future what-ifs
