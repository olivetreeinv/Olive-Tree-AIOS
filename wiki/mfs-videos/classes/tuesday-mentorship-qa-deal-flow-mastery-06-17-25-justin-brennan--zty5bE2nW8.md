## Key Takeaways
1. Use Real Estate Radar software with filters: 10-40 unit deals, loan maturity dates 06/2025-12/2026 to identify distressed sellers who must refinance or sell
2. Direct-to-broker approach requires least time/effort initially—build rapport by submitting lower LOIs with phone calls explaining your underwriting before expecting pre-market deal flow
3. When higher interest rates kill deal math (e.g., 7.25% rates), back into offer price explaining to brokers: seller must either finance portion of deal or accept lower price reflecting current market fundamentals
4. Propose seller financing by asking broker: Does seller need all money out now? Are they facing large taxable event? If no to both, offer higher price in exchange for 2-3 year seller note
5. Vacancy credit loss industry standard 5-7% on pro formas is unrealistic—use 7-10% for most markets, adjust year-by-year as operations improve; Texas market currently seeing 15-20% rent drops with 1-2 months concessions
6. Earn equity into partnerships through measurable tasks: sourcing deals, due diligence, boots-on-ground management, loan guarantor, capital raising (allocate up to 30% of GP for capital raised)
7. Never overleveraged properties (65% debt max) allows survival through down cycles; multifamily historically doubles in value within 10-year hold period if not forced to sell
