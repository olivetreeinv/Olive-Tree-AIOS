## Key Takeaways
1. Use broker listings as 'bait' to start conversations without needing to pitch upfront—discuss the property then pivot to 'Are there other areas you work in or other deals?'
2. State your buy box clearly with specific metrics: '10 to 50 unit, B and C class, light to medium value-add, mid-teens IRR (15%+) on property-level returns' to build broker trust and credibility
3. Set 30-40 minute maximum drive time from main metro MSA to manage recession risk; properties beyond this threshold face tenant demand risk if economic downturn forces job-center migration
4. Target MSAs from top 100 list via ChatGPT; avoid secondary/tertiary markets
5. For mixed-income properties, cap section 8 at ~10% of units (3-4 units per 30-unit complex) rather than 100% section 8 due to screening/management challenges at scale when out-of-state
6. After broker introduction, add contact to CRM tagged by market, then 'drip' on them weekly with relevant touches; visit in person when conducting market trip to 'shake hands and kiss babies'
7. On leverage: acceptable to go 75% LTV on acquisition if operational value-add will rapidly push property value higher; avoid 80-85% LTV; monitor year-two projected LTV drop to 60-67% range
