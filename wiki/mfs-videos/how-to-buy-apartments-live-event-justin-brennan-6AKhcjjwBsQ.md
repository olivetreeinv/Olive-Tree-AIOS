## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches a comprehensive framework for buying 5+ unit apartment buildings, covering deal sourcing through property management. He demonstrates that apartment investing is more accessible and lucrative than single-family homes, using specific examples (10-unit Huntsville AL property at $625k, 31-unit Kansas City deal) to show how investors can generate 26-35%+ cash-on-cash returns through acquisition fees, cash flow, and profit splits as a general partner.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Find deals on CoStar.com (40% on-market), then build broker relationships to access 60% off-market deals—must execute 1-2 on-market deals first to gain credibility
2. Structure deals as 10% GP equity / 90% LP investor capital; GP receives 30% profit split (vs 70% for LPs) despite 10% capital, creating leverage opportunity
3. Day-one acquisition fee: 3% of purchase price (e.g., $47k on $1.58M purchase); GP also collects 1% asset management fee on operating income and 5% construction management fee
4. Purchase $625k property with $100k renovations at $10k/unit; expect 20% property-level return and 26%+ cash-on-cash return on GP capital after fees
5. Proforma model plugs in blue fields (rates, down %, units, rents, expenses); black formulas auto-calculate NOI, returns, and cash flows—templates provided
6. Manage via four critical reports: Available Units List (vacancy status), Work Order Summary (repairs responded to in 24-48hrs, completed in 7 days), Delinquency List, Expiring Leases (90-day forward view)
7. Each tenant departure costs minimum $3,500 (lost rent + turnover); focus on work order execution and lease renewals rather than replacing tenants
