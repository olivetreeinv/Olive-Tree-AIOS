## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches a comprehensive 90-day framework for buying multifamily apartments, covering deal finding through brokers, financial analysis using NOI, and capital raising through OPM. He emphasizes that for 15-50 unit properties, the process follows identical patterns regardless of size, and demonstrates a case study where he made $590K on an $18K investment through acquisition fees, cash flow, refinancing, and profit sharing.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Find deals via brokers using on-market listings to access off-market deals: 60% of deals are off-market, 40% on-market; on-market deals are overpriced
2. NOI is the critical metric: $1,000/month increase in NOI = $220,000 increase in property value
3. Use cap rate approach: ask brokers 'What cap rate are properties like this selling for?' to determine if deal makes sense
4. Target IRR of 14-20% annually, cash-on-cash return of 6-10% per year, equity multiple of 1.8X minimum
5. Acquisition fees: 2-3% of purchase price paid day one at closing, reducing your out-of-pocket capital
6. Economic loss typically runs 12-18% of gross income (accounts for vacancy, loss to lease, bad debt, concessions)
7. First deals should be 10-30 units to keep capital requirements manageable and work with mom-and-pop brokers/sellers
