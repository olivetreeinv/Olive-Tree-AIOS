## Summary
Justin Brennan walks through a complete deal analysis framework using a real 17-unit Cincinnati property ($2M asking price) as a case study. He demonstrates how to plug property data into a deal analyzer spreadsheet, evaluate three key metrics (IRR, cash-on-cash, equity multiple), and determine whether a deal meets investment thresholds of 14-20% IRR and 6-10% cash-on-cash return. He then shows how to negotiate price downward when a deal doesn't work at asking price, and introduces the critical concept of leveraging on-market deals to access off-market/pre-market opportunities through broker relationships.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Target IRR between 14-20% annual rate; cash-on-cash return 6-10% per year; equity multiple 1.8-2.0x as primary deal filters
2. Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DCR) must be 1.15-1.25 minimum for commercial loans (5+ units); banks underwrite the property, not the borrower
3. On-market deals represent only 40% of available market; 60% are off-market/pre-market deals that typically make better financial sense
4. Use broker relationships strategically: tour 3 properties in 48 hours, meet property managers and lenders during same trip to build local market intelligence
5. When deal doesn't pencil at asking price, adjust these levers in order: (1) interest rate down, (2) down payment percentage, (3) rental rate increases, (4) purchase price reduction
6. Closing costs should include 2-3% acquisition fee for deal assembly, which can offset initial cash outlay
7. Write Letter of Intent (LOI) at your maximum price even if below asking; use rejection as entry point to ask broker for pre-market deal access
