## Summary
This is a lengthy autobiographical interview with real estate developer Henish Pulickal covering his career journey from mortgage underwriting through REO asset management to house flipping and multifamily development. While Henish builds multifamily projects, the conversation focuses heavily on personal background, philanthropy, marriage advice, and general real estate philosophy rather than specific deal analysis, underwriting techniques, or sourcing strategies relevant to Brian Norton's 15-50 unit value-add multifamily focus in GA/TN/AL.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Henish targets 1,000 homes built by 2033 in San Diego via construction company
2. All multifamily projects built as rentals (not condos) to avoid 10-year liability window and condo conversion litigation risks
3. Less than 0.2% change orders on construction projects due to rigorous upfront budgeting and avoiding scope creep
4. Hard money rates: 10-10.5% interest, 1-2 points origination, plus interest reserve for development deals
5. Henish hosts monthly 'Deal or No Deal' networking event (3rd Tuesday) with 20-30 project pitches using crowdsourced underwriting approach
6. Construction costs baseline: ~$300/foot vertical (hard costs only), reduced to ~$275/foot with new off-site wall panel robot framing system
7. Deal sourcing: direct-to-seller, pre-market agent deals, and networking events; avoids MLS wholesalers due to insufficient spreads
