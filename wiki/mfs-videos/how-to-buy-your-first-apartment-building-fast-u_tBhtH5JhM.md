## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches how to buy 10-30 unit apartment buildings with minimal capital ($20K out of pocket) using syndication and OPM (other people's money). He breaks down a real 31-unit case study showing how to structure deals with 70% debt financing, leverage acquisition fees, and execute multiple exits including refinancing and cash-out strategies. The video targets newer investors, emphasizing that expertise is not required—only skill-building and action.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Focus on 10-30 unit deals as a newbie (easier than 50+ units with mom-and-pop sellers/brokers)
2. Syndication structure: You bring 10% of capital (~$50K), control 40% of deal; OPM brings 90% (~$450K), gets 60%
3. Acquisition fee: 2-3% of purchase price, paid within days of close, built into closing costs (reduces your true out-of-pocket to $20K on a $50K investment)
4. 31-unit case study: $1.525M purchase + $400K renovation + $225K closing = $2.15M all-in; 70% LTV loan = $1.505M; your $64K in, $45K acquisition fee out = $19K net out-of-pocket
5. Pre-market deals: 80% of multifamily deals sell to brokers' 25-50 investor friends before hitting websites (leftovers are only 20% of market)
6. 3-4 deals in 10-30 unit range generate $10-20K monthly passive income; plan 2-3 years to scale
7. Don't overlever: Use 70% debt + 30% equity + fixed-rate debt; cash-flowing assets are inflation-proof regardless of market conditions
