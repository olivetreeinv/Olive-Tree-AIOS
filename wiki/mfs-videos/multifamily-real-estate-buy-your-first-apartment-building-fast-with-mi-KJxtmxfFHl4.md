## Summary
Justin Brennan teaches how to buy a 10-30 unit multifamily property in 90 days with minimal out-of-pocket capital ($4,000-$20,000) by leveraging other people's money, acquisition fees, and proper deal structuring. He breaks down deal analysis metrics (14-20% IRR, 6-10% cash-on-cash returns, 1.8x equity multiple), explains the net operating income formula and its impact on property value, and demonstrates a real 31-unit deal where $18,200 out-of-pocket generated $590,000+ in total returns.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Focus on 10-30 unit deals as a sweet spot; avoid 50+ units without experience because sophisticated brokers and lenders won't engage
2. Net operating income drives property value: value = NOI divided by cap rate; a $1,000/month NOI increase = $220,000 property value increase at 5.5% cap rate
3. Leverage on-market listings (40% of deals) to access off-market brokers (60% of deals never listed); tour 3 on-market properties to build rapport and get pre-market deal access
4. Acquisition fees typically 2-3% of purchase price are built into closing costs and often offset your initial out-of-pocket capital
5. You get paid 5 ways in multifamily: acquisition fees, cash flow (30-40% of deal), asset management fees (1-2% of revenue), profit splits (30-40%), and tax benefits through depreciation
6. Structure deals as general partner with 30% promote for 10% capital contribution; double-dip by also receiving limited partner returns on your own money
7. Target minimum returns: IRR 14-20% annually, investor cash-on-cash 6-10%, equity multiple 1.8x or higher to attract capital
