## Summary
Kenneth demonstrates a covered call on HIMS that's deep in-the-money (stock at $58.08 vs $49.50 strike). He addresses whether to buy back the contract to avoid assignment, concluding it's not worth the $880/contract cost ($2,600+ loss) and instead accepts assignment as part of the strategy's design, then plans to re-enter via cash-secured puts or re-buying stock.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. When stock gaps significantly above strike (HIMS $58.08 vs $49.50), buying back costs $880/contract—calculate total loss before deciding to close
2. Accept assignment when cost to buyback exceeds acceptable loss threshold; it's built into the covered call strategy
3. After assignment, rotate into cash-secured puts or re-establish position the following week rather than fighting the cap
4. Covered calls intentionally cap upside—acknowledge this risk upfront; only suitable if you can accept getting called away at strike price
5. Covered call wheel strategy requires minimal active management—Kenneth's stated preference despite occasional capped gains
