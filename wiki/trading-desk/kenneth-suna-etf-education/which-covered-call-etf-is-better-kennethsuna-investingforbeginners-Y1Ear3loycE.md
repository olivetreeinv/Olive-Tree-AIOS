## Summary
Kenneth compares two covered call ETFs (JEPPY vs JEEPQ), explaining their structural differences, holdings compositions, and risk-return profiles. JEPPY focuses on S&P 500 with 18.75% tech exposure and 8% yield, while JEEPQ focuses on NASDAQ-100 with 50% tech exposure and 9% yield. Both use hybrid approaches combining long-term stock holdings with covered call selling.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Both JEPPY and JEEPQ sell covered calls on a portion of holdings while maintaining long-term stock investments (hybrid approach)
2. JEPPY has 132 total holdings vs JEEPQ's 98 holdings, indicating different diversification strategies
3. JEPPY dividend yield around 8%, JEEPQ yield slightly higher around 9%
4. JEPPY up 3% over 1-year, JEEPQ up 22% over 1-year, driven by NASDAQ exposure difference
5. JEPPY 18.75% technology allocation vs JEEPQ 50% technology allocation reflects underlying index focus
6. Both funds relatively new (JEPPY since 2020, JEEPQ since 2022), limiting historical performance data
7. Identical expense ratios between the two funds
