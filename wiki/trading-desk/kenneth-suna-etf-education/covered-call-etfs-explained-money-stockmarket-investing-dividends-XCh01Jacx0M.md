## Summary
Kenneth explains covered call mechanics and compares covered call ETFs (JEPI vs QYLD) as passive income vehicles. He demonstrates how selling covered calls generates premium income while potentially capping upside, and provides specific calculations for dividend yields and share requirements to hit income targets.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell covered calls monthly on shares you already own to generate extra income
2. Premium is kept regardless of whether shares are called away
3. If shares called away, you owe taxes on the gains
4. JEPI pays $6/share dividend (11.3% yield) at $53/share; QYLD pays $2.09/share (13.3% yield) at $15.87/share
5. To generate $1,000/year from JEPI requires 166 shares costing ~$8,930; from QYLD requires 478 shares costing ~$7,500
6. To generate $20,000/year from JEPI requires 3,546 shares costing ~$191,000
7. Covered call ETFs limit explosive growth but provide consistent high dividend income as part of diversified portfolio
