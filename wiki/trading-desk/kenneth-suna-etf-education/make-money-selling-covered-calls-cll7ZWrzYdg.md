## Summary
Kenneth Suna critiques unrealistic covered call income claims on TikTok, using Tesla and Palantir examples to show actual premiums ($695 for Tesla, $159 for Palantir) and the capital requirements (100 shares needed per contract). He emphasizes realistic expectations for retail investors selling covered calls on stocks they already own.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Selling one covered call contract requires owning 100 shares of the underlying stock
2. Tesla example: $165/share × 100 = $16,500 capital required to sell one $170 strike covered call for $695 premium
3. Palantir example: ~$2,100 for 100 shares, selling $22 strike covered call generates $159 premium
4. Income claims of $700-$900k monthly on social media are misleading and unrealistic for most retail investors
5. Covered call premium is kept regardless of stock direction, but shares can be called away if stock closes above strike price at expiration
