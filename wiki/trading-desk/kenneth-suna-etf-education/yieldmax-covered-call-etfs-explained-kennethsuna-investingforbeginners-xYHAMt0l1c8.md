## Summary
Kenneth Suna explains covered call ETFs (Yieldmax, JEPI, QQQ) as a way to generate passive income through premium selling, but warns against single-stock covered call ETFs due to volatility risk and inconsistent dividend payouts. He recommends diversified covered call ETFs and demonstrates his own TS position strategy of not reinvesting dividends.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Covered calls require owning at least 100 shares to sell one contract
2. Single-stock covered call ETFs like TS (Tesla) generated 96% yields initially but dividends dropped from 80 cents to 55 cents as volatility declined
3. Diversified covered call ETFs (JEPI, QQQ) are less volatile and more consistent than single-stock alternatives
4. Do not reinvest dividends from high-volatility covered call ETFs; take cash instead to redeploy into quality assets
5. High dividend yields on volatile stocks often precede both premium and share price declines when momentum slows
6. Covered call ETFs underperform index funds because shares get called away, but provide consistent income trade-off
7. Kenneth's risk rule: only put in what you're willing to lose, and redirect high-volatility covered call income to stable assets like S&P 500 index funds
