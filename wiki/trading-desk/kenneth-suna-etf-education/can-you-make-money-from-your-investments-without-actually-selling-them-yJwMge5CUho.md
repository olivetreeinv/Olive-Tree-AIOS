## Summary
Kenneth Suna demonstrates three methods to profit from investments without selling: dividend collection, selling covered calls on long-term holdings, and collateralized lending (pledged asset lines). The primary focus is on covered calls as an income strategy, using Palantir as an example where weekly call sales generate $370+ per contract.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell weekly expiring covered calls to generate consistent premium income without liquidating shares
2. Require 100 shares minimum per covered call contract to establish position-building as long-term strategy
3. Use delta as probability metric: 30% delta ≈ 30% probability of assignment (e.g., $99 strike on $93 stock)
4. Target out-of-the-money strikes (e.g., $94-$99 on $93 stock) to reduce assignment risk while collecting premium
5. Can buy back options before expiration to lock in gains and retain shares if underlying rallies
6. Weekly expirations provide more frequent premium collection opportunities than longer-dated contracts
7. Pledged asset lines (Charles Schwab) allow borrowing against portfolio without liquidation, avoiding capital gains taxes
