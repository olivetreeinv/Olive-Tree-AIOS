## Summary
Kenneth Suna explains covered call ETFs, highlighting that they underperform underlying stocks during rallies (Nvidia +76% vs YieldMax +22%) and carry high fees. While they provide passive income through dividends, he suggests alternatives: selling your own covered calls if you own 100 shares, owning both individual shares and the ETF to preserve cost basis and avoid taxes, or buying the ETF purely for passive income if uninterested in stock ownership.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Covered call ETFs will underperform underlying stocks during strong rallies—example: Nvidia stock +76% YTD vs YieldMax covered call ETF +22% YTD
2. Covered call ETFs carry very high fees due to active management
3. Dividends from covered call ETFs are variable monthly based on stock volatility—Tesla dividends ranged from $2.18 to $0.80 as volatility decreased
4. To sell your own covered calls, you must first own 100 shares outright
5. Owning both individual shares (low cost basis) and the covered call ETF preserves tax efficiency and cost basis if shares get called away
6. If shares are called away from an ETF, you miss upside participation in further rallies
