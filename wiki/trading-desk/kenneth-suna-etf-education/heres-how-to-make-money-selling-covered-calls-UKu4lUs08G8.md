## Summary
Kenneth demonstrates how to sell covered calls on Meta using Thinkorswim, earning $1,800 premium by Friday, but advises against this specific trade due to upcoming earnings and stock volatility risk. He illustrates the capital requirement ($46,000 for 100 Meta shares) and contrasts returns across different stock prices (Palantir example yields only $13).

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Avoid selling covered calls on stocks down dramatically when earnings are imminent—risk of assignment outweighs premium collected
2. Strike prices further out-of-the-money yield smaller premiums but lower assignment probability
3. Capital requirement: $46,600 needed to sell one covered call contract on Meta at $466/share to collect $1,800 premium
4. Covered call premium collection requires owning 100 shares per contract
5. Lower-priced stocks like Palantir ($27-28 range) generate minimal weekly premium (~$13/contract) vs. expensive stocks like Meta/Tesla
6. One-week expiration on covered calls during earnings week is too risky despite attractive premium dollar amounts
