---

## Key Takeaways
1. Average up into strength rather than waiting for dips; buy quality stocks as they go up (NVDA example: bought at $125, $154, $226, $389, $413, $380, $400)
2. QYLD only sells covered calls with no buy-and-hold component, so it won't capture full market upside in rallies like hybrid ETFs (JEPI, SCHD) do
3. Don't chase stocks up 1,000% in a year or chase penny stocks up 60%+ in days—usually dumps afterward
4. For speculative positions, focus on 'how much am I willing to lose' not 'how much can I make'; size positions accordingly (e.g., $3M NVIDIA investment on $2.18T market cap)
5. Covered calls lock in opportunity cost—if you bought NVDA at $125 and sold calls, you'd miss the $900 move; only sell calls on stocks you're planning to exit anyway
6. Don't wait for pullbacks or corrections; young investors sitting on sidelines missed 20%+ S&P returns and 60%+ tech returns in recent years
7. When buying speculative assets like Joby Aviation at $5.29/share, ask how many shares you'd buy if bankrupt (e.g., 100 shares = $529 loss tolerance)
