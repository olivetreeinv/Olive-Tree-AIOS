## Summary
Kenneth demonstrates a weekly covered call strategy on HIMS, selling $1 OTM calls for ~$25 premium with the goal of $180-200/week. In this example, he made $300 when shares were assigned at $48 strike, combining $25 premium + $95 stock gain, and explains why rolling calls (buying back early) often doesn't make sense versus accepting assignment and restarting the cycle.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell covered calls $1 out-of-the-money (OTM) on high-volatility stocks like HIMS to target $25 premium per weekly contract
2. If assigned, profit = premium collected + difference between purchase price and strike price; if not assigned, keep both premium and shares plus stock appreciation
3. Rolling/buying back contracts to prevent assignment costs capital (e.g., $400) and often destroys P&L; only roll if you have substantial unrealized gains or tax concerns worth the cost
4. Weekly covered calls work best on stocks with consistent volatility; as volatility decays, premiums shrink and you must find replacement stocks
5. Entry price matters for total return: buying at $47 and selling calls at $48 strike yields ~$95 profit on assignment plus $25 premium = $120 per round
6. Target $1 strike distance (OTM) balances high assignment probability with acceptable premium collection for consistent weekly income
7. Track weekly outcomes: update Monday on new purchase/sale, then Friday/Saturday on assignment or roll decision to maintain accountability.
