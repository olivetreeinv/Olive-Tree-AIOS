## Summary
Kenneth Suna discusses whether to sell covered calls on Amazon shares, covering strike selection strategies (at-the-money vs. out-of-the-money), capital requirements (~$22,700 for 100 shares at $227), and risks including assignment and opportunity cost if the stock gaps higher. He avoids recommending a specific approach, emphasizing personal decision-making.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. At-the-money covered calls (227.50 strike on 226 stock) yield $830 premium but result in assignment if stock closes above strike by expiration
2. Out-of-the-money covered calls (235 strike) yield lower premium ($540) but capture $800 additional profit if stock rises to strike price (8-point gain)
3. Assignment risk peaks when selling covered calls close to purchase price (buying at 227, selling 228 call caps gains at 228)
4. Stock drops after purchase eliminate covered call income generation; sitting on losses with shares you don't want to sell creates forced holding
5. Buying additional 100-share positions enables simultaneous long holding and covered call selling to preserve upside while generating premium
