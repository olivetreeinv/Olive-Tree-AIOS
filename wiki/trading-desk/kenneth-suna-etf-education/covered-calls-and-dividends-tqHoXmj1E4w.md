## Summary
Kenneth Suna compares covered call premiums across stocks of varying volatility, demonstrating that stable dividend-paying stocks (Coca-Cola, JNJ, McDonald's) generate 1-1.86% weekly premiums but require large capital outlays, while riskier stocks like HIMS generate 4.4% weekly premiums. He emphasizes understanding individual risk tolerance and warns that early assignment on dividend stocks can cause investors to miss dividend payments.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Stable stocks like Coca-Cola yield ~1.24% premium per week on $6,900-$7,000 capital
2. HIMS generates 4.4% weekly premium but carries elevated volatility risk
3. Johnson & Johnson pays only ~0.6% premium on $16,000 capital required
4. Waste Management yields 1.86% monthly (not weekly) on $22,586 capital
5. McDonald's yields ~0.9% weekly on ~$30,000 capital
6. Monitor X-dividend dates closely when selling covered calls; early assignment forfeits dividend rights
7. Higher premiums correlate directly with stock volatility; stable stocks = lower premiums but more predictable risk
