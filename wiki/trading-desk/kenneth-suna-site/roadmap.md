## Summary
A beginner's checklist/roadmap: brokerage + HYSA + research tools + recommended books + budgeting app, ending with a paid Research Vault upsell. Mostly a curated link list, light on original explanation.

## Key Content
This is your roadmap to becoming the best investor you can be. Ready? Let’s get started.

- First, you need a brokerage. I use Charles Schwab (https://www.schwab.com/client-referral?refrid=REFERUYD9QE9V). You should consider opening TWO accounts: A Roth IRA and an Individual Account.

- You’ll need a High Yield Savings Account (HYSA). There are a lot of good ones. I like SOFI’s (https://www.sofi.com/invite/money?gcp=795da27f-cc05-4a83-b153-29d8ab47dfe0&isAliasGcp=false).

- You need a hub for researching stocks. I like a few. Seeking Alpha is great in a lot of ways (but their “analysts” suck). You might not need a premium account, but if you want to try it:
a. Seeking Alpha (https://link.seekingalpha.com/326NL2/4G6SHH/?creative_id=12)
b. Market Watch (https://www.marketwatch.com/)
c. Google Finance (https://www.google.com/finance/beta)

- Read a book, nerd. The first two, I read. The others, I didn’t read but have come highly recommended.
a. Neatest Little Guide to Stock Market Investing (https://amzn.to/4a7yf34)
b. How I Made $2,000,000 In The Stock Market (https://amzn.to/4pcm7Cq) (for swing trading)
c. The Intelligent Investor (https://amzn.to/4o97EX0)
d. The Richest Man in Babylon (https://amzn.to/3M8ygKa)

- My free investing for beginners course

- Let AI Work FOR you. Fin-gist (https://fin-gist.info/4odTQLe) summarizes stock headlines.

- An investing calculator (https://www.calculator.net/investment-calculator.html). I live on this website.

- An app to help you budget. (http://monarchmoney.sjv.io/vnedoL)

- Finally, if you want to spend a few bucks, you can get lifetime access to my Research Vault for $25.
