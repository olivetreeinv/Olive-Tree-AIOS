## Summary
Kenneth demonstrates a weekly covered call strategy on HIMS, selling $25 premium contracts struck $1 out-of-the-money against 100-share positions. He explains the mechanics of assignment, profit scenarios, and why he doesn't roll contracts despite near-assignment situations, netting $300 this week versus $236 from outright stock trading.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell covered calls $1 out-of-the-money on volatile weekly series stocks like HIMS
2. Target $25 premium per weekly contract (~5% return on $475 stock cost)
3. Win either way at assignment ($95 profit) or stock retention ($205 profit + premium)
4. Don't roll contracts when cost to buyback (~$400) exceeds profit from extension
5. Monitor stock price daily starting Thursday to anticipate assignment likelihood
6. Use for short-term income generation separate from long-term buy-and-hold positions
7. Stock volatility determines premium sustainability—switch stocks if premiums decline
