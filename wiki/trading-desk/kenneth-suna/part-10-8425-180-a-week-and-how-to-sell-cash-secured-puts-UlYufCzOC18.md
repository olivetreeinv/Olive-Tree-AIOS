## Summary
Kenneth Suna demonstrates how to sell cash-secured puts as part of a premium-selling strategy, using HIMS earnings as a high-risk example. He sold 3 contracts at $57 strike for $814 premium (4.7% return) while managing downside risk by reserving $17,100 in capital. The video includes a step-by-step tutorial on executing cash-secured put trades via broker platform.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell cash-secured puts on stocks you're comfortable owning long-term
2. Strike selection: choose strikes where stock would need to drop dramatically (e.g., HIMS at $64, sold $57 puts)
3. Premium must compensate for max loss: $35 premium + $55.95 net cost = $59 strike price
4. Reserve full capital requirement upfront: selling 3 contracts at $57 requires $17,100 reserved in account
5. Avoid greedy exits: lock in $193 on Oscar Health rather than chase extra $10-15 per contract
6. Earnings trades are high-risk, suitable only for experienced traders (15+ years trading)
7. Weekly target: $1,200 premium on $50k portfolio (~5% return for the week)
