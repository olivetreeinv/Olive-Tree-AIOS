## Summary
Kenneth demonstrates his weekly covered call selling strategy across a diversified portfolio, generating $1,400-$1,700 in premium income over the week of 9/8-9/12. He explains implied volatility (IV) as the key driver of premium income, using IV percentages to justify higher returns on volatile stocks versus blue chips, and emphasizes accepting opportunity cost rather than chasing missed gains.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Implied volatility directly determines premium size: QBTS and D-Wave at 88% IV generate much higher premiums than Coca-Cola at 12% IV
2. Target high-IV stocks (70%+ IV range) for selling covered calls to maximize weekly premium income
3. Accept that you won't capture every gain: focus on consistent weekly premium, not hindsight-maximized returns
4. Roll calls early when underlying jumps significantly (Open: $5→$10) rather than wait for expiration, even if early assignment costs opportunity gains
5. Weekly covered call strategy on volatile micro-cap/growth stocks can generate 3-5% weekly returns versus typical 1% industry average
6. Patience with losing positions pays off: hold underwater positions and sell calls to generate income while waiting for recovery
7. Diversify across multiple positions to smooth income and reduce single-stock risk in high-volatility portfolio
