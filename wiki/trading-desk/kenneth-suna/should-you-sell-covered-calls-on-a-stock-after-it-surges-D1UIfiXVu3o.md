## Summary
Kenneth advises against selling covered calls immediately after a stock has surged dramatically (e.g., HIMS up 16% in one day). The premiums appear attractive but the risk of a sharp pullback in the following days/weeks makes the risk-reward unfavorable for small premium gains. Instead, he recommends waiting for the inevitable pullback before entering the position.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Do not sell covered calls on a stock that has just experienced a massive single-day rip (16%+ moves)
2. After extreme runups, expect a pullback within days to 1-2 weeks; use Fibonacci retracements to identify likely support levels
3. Calculate if the premium justifies the capital at risk; $159-$182 premium on $5,800 capital is insufficient risk-reward
4. When a stock is overbought after a rip, redeploy capital to other stocks with better risk-adjusted premiums rather than forcing a trade on the surged stock
5. Cash-secured puts on pulled-back stocks offer better entry points than covered calls on fresh runups, if the strike price aligns with your buy target
6. Wait for the stock to drop to your desired entry price (e.g., $54 on HIMS) before committing capital, rather than chasing after a surge
