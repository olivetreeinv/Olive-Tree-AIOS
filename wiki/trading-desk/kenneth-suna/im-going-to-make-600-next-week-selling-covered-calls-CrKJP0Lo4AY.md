## Summary
Kenneth Suna demonstrates a weekly covered call strategy targeting volatile stocks to generate consistent premium income. He analyzes three high-volatility stocks (BBAI, Archer Aviation, Joby Aviation) with 1,000-share positions, comparing returns on premium income alone versus total profit including stock appreciation, and shows how position sizing can optimize dollar returns for fixed capital.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell covered calls on 1,000-share positions (10 contracts per position)
2. Target weekly expirations for faster income cycles
3. BBAI example: $7 entry, $7.50 strike, $400 premium = 5.6% weekly return
4. Archer Aviation example: $10.78 entry, $11 strike, $340 premium = 3.1% weekly return
5. Joby Aviation example: $12.33 entry, $12.50 strike, $500 premium = 4.0% weekly return
6. Choose strike prices 5-10% above current stock price for high premium
7. Accept capped upside: "I'm not here to make all the money, just some of it"
8. Reposition sizing for better returns: same $12k in BBAI (1,700 shares) yields $680 premium vs $500 in Joby
