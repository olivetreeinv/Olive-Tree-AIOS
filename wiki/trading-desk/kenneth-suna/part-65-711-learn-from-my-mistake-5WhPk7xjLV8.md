## Summary
Kenneth reviews his weekly covered call performance on HIMS stock, highlighting a timing mistake where he sold a $50 call Monday morning for only $50 premium when the stock was down, versus waiting until Wednesday when he could have earned $130 on a similar strike. He also discusses a successful position where 200 shares were called away at $46, generating $392 total profit and freeing $9,000 in capital for reinvestment.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Wait for stock recoveries before selling covered calls—RSI oversold + moving average crossovers + volume increase indicate better premium timing
2. Premium improvement example: $50 call sold at $46-47 stock price=$50 premium vs. waiting to $50 stock price=$130 premium (2.6x difference in 2-3 days)
3. Called away shares at $46 strike purchased at $45.87 = $26 profit on stock plus $366 in premiums = $392 total on 200-share position
4. Assignment frees capital for redeployment—200 shares called away freed ~$9,000 for next investment
5. Covered call strategy: sell calls slightly OTM, wait for intra-week stock strength to maximize premium rather than selling immediately on down days
