## Summary
Kenneth documents his weekly covered call selling strategy across multiple stocks (HIMS, OLO) targeting $180/week in premium. He buys shares at market prices and immediately sells covered calls at or slightly above purchase prices, collecting premium regardless of assignment while capturing modest capital gains if assigned. This week he generated $642.67 in premium across three positions.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell covered calls at strike prices at or slightly above purchase price to enable assignment gains of $0.16-$0.32 per share while keeping premium
2. Check premium levels and underlying risk before selling—avoid writing calls when implied volatility spikes and risk increases
3. Premium collected is kept regardless of assignment outcome; unassigned shares can be re-sold with new covered calls the following week
4. At assignment on OLO: $255 total ($240 premium + $15 capital gain on 100 shares purchased at $56.85, sold at $57)
5. HIMS position 1 (100 shares held from June 30, ~$50 cost basis): sold a breakeven $50-strike call for $80 premium, no capital gain if assigned
6. HIMS position 2 (200 shares purchased same day at $47.84): sold $48-strike calls for $322.67 premium; if assigned, +$32 capital gain ($0.16/share x 200) for $354.67 total
7. Weekly premium target: $180 across multiple positions; actual result $642.67 across all 3 trades (OLO $255 + HIMS pos.1 $80 + HIMS pos.2 $354.67 if fully assigned) — 356% of target
8. Diversify across multiple stocks and positions rather than single holding to reduce concentration risk
