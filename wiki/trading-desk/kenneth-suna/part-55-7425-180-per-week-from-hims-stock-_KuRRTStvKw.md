## Summary
Kenneth Suna demonstrates a covered call wheel strategy on HIMS and Nphase stocks, selling weekly calls at predetermined strike prices to generate consistent premium income. He earned $68 this week ($24 from HIMS, $44 from Nphase) on a $13,018 deployed capital base, emphasizing the trade-off between guaranteed premium and potential upside capture.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell covered calls at strike prices you're willing to sell shares at (HIMS: bought at $50, sold calls at $50 strike)
2. Accept partial gains rather than maximum gains; 'your goal is not to make all of the money, just some of it'
3. Know your break-even: calculate both premium gain AND stock gain/loss if assigned (e.g., Nphase position: $144 premium + $20.34 stock gain = $164.34 total gain)
4. If shares fall below purchase price, selling higher strike calls risks assignment at a loss (HIMS now at $47 vs. bought at $50: $49 strike could lock in a loss)
5. Target weekly premium of $180+ on a $50k portfolio; adjust strike selection based on current price and desired assignment probability
6. Roll calls weekly if not assigned to compound premium income across the same shares
7. Deploy capital systematically: ~$13k capital generated $68 premium this week (~0.52% weekly return)
