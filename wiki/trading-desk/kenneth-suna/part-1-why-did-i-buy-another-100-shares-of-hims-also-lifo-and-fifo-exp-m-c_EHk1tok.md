## Summary
Kenneth explains his covered call strategy on HIMS stock, buying additional 100-share lots to protect long-term positions from assignment while selling weekly calls. He details the LIFO vs FIFO lot selection method on Charles Schwab to ensure newer positions get called away instead of profitable older positions.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell covered calls one strike price out of the money to cap upside but generate premium
2. Use LIFO (Last In First Out) lot selection instead of default FIFO to protect oldest, most profitable positions from assignment
3. Expects $170/week per covered call contract on HIMS in current high-volatility environment (~$600-700/month potential)
4. If assigned, repurchase shares immediately and resume weekly covered call selling on same stock
5. Recognize covered call upside is capped at strike price; if stock rallies above strike, shares are called away at that level
6. Monitor volatility: premium generation ($170/week) depends on high volatility; when volatility drops, premiums may fall to $50/week or less
7. Can buy back options contract early if stock skyrockets to avoid unwanted assignment, though may incur loss on the contract
