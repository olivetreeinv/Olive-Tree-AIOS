## Summary
Kenneth Suna explains the covered call repair strategy for managing underwater covered call positions. When the underlying stock declines below the original strike price, traders can sell lower-strike covered calls to generate premium while waiting for recovery, and roll those contracts weekly to higher strikes as the stock rebounds, offsetting losses and lowering cost basis.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell covered calls at strike prices lower than current stock price when stock tanks (e.g., buy at $55, stock drops to $40, sell $45 strike calls)
2. Avoid selling calls at very low strike prices (e.g., $41) because early assignment risk before expiration is too high
3. Rolling contracts requires buying back the expiring call and selling a new call at higher strike for next week
4. Premium earned from rolling can offset or completely erase the loss from buying back the original contract
5. When rolling, verify your brokerage app defaults to 'sell' not 'buy' for the new covered call leg
6. Check market price near market close on Friday; if stock likely closing above strike, roll to next week at higher strike price
