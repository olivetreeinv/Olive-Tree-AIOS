## Summary
Kenneth Suna demonstrates using stock screeners (Charles Schwab) to identify volatile stocks suitable for covered call selling. He filters by sector (Information Technology), price range ($20-60), optionability, short interest (10-20%), and beta (1-2) to narrow candidates from 1,200 stocks to 9, then shows how to evaluate premium income on specific strikes.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Filter stocks by sector and price range to reduce screener results (1,200 → 158)
2. Use beta of 1-2 to identify appropriate volatility for covered calls
3. Target short interest 10-20% to avoid excessive short squeeze risk
4. Sell weekly options expiring Friday for covered calls
5. Go slightly out-of-the-money (e.g., $0.26-0.76 above current price) to capture $120-140 premium per contract on $5,000 position
6. High volatility sectors (AI, cybersecurity, healthcare) offer better premiums than consumer staples
7. Kenneth generates ~$600/week from covered call premium selling
8. Verify optionability filter to ensure stocks support options trading
