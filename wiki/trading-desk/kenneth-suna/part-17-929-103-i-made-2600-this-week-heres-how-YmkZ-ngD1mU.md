## Summary
Kenneth documents his week trading covered calls across 6 stocks with $64,881 deployed, generating $2,647 in net premium (4% return). Five positions were assigned at expiration while HIMS declined, leaving $23,420 tied up—illustrating both the income consistency and capital lock-up risks of the covered call wheel strategy.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Target stocks with 100-share minimum positions (1 contract = 100 shares)
2. Accept assignment as normal outcome; capital gets returned and redeployed
3. Stock appreciation beyond strike price means missed upside but consistent premium income—'the goal isn't to make all of the money, just some of it'
4. Multiple positions per week (5-6 stocks) provides diversification and consistent income vs. timing single home runs
5. Recognize downside: capital can be tied up for extended periods if shares don't clear (HIMS locked $23,420 after dropping from $58 to $52)
6. Weekly format allows flexibility to adjust subsequent week selling if mid-week stock recovery occurs
7. Frame strategy as generating predictable passive income where 'you know what you're going to make ahead of time' vs. day/swing trading
