## Summary
Kenneth demonstrates executing the covered call wheel strategy on HIM and NPhase stocks to generate weekly premium income. He shows multiple entry points, strike selection logic, and premium collection of $540 in one week across three positions, explaining assignment scenarios and profit outcomes.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell covered calls 1 week out when entering position late in trading week to optimize premium collection
2. Strike selection: sell calls at or very close to purchase price ($50 strike on $50 HIM purchase, $41.50 strike on $41.50 NPhase purchase) to maximize premium while accepting assignment
3. Premium collection prioritized over capital gains: $24-$193 premium per call is the primary profit target, not stock appreciation
4. Assignment outcome math: retain all premiums regardless of assignment; if assigned, realize small profit ($33-$34) plus premium; if not assigned, keep shares plus premium ($370+ total)
5. Buy dips opportunistically within same strategy: re-buy HIM at $50 after assignment, buy NPhase again at $38.66 after initial drop for another call sale
6. Target weekly premium income of $540 minimum across portfolio ($180/week stated goal appears conservative relative to actual results)
7. Multiple simultaneous positions on different stocks compress timeline and reduce single-stock risk while compounding premium income
