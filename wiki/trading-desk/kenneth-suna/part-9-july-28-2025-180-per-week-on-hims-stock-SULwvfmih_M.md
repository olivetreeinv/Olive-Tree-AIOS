## Summary
Kenneth demonstrates his $180/week HIMS covered call strategy during a volatile earnings week. He discusses position sizing discipline, avoiding high-risk earnings plays despite premium temptation, and executing trades across HIMS, QuantumCape, and Enphase with varying premium targets while managing portfolio risk.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Avoid selling covered calls on stocks with huge recent run-ups before earnings—premium is high but downside risk is severe
2. Position sizing: ease into unfamiliar stocks gradually (Kenneth bought 500 HIMS shares but only 500 QuantumCape shares pre-market at $11.47 due to unfamiliarity)
3. Accept lower premiums on defensive positions—Enphase trade netted only 2.7% but Kenneth prioritizes premium capture over upside participation
4. Target $180 premium per 100 shares per week on HIMS; Kenneth achieved $206 on 100-share equivalent (2 contracts × $416 ÷ 2)
5. Strike selection: sell calls 1-2 strikes above current price ($58 calls on $57.55 HIMS, $115 calls on $114.70 QuantumCape) for high probability assignment
6. Sit out high-risk weeks—Kenneth explicitly avoided earnings trades on Roblox ($12k capital for $760 premium), Robin Hood, Etsy, and SoFi despite attractive premiums
7. Portfolio allocation: Kenneth deployed $27,892 across 3 positions targeting 4%+ weekly return; previous positions expired Aug 1 rather than week-end Friday
