## Summary
Kenneth demonstrates his weekly covered call strategy, showing how he deployed $64,881 across multiple positions to generate $2,600 (4%) in premium income in half an hour. He walks through the mechanics of buying 100 shares and selling weekly covered calls using Charles Schwab's Think or Swim platform, using Snapchat ($8.40/share) as a low-cost example.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell covered calls on weekly expiration for recurring income
2. Minimum 100 shares required per covered call contract (1 contract = 100 shares)
3. Find stock candidates using CNBC market movers filtered by volatility, unusual volume, or most active
4. Use bid column in Think or Swim to sell covered calls at bid price
5. Calculate percentage gain on capital deployed: premium received ÷ stock cost × 100 (e.g., $19 premium on $839 cost = 2.26%)
6. Target 4% return weekly through multiple simultaneous positions ($64,881 deployed for $2,600 premium)
7. Lower-priced stocks like Snapchat ($840 total for 100 shares) are more accessible than expensive stocks ($23,000+)
