## Summary
Kenneth Suna reviews his worst week trading options, down to $1,142 from typical $2,500/week earnings. He demonstrates the covered call recovery strategy applied across multiple underwater positions (HIMS, D-Wave, Enphase, SoundHound, ELF) and emphasizes the critical risk of trading into earnings season, exemplified by an ELF position that collapsed 44% from $131 to $73.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Use covered call recovery strategy when stock drops significantly below purchase price to generate income while waiting for recovery
2. Never trade into earnings season due to extreme volatility and unpredictability of guidance impact, even if earnings beats expectations
3. Monitor earnings dates before entering positions—Kenneth missed ELF earnings check and lost $58/share ($131 to $73)
4. Current portfolio totals $68,184 across multiple positions accumulated over weeks/months
5. Typical weekly premium target is $2,500; worst week generated only $1,142
6. Strike prices sold typically 20-40% below current stock price (e.g., HIMS at $42, sold $50 strike; QuantumCape at $16.62, selling $17 strike)
7. Covered calls become problematic during earnings season when unrealized losses can suddenly become severe
