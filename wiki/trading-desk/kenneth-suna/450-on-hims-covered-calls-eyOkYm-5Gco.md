## Key Takeaways
1. Sell covered calls at strike prices just out-of-the-money (1-3% above current price) to maximize premium while accepting higher assignment probability
2. Use technical indicators (RSI, volume) and chart patterns to identify profit-taking zones after strong rallies as optimal entry points for selling calls
3. Recognize that selling covered calls on volatile stocks creates two competing risks: upside cap (if stock rallies hard) and share loss (if assigned)
4. Weekly expirations (Friday expiration dates) allow faster premium decay and frequent re-positioning opportunities
5. Maintain a two-tier position structure: core shares (long-term holdings) vs. flexible shares (tactical call-selling candidates)
6. If unwilling to lose shares or cap upside, simply do not sell covered calls—the strategy fundamentally requires accepting these outcomes
7. Further out-of-the-money strikes (e.g., 34 vs. 32) reduce assignment likelihood but collect less premium, requiring a conscious trade-off decision
