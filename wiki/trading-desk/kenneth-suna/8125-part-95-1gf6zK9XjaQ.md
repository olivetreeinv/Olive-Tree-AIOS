## Summary
Kenneth reviews his weekly covered call portfolio ($1,100-$1,400/week target) with mixed results: profitable on HIMS but significant losses on QuantumCape and NPhase. He emphasizes that covered calls carry real downside risk beyond capped upside, and warns viewers against blindly copying his positions without proper risk tolerance and experience.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Initial position sizing: Only allocate capital you're comfortable risking (e.g., $5,700 for QuantumCape despite 8% premium)
2. Strike selection must be realistic: QuantumCape at $11.50 strike became worthless out-of-money when stock dropped to $8.26
3. Premium targets: $1,100-$1,400/week on portfolio (up from initial $180/week HIMS goal)
4. Weekly expiration preference to avoid capital tie-up (avoid 2-week expirations when possible)
5. Real downside risk exists despite premium collection (15 years experience needed to manage $15k in concurrent risky positions)
6. Monitor during-week volatility: Buy back losing covered calls or sell stock at loss if thesis breaks, don't just hold
7. Post losses on QuantumCape and NPhase tied up ~$15k in capital while waiting for potential rebounds
