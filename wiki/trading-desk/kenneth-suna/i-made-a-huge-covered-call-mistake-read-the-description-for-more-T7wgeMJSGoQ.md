## Summary
Kenneth Suna demonstrates the risks of selling cash-secured puts into earnings using a HIMS trade that went against him. He illustrates the decision framework when assignment is at risk: either buy back the contract and exit for a loss, or hold for assignment at the original strike price.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Never trade into earnings volatility—too risky and unpredictable
2. When selling cash-secured puts, you must reserve the full strike price × shares in cash (e.g., $17,100 for 300 shares at $57 strike)
3. If assigned, you're forced to buy at strike price regardless of current market price—if HIMS is $51 but you sold $57 puts, you buy at $57
4. Buying back an options contract to exit early locks in losses but may be worth it if conviction changes or downside risk is severe
5. Assignment losses can exceed premium collected—he collected $814 but could lose $17,100 in capital if assigned and stock continues falling
6. The wheel strategy (covered calls + cash-secured puts) is not risk-free and experienced traders make mistakes trading into earnings
