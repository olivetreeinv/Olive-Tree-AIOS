## Summary
Kenneth Suna demonstrates selling covered calls on Broadcom to generate premium income (~$620 per two-day cycle) to fund a vacation trip, targeting five cycles to cover the cost. He buys 100 shares at $40,654 and repeatedly sells out-of-the-money calls at the 407.50 strike, exploiting Broadcom's biweekly expiration cycle.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell covered calls at the next price right out of the money (DITM by one strike)
2. Broadcom offers covered calls expiring every other day enabling rapid premium cycling
3. Each covered call contract on 100 shares generates ~$620 premium at current levels
4. Sell at the bid column (enter negative contract quantity)
5. Use Fibonacci retracements to identify support levels for optimal entry (e.g., 360 support on Broadcom)
6. Five cycles of $620 premium = $3,100 total trip funding target
7. Monitor market technicals; expect pullbacks after all-time highs in account or broader indices
