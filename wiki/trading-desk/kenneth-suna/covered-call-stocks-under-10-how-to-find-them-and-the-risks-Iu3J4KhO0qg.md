## Summary
Kenneth Suna demonstrates how to find sub-$10 stocks suitable for covered call selling using stock screeners, using Xerox as a case study. He emphasizes the elevated risks inherent in low-priced stocks, including extreme volatility, potential 25%+ weekly drops, and asymmetric payoff structures where small premiums ($15 per contract on a $524 investment = 2.8%) are offset by catastrophic downside risk.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Screen for optionable stocks under $10 with price range 0-$10
2. Filter for tech sector (higher volatility)
3. Select short interest 10-20% or 20-30% (avoid >30%)
4. Use beta screening 1-2 (stocks more volatile than overall market)
5. Target nearest strike price to current stock price for income generation
6. Accept capped upside (e.g., Xerox $6 strike caps gains if stock rallies to $11)
7. Recognize asymmetric risk: small premium ($15) vs large potential loss ($2,400 on $524 investment)
