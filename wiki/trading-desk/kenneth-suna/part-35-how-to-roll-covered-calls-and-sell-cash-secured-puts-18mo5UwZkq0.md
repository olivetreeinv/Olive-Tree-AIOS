## Summary
Kenneth Suna demonstrates rolling covered calls and evaluates whether to sell cash-secured puts on HIMS stock. He decides against rolling (to avoid eating into the $25 premium) and against selling a cash-secured put (gap risk too high relative to $188 premium), instead planning to repurchase shares Monday and sell a new covered call at a higher strike.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Rolling a covered call means buying back the contract and selling a new one for a later week—use this when you want to keep shares and have a low cost basis with big tax consequences
2. A cash-secured put requires capital equal to 100 shares × strike price (e.g., $5,700 for a $57 strike on HIMS at $63)
3. Put premium is typically lower than call premium on the same stock at similar strikes
4. Gap risk at market open (stock could open well below strike over a weekend) can erase multiple weeks of premium—skip the trade if the risk-to-reward doesn't justify it
5. When assignment is likely on a covered call, compare the net profit (strike gain + premium) versus opportunity cost if stock rallies past strike
6. For a $50k portfolio targeting $1,250/mo premium, prioritize high-probability near-the-money strikes over long-shot out-of-the-money strikes with low premium
