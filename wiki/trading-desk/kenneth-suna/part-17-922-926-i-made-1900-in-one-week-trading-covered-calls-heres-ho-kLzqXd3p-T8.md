## Summary
Kenneth demonstrates a $1,900 weekly return using covered calls across multiple stocks (HIMS, D-Wave, SoundHound, Marin, APLD), emphasizing that the strategy's goal is capturing some premium rather than maximizing directional gains. He illustrates disciplined position management, including handling assignment and redeploying capital.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. The covered call goal is to make some of the money, not all of it—accept partial profits rather than chase full upside
2. Minimum capital requirement is $10,000 to buy 100 shares of a $100 stock to sell 1 covered call contract
3. Weekly covered call returns of $454–$631 per position are achievable on $17,000–$40,000 deployments (roughly 1.1%–4.3% per week)
4. When shares are assigned, immediately redeploy capital to new covered call positions the following trading day
5. Even significant unexpected upside (MARIN from $40 to $50) can coexist with successful covered call execution if strike is set below the spike
6. Do not try to predict or time explosive stock moves; focus on consistent premium collection across multiple positions
7. Holding losing positions (QuantumCape from $11.40 to $7) and selling covered calls against them can recover losses when stock rebounds
