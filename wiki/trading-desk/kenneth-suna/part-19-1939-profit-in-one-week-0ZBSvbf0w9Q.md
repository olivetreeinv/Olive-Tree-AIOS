## Summary
Kenneth reviews his weekly covered call results across 6 positions, netting $1,939 profit (3.2% return on $59.8k deployed). Key lesson: HIMS stock became too volatile for covered calls after CEO insider selling caused a 15% drop; he plans to switch to swing trading HIMS instead while continuing the wheel strategy on other positions.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Exit covered call positions immediately when IV spikes dramatically—HIMS premiums jumped to $535/contract (from $317 two hours earlier) representing $2,100 vs $317 missed profit
2. CEO insider share sales typically signal liquidity needs (yachts, real estate) not company deterioration—stock usually recovers after the panic sell-off
3. Abandon covered calls on stocks with realized volatility >15% daily swings; redeploy capital to swing trading instead for better risk-adjusted returns
4. Track portfolio turnover: 6 positions rotated weekly, 2 assigned (Soundhound, D-Wave), 2 rolled (QuantumCape, Nphase), 2 unassigned (Oscar, HIMS)
5. Set strict position size: 400 shares = 4 covered call contracts per position to standardize premium collection
6. Accept sub-optimal exits ($317 on HIMS vs potential $2,100) as cost of not timing tops—this is unavoidable in options selling
