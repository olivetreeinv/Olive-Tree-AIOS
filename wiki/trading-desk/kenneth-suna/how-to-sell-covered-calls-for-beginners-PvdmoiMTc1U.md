## Summary
Kenneth Suna demonstrates how to sell covered calls for beginners using Rivian as an example, walking through buying 100 shares ($1,218) and immediately selling a weekly call contract to generate premium income (~$16 per week, or 1.5% weekly return). The video covers the mechanics of the trade, including assignment risks, capped upside, downside exposure, and tax implications.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell weekly contracts for consistent income generation
2. Buy 100 shares first (one contract = 100 shares)
3. Strike selection: use strikes where assignment occurs if stock closes at or above strike price on expiration
4. Premium collected is kept regardless of assignment outcome
5. Expect ~1.5% weekly return on capital as realistic for lower-priced stocks like Rivian (~$12)
6. Cap profits at strike price (e.g., $12.50 strike = $16 max profit on $1,218 investment)
7. Short-term capital gains tax treatment applies to premium; consult accountant on tax reserve requirements
