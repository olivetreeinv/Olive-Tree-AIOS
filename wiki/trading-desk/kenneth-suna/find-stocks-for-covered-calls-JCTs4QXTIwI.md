## Summary
Kenneth teaches a systematic method for finding stocks suitable for covered call selling by cherry-picking mid-cap growth ETF holdings, calculating premium-to-share-price ratios, and identifying stocks that are affordable yet generate meaningful premiums. The core principle is seeking volatile stocks with high premiums while avoiding mega-cap names like Apple and Google that are expensive and low-premium.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Target stocks with 1-3% weekly premium yield (divide premium by share price, multiply by 100)
2. Anything over 3% premium indicates high-risk/unusual activity (earnings, etc.)
3. Sell covered calls at out-of-the-money strike prices for highest premium possible
4. Require minimum 100 shares per contract to execute covered calls
5. Use mid-cap growth ETFs as sourcing tool, cherry-pick top 10 holdings for individual stock research
6. Avoid Magnificent Seven (Google, Nvidia, Apple, Meta) - too expensive per share, low premiums
7. Select stocks with lower share price (<$1,500 per 100 shares) for retail accessibility
