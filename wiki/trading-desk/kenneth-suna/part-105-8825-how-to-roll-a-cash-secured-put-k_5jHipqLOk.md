## Summary
Kenneth demonstrates how to roll cash-secured puts using HIMS as a real example, showing the buy-to-close and sell-to-open process to avoid unfavorable assignment. He turned a potential $17,100 loss into a $98 net loss by rolling the position one week further out at a different strike price.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Rolling CSP involves two steps: buy-to-close the existing position, then sell-to-open a new series one week further out
2. Can roll to a higher strike price if bullish on the stock (HIMS example: rolled from $57 to $52 strike)
3. Rolling premium ($985 in HIMS case) helps offset the buy-to-close loss ($1,897)
4. Net loss on HIMS roll was $98 ($814 initial premium - $1,897 buy-to-close + $985 new premium), vastly better than taking assignment at $57 when stock was at $51-52
5. Avoid selling CSP into earnings events due to high volatility risk
6. Use roll functionality in brokerage platform: click 'roll selected' to access buy-to-close and sell-to-open in one workflow
