## Summary
Kenneth demonstrates a systematic covered-call selling approach targeting $180/week premium on individual stocks (HIMS, OLO, JOBY, Oscar Health). He achieved $1,192 in premiums on $25,930 deployed capital (4.6% weekly return) while emphasizing the strategy's income focus, position sizing discipline, and risks around earnings events and assignment.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Target $180-200 per week in premium per covered-call position as attainable goal (vs. unsustainable claims of thousands/week)
2. Sell the highest premium strike price even if slightly out-of-money (e.g., sold $49.50 strike on HIMS bought at $49.57 for 7-cent loss per share acceptable for premium maximization)
3. Scale into new positions smaller initially (e.g., $2,100 on new Oscar Health vs. $15,000 on established HIMS) until familiar with stock behavior
4. Use 1-2 week expirations (weekly or monthly) to maintain flexibility and compound premium income
5. Avoid wheel strategy on volatile stocks showing pullback risk (e.g., JOBY up to 52-week highs, avoided CSP to prevent capital lockup on $14-15 pullback scenario)
6. Do not sell covered calls on stocks you intend to hold long-term; maintain separate core positions without call obligations
7. Accept assignment risk and don't buy back calls early unless forced to preserve long-term position, as buyback losses can be significant
