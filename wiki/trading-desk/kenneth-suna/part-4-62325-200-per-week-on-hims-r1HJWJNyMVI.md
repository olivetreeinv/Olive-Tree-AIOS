## Summary
Kenneth demonstrates a covered call strategy on HIMS stock following a 30% crash, buying 100 shares at $46 and immediately selling a $48 strike call for $2.05 premium ($200/week). He explains why he avoided selling cash-secured puts during volatility and outlines decision rules for managing positions when stock price moves below entry cost.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sold covered call at $48 strike for $2.05 premium ($200/week target) on 100-share position
2. Bought shares at $46 after previous assignment at $58 (captured $200 premium + $200 stock gain = $400 total)
3. Avoid selling cash-secured puts during major negative catalysts (downgrade, partnership end) to prevent forced assignment at bad prices
4. If stock drops below covered call strike at expiration, wait for stock to recover above entry ($46) before selling next call, or sell lower strike ($44) accepting assignment loss risk
5. Strike selection rule: only sell call strikes above entry cost to avoid losses on assignment
