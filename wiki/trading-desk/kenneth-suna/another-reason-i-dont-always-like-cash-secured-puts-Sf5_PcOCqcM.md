## Summary
Kenneth Suna explains why he avoids selling cash-secured puts using HIMS stock as an example. He demonstrates that while a $54 strike put on HIMS offers $115 in premium, the inability to exercise early and the obligation to wait until Friday expiration creates opportunity cost—missing chances to buy at better prices mid-week if the stock rebounds. He prefers keeping cash available for flexible entry points rather than locking in small premiums.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Cash-secured puts cannot be exercised early; must wait until Friday expiration date
2. $115 premium on HIMS $54 put strike requires $5,400 cash tied up
3. Opportunity cost example: stock drops to $52 Wednesday but rebounds to $57 Friday—miss better entry point while earning only $115
4. Use Fibonacci retracements to identify potential pullback targets ($54 level on HIMS)
5. Keep cash available in account for flexible buying power at optimal prices throughout the week, not locked into Friday close
