## Summary
Kenneth documents his attempt to generate $180/week from covered calls on HIMS stock, which failed to meet the target. He discusses the trade-off between strike price selection and premium collection, ultimately securing $420 in premium across two positions (one original 100-share position generating $54, and a new 200-share position generating $366) with potential total profit of $446 if assigned by July 11th expiration.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell calls at $1 out-of-the-money (ATM or near-ATM) to maximize premium rather than selling further OTM where premiums decrease
2. Initial HIMS position: bought 100 shares at $50, sold $50 strike call for $54 premium when stock dropped to $47
3. When stock declines below purchase price, selling deeper OTM calls (e.g., $48 strike) creates larger losses on assignment ($2 loss on $50 buy/$48 sale) despite higher premiums—avoid this trade-off
4. Added 200 shares of HIMS at $45, sold two calls at $46 strike for $366 premium, targeting $392-$446 total profit if assigned by July 11
5. Premium focus over delta/Greeks: Kenneth prioritizes collecting premium dollars rather than analyzing Greeks or delta targets
