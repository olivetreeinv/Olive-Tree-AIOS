## Summary
Kenneth Suna explains covered call mechanics using Skittles as a visual aid for a stock portfolio. He demonstrates selling OTM covered calls on Broadcom at $315 strike (vs $313 current price) expiring Friday April 10th, collecting $525/contract premium ($1,600 for 3 contracts), with the tradeoff of capped upside if stock rises above strike versus keeping shares and premium if it stays below.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell covered calls at the next strike price OTM (e.g., $315 strike when stock at $313) to maximize premium while accepting assignment risk
2. Adjust contract quantity down if unwilling to part with all shares (e.g., sell 3 contracts for 300 shares instead of maximum available)
3. Premium collected per contract example: $525/contract × 3 contracts = $1,600 per week on Broadcom trade
4. Accept tradeoff: keep both shares AND premium if stock closes below strike; lose shares but keep premium + profit if stock rises above strike
5. Can repeat covered call sales weekly on shares not assigned, building consistent premium income
6. Use options chain expiration dates close to current date (e.g., Friday expirations on Monday filming) for faster premium decay
7. OTM covered calls allow upside participation up to the strike price, balancing premium income with some growth potential
