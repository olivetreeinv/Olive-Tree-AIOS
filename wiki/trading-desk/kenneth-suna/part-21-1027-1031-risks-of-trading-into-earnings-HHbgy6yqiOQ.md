## Summary
Kenneth discusses the risks of trading into earnings weeks, using his portfolio as a case study. He demonstrates how major earnings announcements (HIMS, D-Wave, etc.) created significant volatility that hurt several positions, and emphasizes the importance of considering whether to sit out high-volatility earnings weeks rather than blindly entering trades.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Avoid or reduce trading activity during major earnings weeks when multiple big-name companies report simultaneously
2. Recognize that covered calls can trap you in losing positions—Kenneth has $23,423 stuck in HIMS at $58 cost basis (now $45-46) and cannot sell calls profitably
3. When stock drops significantly below your cost basis, covered call premium becomes too small to justify the risk
4. Monitor earnings dates on your positions (HIMS Monday, D-Wave Thursday, Oscar Health, etc.) to anticipate volatility spikes
5. Consider waiting until mid-week (Tuesday/Wednesday) after earnings volatility settles before selling new covered calls for better premiums
6. Earnings can cause sharp directional moves (ELF: $131 to $122, HIMS: $58 to $45-46) that wipe out premium gains
7. Accept opportunity cost: liquidity from car sale ($12k) should have stayed in cash or deployed cautiously rather than chasing ELF on an earnings-volatile week
