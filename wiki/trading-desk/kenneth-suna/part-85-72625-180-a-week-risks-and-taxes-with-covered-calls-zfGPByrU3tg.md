## Summary
Kenneth documents a weekly covered call trading session generating $1,454 profit (4.6% return) on a $31,400 deployed capital across HIMS, OLO, and Oscar Health positions. He addresses two critical risks: forced stock ownership during downturns and capped upside potential, plus taxation implications of short-term capital gains from weekly expiration cycles.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Weekly covered calls target ~$180 profit per week with Friday or 2-week expirations
2. Deploy 100-300 shares per position to generate $300-$500 premium per contract
3. Accept capped upside (e.g., OLO skyrocketed to $77 but shares called away at $68.88 strike, limiting profit to 4% vs. potential 11%)
4. Extended expirations (2 weeks vs. 1 week) can generate higher premiums—compare both before selling
5. Short-term capital gains tax is cost of doing business; set aside ~22% of profits in HYSA to cover tax liability
6. If stock drops after purchase, can either buy call back and sell stock at loss, or hold and collect premium—requires comfort owning underlying
7. Risk: Must be willing to own the stock if assigned; if you wouldn't hold the stock long-term, don't sell covered calls on it
