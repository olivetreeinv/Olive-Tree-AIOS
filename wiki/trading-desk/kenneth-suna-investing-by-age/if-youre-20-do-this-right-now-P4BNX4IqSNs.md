## Summary
Kenneth Suna discusses the importance of starting retirement investing early, using S&P 500 ETF examples showing that investing $100/month from age 20 yields $637,000 by age 60 versus $228,000 starting at age 30. This is general financial advice about long-term wealth building, not actionable options trading or premium-selling tactics.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Starting at age 20 with $100/month invested in S&P 500 ETF yields $637,000 by retirement at 60
2. Starting at age 30 with same $100/month yields only $228,000 by retirement at 60
3. 10-year difference in start time creates $409,000+ difference in retirement portfolio
4. Compound growth makes early investing exponentially more valuable than delayed investing
