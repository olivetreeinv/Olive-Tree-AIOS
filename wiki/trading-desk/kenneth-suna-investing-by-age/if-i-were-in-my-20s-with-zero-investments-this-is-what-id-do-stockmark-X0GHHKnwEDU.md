## Summary
Kenneth Suna provides long-term retirement investment advice for someone in their 20s with no investments, focusing on Roth IRA account setup, S&P 500 index funds, dividend ETFs, tech ETFs, and REITs. This is foundational wealth-building education rather than options trading or premium-income strategies.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Open a Roth IRA as the account structure (not the investment itself)
2. Invest $250/month into S&P 500 Index Fund for ~$1M in 40 years at ~9% average annual return (accounting for inflation)
3. S&P 500 Index Fund is the foundation with ~150% returns over 10 years and low expense ratios
4. Consider dividend ETFs (~4% yield with 11 years of dividend growth) for supplemental allocation
5. Consider tech-focused ETFs like VGT (~400% returns over 10 years, 99% technology holdings) for growth exposure when young
6. Consider real estate investment trusts (REITs) for real estate exposure and monthly dividend payments
7. Max Roth IRA contribution is $6,500/year; open individual taxable account for amounts exceeding this limit
