## Summary
Kenneth Suna discusses long-term wealth-building strategies for people in their 20s, emphasizing the importance of opening both Roth IRA and individual brokerage accounts rather than relying solely on retirement accounts. He recommends portfolio allocation across tech funds, S&P funds, dividend funds, and individual stocks but explicitly states these are not financial advice and are only loose guidelines.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Open both a Roth IRA and an individual brokerage account in your 20s to avoid being financially trapped at 40
2. Keep Roth IRA untouched until age 59.5; use individual brokerage account for near-term purchases in your 40s-50s
3. Allocate tech funds to Roth IRA for tax-advantaged growth, and split S&P 500 funds, dividend funds, and individual stocks in taxable brokerage account
4. Most common financial mistakes by age 40: not investing in 20s, investing incorrectly with only retirement accounts, overspending on luxury items and lifestyle inflation
