## Summary
Kenneth shares a personal anecdote about meeting a 64-year-old man with no investments who regrets not starting earlier, contrasted with a 74-year-old who retired at 54 through long-term investing. The core message is that investing is a long-term wealth-building tool, not a get-rich-quick scheme, and starting early is critical to financial security.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Start investing as early as possible; the 74-year-old retired at 54 by investing long-term from a young age
2. Investing builds wealth over decades, not days or months; it is not a vehicle for immediate money-making
3. Do not expect to become a millionaire tomorrow through investing; that is not the purpose of market participation
4. Avoid being the 64-year-old with no savings and no investments who desperately wishes to retire but has no assets
5. Build investments alongside other income sources rather than relying on investments alone for immediate cash needs
