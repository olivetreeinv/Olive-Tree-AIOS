## Summary
Kenneth Suna discusses long-term index fund investing and retirement planning by comparing investment outcomes starting at age 20 versus age 40. The video emphasizes compound growth over decades using S&P 500 index funds with a 10% average annual return, showing that starting early reduces required monthly contributions significantly ($250/month from age 20 vs. $1,600/month from age 40 to reach $1M by age 60).

---

## Key Takeaways
1. S&P 500 average annual return since 1957 is approximately 10% after adjusting for inflation
2. Starting at age 20 with $250/month invested for 40 years yields $1.4 million
3. Starting at age 40 requires $1,600/month for 20 years to accumulate $1 million by age 60
4. After 10 years of monthly $250 investments starting at age 20, portfolio reaches $51,000
5. After 20 years of monthly $250 investments starting at age 20, portfolio reaches $182,000
6. After 30 years of monthly $250 investments starting at age 20 (reaching age 50), portfolio exceeds $500,000
