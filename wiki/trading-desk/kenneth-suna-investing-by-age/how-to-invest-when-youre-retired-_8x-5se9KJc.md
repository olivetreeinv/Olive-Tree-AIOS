## Summary
Kenneth Suna discusses retirement portfolio allocation strategy focusing on ETF diversification (60% growth: S&P 500, dividends, tech; 40% preservation: bonds, treasuries, CDs, I-bonds, cash). The content is retirement planning and asset allocation education, not options trading or income strategies.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Shift mindset from wealth-building to wealth-preservation in retirement
2. Allocate approximately 30% to S&P 500 ETFs, 20% to dividend ETFs, 10% to technology ETFs for growth portion
3. Remaining 40% of portfolio should go to safer investments like bonds, treasuries, CDs, and cash
4. Consider BND (total bond ETF at 3.7% yield), TLT (20-year Treasury at 4.27%), SHV (0-3 month Treasury at 4.27%)
5. I-bonds offer inflation adjustment with typical 1-year lock-up available through treasurydirect.gov or BlackRock ETFs like IBTG (2026 maturity at 4.05% yield)
6. Money market funds (e.g., SWVXX for Charles Schwab) or high-yield savings accounts for cash allocation
7. Personal rule: hold zero bonds until late 60s or early 70s, rejecting the 'invest your age in bonds' Wall Street saying
