## Summary
Kenneth Suna discusses portfolio rebalancing strategy as investors approach retirement, advocating for a gradual shift from growth stocks to bonds and dividend ETFs. While focused on long-term asset allocation rather than active options trading, the content addresses portfolio construction and risk management principles relevant to managing the Premium Desk's capital preservation goals.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Rebalance away from growth stocks starting 10 years before target retirement date
2. At ages 20-40: 50% growth tech, 30% S&P 500, 20% dividends, 0% bonds
3. At ages 40-50: 40% growth tech, 30% S&P 500, 20% dividends, 10% bonds
4. At ages 50+: 20% growth tech, 20% S&P 500, 30% dividends, 30% bonds
5. Avoid target-date funds with expense ratios over 1% due to underperformance
6. Individual brokerage accounts enable early retirement strategies vs. locked 401k/IRA accounts
7. Bond and high-quality dividend allocations provide portfolio stability during recessions
