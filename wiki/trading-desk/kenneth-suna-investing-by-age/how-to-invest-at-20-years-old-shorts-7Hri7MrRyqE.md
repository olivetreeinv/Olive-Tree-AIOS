## Summary
Kenneth Suna discusses long-term asset allocation strategies for investors aged 20-50, recommending age-based portfolio splits (e.g., 50% tech/30% S&P 500/20% dividends for ages 20-40) and gradual bond additions as retirement approaches. The content focuses on buy-and-hold investing philosophy and psychological resilience during market downturns, not options selling or premium generation strategies relevant to Brian Norton's Premium Desk operation.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Ages 20-40: target 50% technology, 30% S&P 500, 20% dividend ETF allocation
2. Ages 40-50: reduce technology to 40%, maintain S&P 500 and dividend ETF, add 10% bonds
3. Ages 50+: shift heavier into bonds as retirement approaches
4. Place highest-growth volatile assets (tech) in Roth IRAs to minimize tax liability
5. During market downturns, dollar-cost-average incrementally rather than deploying full capital at once
6. Historical pattern: markets recover from all recessions within 1-10 years; previous recoveries support this thesis
