## Summary
Kenneth Suna provides long-term investment guidance for 16-year-olds, recommending custodial and Roth IRA accounts with a diversified allocation across technology funds (50%), S&P 500 index funds (30%), and dividend funds (20%). The video emphasizes foundational passive investing principles and tax-advantaged accounts rather than active trading or options strategies.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Open custodial account (transfers at age 18) for taxable investing
2. Max out Roth IRA contribution at $7,000/year when employed
3. Allocate 50% technology (FTECH), 30% S&P 500 (SPLG), 20% dividends (SCHD) in early years
4. Reinvest dividends over time for compound growth
5. Shift from growth to dividends/bonds allocation starting at age 50
6. Technology funds suitable for long-term horizons despite short-term volatility when young
