## Summary
Kenneth Suna discusses portfolio rebalancing strategies based on age, recommending gradual shifts from growth assets (tech, S&P 500) toward bonds and dividends as investors approach retirement. The video focuses on long-term asset allocation and tax-advantaged account placement rather than options trading or income generation strategies.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Ages 20-40: 50% technology, 30% S&P 500, 20% dividends
2. Ages 40-50: 40% technology, 30% S&P 500, 20% dividends, consider adding bonds
3. Ages 50+: 20% technology, 20% S&P 500, 30% dividends, 30% bonds
4. Place high-growth assets (tech) in Roth IRA to avoid tax bills on multi-hundred-percent gains over 30 years
5. Place bonds and dividends in individual taxable accounts
6. Rebalance slowly and gradually, not abruptly
7. By age 70, consider 50-60% bonds but maintain growth exposure
