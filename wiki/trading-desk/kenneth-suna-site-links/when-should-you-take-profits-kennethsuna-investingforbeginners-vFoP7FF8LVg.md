## Summary
Kenneth Suna discusses when to take profits on long-term stock investments, covering scenarios like life events (home purchase), portfolio rebalancing, trimming overweight positions, and managing gains before potential downturns. The content focuses on stock investing psychology and timing rather than options strategies.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. You don't have to sell 100% of holdings—can trim 25%, 50%, or 75% of shares selectively
2. Rebalancing involves trimming massive gains in growth stocks and shifting proceeds into safer investments like bonds, CDs, or dividend ETFs
3. When approaching retirement after 10-30 years of growth, consider moving from growth stocks to conservative positions
4. If a 1,000% gain target is hit in 3 years instead of 10, consider taking profits to pay down debt or major expenses
5. Selling to lock in gains before an expected recession triggers capital gains taxes and loses the original low cost basis—not ideal for 20-30 year hold plans
6. Time horizon matters: recession risk is irrelevant if your investment plan is 20-30 years; use downturns to average down instead
7. Life events (mortgages, debt, student loans) justify trimming long-term positions even if holdings are overweight
