## Summary
Kenneth compares money market funds versus high-yield savings accounts as cash storage vehicles. The video explains structural differences, yields (3.65-4.01%), fee structures, and safety considerations, but contains no options trading strategy or guidance relevant to Brian Norton's covered call and cash-secured put Premium Desk operation.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Money market funds typically yield slightly higher (4.01% at Schwab SWVXX) than HYSAs (3.65% at Marcus)
2. Money market fund expense ratios are extremely low; Schwab SWVXX charges 0.34%
3. Money market funds are not FDIC insured but backed by US government and considered among safest investments
4. HYSA rates can drop periodically via email; money market fund rates change faster due to short-term loan maturity cycles
5. Cannot cross-broker for money market funds (e.g., cannot buy Vanguard MMF in Schwab account); each brokerage has proprietary versions
6. Money market fund values tied to US dollar and do not fluctuate in price unless major crisis occurs
