## Summary
Kenneth Suna explains the three-fund portfolio approach for long-term investors seeking broad market exposure through ETFs in S&P 500, dividend, and growth categories, plus REITs as a fourth holding. This is foundational investing education unrelated to options selling or premium generation.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Expense ratios above 1% are considered very high; prioritize low-fee funds
2. Three-fund approach provides S&P 500, dividend, and growth stock exposure
3. Pick one ETF per category rather than multiple to reduce fee overlap
4. REITs serve as a fourth portfolio diversification element
5. Allocation percentages are flexible (e.g., 25% each or skewed by market outlook)
