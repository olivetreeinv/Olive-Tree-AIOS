## Summary
Kenneth Suna discusses age-based ETF allocation percentages (S&P 500, Tech, Dividend funds) for long-term buy-and-hold investing across different life stages (20s through 60s). This is foundational investment advice unrelated to options selling, premium collection, or active trading strategies.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Ages 20-30: can allocate 50% S&P 500, 30% Tech, 20% Dividends or more aggressive
2. Ages 30-40: maintain 20s allocation or shift to 50% S&P 500, 30% Tech, 20% Dividends
3. Ages 40-50: reduce tech exposure; allocate 45% Dividends, 40% S&P 500, 15% Tech due to shorter time horizon
4. Ages 50-60: prioritize capital preservation with 60% Dividends, 30% S&P 500, 10% Tech
5. Max out $7,000 Roth IRA contribution with highest-growth assets (tech) for tax efficiency
6. Use Roth IRA for retirement accounts (age 59.5+) and separate taxable account for dividend-producing assets
7. Start investing in 20s to benefit from compounding; late starts require significantly more capital in compressed timeframe
