## Summary
Kenneth Suna discusses whether you can outperform the S&P 500 by comparing historical returns across various ETFs (QQQ, SMH, XLK) and individual stocks (NVDA, AAPL, NFLX) over 5- and 10-year periods. The video argues that while some assets have dramatically outperformed the S&P 500, they may arrive too late in their growth cycles and lack the stability and consistent long-term returns that make the S&P 500 a preferred foundational holding.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. S&P 500 returned 133% in last 5 years and 176% in last 10 years as benchmark baseline
2. QQQ (tech-heavy) returned 170% in 5 years and 367% in 10 years
3. SMH (semiconductor ETF) returned 300% in 5 years and 736% in 10 years
4. NVDA returned 1,000% in 5 years and 23,000% in 10 years
5. Investors often arrive late to high-growth sectors after massive runs, missing early exponential gains
6. S&P 500 preferred as foundational holding due to decades of backtesting and peace of mind despite lower returns than some alternative assets
