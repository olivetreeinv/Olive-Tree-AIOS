## Summary
Kenneth Suna discusses long-term S&P 500 investment strategies for retirement accounts, focusing on lump-sum vs. dollar-cost averaging approaches for building wealth over 30-40 years. This is foundational personal finance advice unrelated to options selling or premium generation.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Lump-sum investing ($7,000/year into Roth IRA) beats dollar-cost averaging but both work
2. Dollar-cost averaging at $580/month over 30 years yields ~$1,000,000 with 9% real return
3. Waiting even 1-2 years to start investing materially reduces final portfolio value ($920k vs $1.1M)
4. Technology ETFs in tax-sheltered Roth IRA, S&P 500 and dividend funds in taxable individual accounts for tax efficiency
5. S&P 500 averages ~12% nominal return (~9% after inflation adjustment
6. Consistency matters more than timing; invest regardless of market levels (highs or lows)
