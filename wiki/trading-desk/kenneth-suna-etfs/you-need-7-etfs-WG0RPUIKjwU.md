## Summary
Kenneth Suna critiques the popular "7 ETFs" investment recommendation and explains why portfolio overlap through overlap-by-weight analysis leads to unnecessary redundancy. He advocates for streamlined portfolio construction using just 3 ETFs (VOO, FTEC, SCHD) with manageable overlap rather than collecting multiple funds with similar holdings.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Overlap-by-weight of 0-20% is good, 20-40% is moderate, 40-60% is high, 60%+ is very high/unnecessary
2. VOO vs QQQM vs VUG vs VGT all have 48-88% overlap—too much redundancy
3. Recommended portfolio: VOO (8% overlap with SCHD), FTEC (37% overlap with VOO, 3% overlap with SCHD), and SCHD
4. International ETF exposure is largely redundant because US large-cap companies generate substantial overseas revenue
5. Focus on understanding where your money is actually deployed rather than collecting ticker symbols
