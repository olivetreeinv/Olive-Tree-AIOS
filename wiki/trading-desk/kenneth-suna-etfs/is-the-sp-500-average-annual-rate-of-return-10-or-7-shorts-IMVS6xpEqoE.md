## Summary
Kenneth discusses whether the S&P 500's average annual return should be quoted as 10% or 7% (inflation-adjusted). He argues 10% is correct when measuring portfolio growth, while 7% applies only when discussing purchasing power. This is general investing education unrelated to options trading or the Premium Desk strategy.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. S&P 500 historical average annual return is approximately 10% before inflation adjustment
2. Portfolio compounds at pre-inflation returns, not inflation-adjusted returns
3. Inflation averages around 3% per year, causing prices to double roughly every 24 years
4. To maintain purchasing power over 30 years with 3% inflation, double the investment amount while the 10% return rate remains constant
