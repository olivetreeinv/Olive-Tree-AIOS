## Summary
Kenneth discusses retirement planning using the 4% rule and S&P 500 index fund investing, calculating how many years and monthly contributions are needed to reach $1.5M by age 60. This is general financial advice about long-term buy-and-hold investing, not options trading or premium income generation relevant to Brian Norton's Premium Desk strategy.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. 4% rule: withdraw 4% of portfolio annually in retirement without running out of money
2. $750/month into S&P 500 at 10% annual return for 30 years (age 30-60) yields ~$1.5M
3. $350/month into S&P 500 at 10% annual return for 40 years (age 20-60) yields ~$1.9M
4. Average retirement living costs estimated at $50k-$75k annually
5. Starting investments in your 20s requires half the monthly contribution of starting in your 30s
