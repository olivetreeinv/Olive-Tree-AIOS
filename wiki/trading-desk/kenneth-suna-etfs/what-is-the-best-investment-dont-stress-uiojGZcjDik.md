## Summary
Kenneth discusses ETF selection for long-term buy-and-hold investors experiencing analysis paralysis, comparing performance of dividend funds, S&P 500 index funds, tech funds (QQQ), total market funds (VTI), and REITs from 2011 onwards. The core message is that diversification across asset types matters more than picking the single 'best' fund, as all have appreciated significantly over the long term.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. All major ETF categories (dividend, S&P 500, tech, total market, REITs) have outperformed zero return over 10+ year periods
2. Diversification across 2-3 different investment types (e.g., one S&P, one tech, one dividend) reduces decision paralysis
3. Long-term performance differences between similar funds are less important than the fact that all appreciated—234% vs 300% both represent significant wealth building
