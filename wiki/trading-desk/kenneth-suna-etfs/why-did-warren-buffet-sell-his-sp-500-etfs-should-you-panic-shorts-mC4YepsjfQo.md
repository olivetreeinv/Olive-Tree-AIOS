## Summary
Kenneth Suna discusses Warren Buffett's sale of S&P 500 ETF positions, using it as a teaching moment about investor psychology and the importance of not panic-selling based on billionaire decisions. The video emphasizes that retail investors with long-term 30-year horizons should ignore short-term portfolio adjustments by institutional investors and maintain discipline with regular contributions.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. Don't panic-sell investments based on billionaire actions; Buffett's 43,000 shares of VO and 39,400 shares of SPY are negligible compared to his 300M Apple, 151M American Express, 680M Bank of America, and 400M Coca-Cola positions
2. Retail investors accumulating $300/month over 30 years benefit from dollar-cost averaging (buying at highs, lows, and everywhere in between)
3. Acknowledge your position: you are not a billionaire running a conglomerate actively trimming positions; stay disciplined with slow and steady accumulation
4. Don't make portfolio decisions based on interpreting what institutional investors know or signal through their 13F filings
