## Summary
Kenneth Suna discusses whether investors should reallocate portfolios to Chinese and Indian ETFs in anticipation of US economic decline. The analysis compares historical performance of international vs. US-based equity funds and concludes that betting against the US economy is not strategically sound for long-term investors.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. China's GXC (S&P equivalent) up 36% YTD but only 59% over 10 years vs S&P 500 up 228% in decade
2. China's ZQQ (QQQ equivalent) up 47% YTD but only 59% over 10 years vs QQQ up 451% in decade
3. India's NDY top 50 stocks up only 5% YTD and 94% over 10 years
4. India's digital technology fund down 4% YTD, only up 18% since 2022 launch
5. VXUS global fund up 28% YTD and 60% over 10 years as alternative to country-specific bets
6. Approximately 480 of 500 S&P companies generate 30-50% of revenue from overseas, providing international exposure without direct currency/regulatory risk
7. US dollar remains world reserve currency; historically people flee TO dollar during fear/recessions, not away from it
