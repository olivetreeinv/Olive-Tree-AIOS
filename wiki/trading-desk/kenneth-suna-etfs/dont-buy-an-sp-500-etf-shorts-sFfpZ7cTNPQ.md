## Summary
Kenneth Suna explains why buying an S&P 500 ETF is superior to manually purchasing 500 individual companies, addressing expense ratios, market-cap weighting, and operational complexity. This is general investment education rather than options trading strategy.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. S&P 500 ETF expense ratios are approximately 0.03%
2. Market-cap weighted funds allocate largest percentages to mega-cap stocks (Nvidia 7.73%, Apple 6.83%)
3. Manually replicating an S&P 500 index requires managing dividend taxes, fund rebalancing, and company additions/removals
4. Equal-weight approach (equal dollar allocation to all 500 companies) is inefficient because it overweights smaller, less-worthy companies
