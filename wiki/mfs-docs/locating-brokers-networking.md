## Summary
This document outlines nine strategies for building a commercial broker and investor network to generate both on-market and off-market multifamily deal flow. Methods range from passive (Crexi, LinkedIn) to active (direct-to-seller marketing, hiring an acquisitions analyst), with an emphasis on conference attendance, strategic partnerships, and relationship-building with equity and debt brokers as primary drivers of off-market opportunities.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Crexi is a prospecting tool, not just a listings tool** — use on-market listings to identify active brokers in target markets, then convert those relationships into off-market deal flow.
2. **VIP conference attendance pays dividends** — paying for premium tickets gets you into dinners and small-group sessions where the most active investors and brokers actually transact.
3. **Equity and debt brokers are underutilized deal sources** — commercial loan officers and capital raisers often know about off-market deals before they hit any platform; cultivating these relationships is high-leverage.
4. **Direct-to-seller marketing requires consistency** — guerrilla outreach only compounds with disciplined, repeated effort and a small support team.
5. **Partnerships unlock larger deals** — no solo operator can scale to 50+ unit acquisitions without co-investors; sourcing partners is as important as sourcing deals.

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| On-market deal | A property listed publicly on platforms like Crexi, LoopNet, or CoStar and available to any buyer. |
| Off-market deal | A property sold without public listing, typically through broker or owner relationships before it reaches a marketplace. |
| Direct-to-seller marketing | Outreach sent directly to property owners (mailers, cold calls, texts) to generate leads outside the broker system. |
| Acquisitions analyst | A hired team member responsible for sourcing, screening, and tracking deals; typically paid base salary plus deal commission. |
| Equity broker | A professional who sources and places passive equity capital into deals; often aware of off-market opportunities. |
| Debt broker | A commercial mortgage broker who arranges financing; frequently learns of off-market deals through lender relationships. |
| VIP ticket | Premium conference tier granting access to smaller networking dinners, speaker meet-and-greets, and deal rooms. |
| Crexi | Commercial Real Estate Exchange — a major online marketplace for listing and searching commercial properties. |
| Offerd | A platform offering direct-to-seller outreach and off-market deal sourcing services. |
| The Broker List | An online directory ([thebrokerlist.com](https://thebrokerlist.com)) of commercial real estate brokers searchable by market. |
