## Summary
This document provides a comprehensive due diligence framework for apartment acquisitions, covering four pre-contract touring criteria, a full physical inspection checklist requiring 2–5 days and seven specialist contractors, a financial due diligence checklist, and 25 targeted questions to ask the seller. It is designed to surface hidden liabilities — deferred maintenance, code violations, environmental hazards, and financial irregularities — before closing on a value-add multifamily deal.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Pre-contract touring has four lenses:** Location feel, personnel quality (staff/tenants/PM), operational systems/software, and competitive pricing — evaluate all four on every site visit before going under contract.
2. **Physical due diligence requires a full specialist team:** Seven contractors (inspector, roofer, pool, rehab contractor, termite, landscaper, HVAC) must all be scheduled within the 2–5 day inspection window — don't stagger them across weeks.
3. **Financial verification goes beyond the T-12:** Confirm bank deposits, RUBS billing, vendor contracts, all leases, and 24 months of work orders. Down units, loss to lease, bad debt, and non-paying tenants must all be independently verified — never accept seller's summary figures at face value.
4. **Pre-construction meeting before close is non-negotiable:** Have rehab scope and costs locked in prior to closing so Day 1 operations are ready to execute.
5. **Give management one week post-close to audit true vacancy:** The actual count of down/vacant units often differs from what the seller reports; don't rely on OM figures for your first-week operating plan.
6. **Seller questions surface capital risk:** Electrical panel brand (Federal Pacific / Stab-Lok), wiring type (aluminum vs. copper), plumbing material (galvanized vs. copper), and roof age/partial replacements are the highest-impact red flags that directly affect CapEx underwriting.
7. **Environmental and legal questions are required:** Lead paint, asbestos, mold history, flood zone / LOMA status, and radon violations must be asked explicitly — sellers are not required to volunteer this information.

## Action Items
- **Add the 7-contractor inspection team to Olive Tree's standard LOI-to-close timeline.** Block 2–5 days immediately after inspection period opens and pre-schedule all vendors at time of contract execution.
- **Build the 25 seller questions into Olive Tree's standard OM/seller Q&A template.** Send as a formal written request within 3 business days of going under contract.
- **Create a financial due diligence request list** based on the checklist items (T-12, utility bills, vendor contracts, leases, bank deposit verification, RUBS, work orders) and attach it to the Olive Tree LOI as a standard exhibit.
- **Flag Federal Pacific / Stab-Lok panels and aluminum wiring as automatic CapEx line items** in underwriting whenever seller confirms their presence — price in full panel replacement.
- **Verify flood zone status and request elevation certificates on every deal.** If no certificate exists, pull the flood policy declarations page and underwrite flood insurance as a known expense.
- **Schedule pre-construction meeting with rehab contractor before closing** on every value-add deal — scope and costs must be set, not estimated, at close.
- **Cross-reference down unit count** from rent roll, seller verification, and management's post-close audit before locking final occupancy assumptions in the model.

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| T-12 | Trailing 12-month profit and loss statement; shows actual income and expenses over the prior year |
| Loss to Lease | The gap between a unit's market rent and the actual lease rent being collected; represents unrealized income |
| Bad Debt | Rent that was billed but never collected and written off; inflates gross income figures if not stripped out |
| RUBS | Ratio Utility Billing System; a method of billing tenants for a proportional share of common utility costs |
| Down Unit | A unit that is offline and not rentable due to damage, renovation, or vacancy — distinct from a merely vacant unit |
| Deferred Maintenance | Repairs or replacements that have been postponed, often hidden in a property's condition and not reflected in the T-12 |
| Federal Pacific / Stab-Lok Panel | Brands of electrical panels known to have fire hazard defects; a discovery triggers mandatory replacement in most lender and insurance underwriting |
| Aluminum Wiring | Older wiring material prone to oxidation and fire risk; more expensive to remediate than copper |
| Galvanized Plumbing | Older steel pipe coated in zinc; corrodes over time and typically requires full replacement — major CapEx item |
| LOMA | Letter of Map Amendment; a FEMA document that formally removes a property or structure from a Special Flood Hazard Area designation |
| Elevation Certificate | FEMA document that records a building's elevation relative to the base flood elevation; required to accurately price flood insurance |
| Aged Receivables Report | A ledger showing outstanding tenant balances by how long they've been unpaid (30/60/90+ days); reveals true delinquency picture |
| Pre-Construction Meeting | A meeting held with the rehab contractor before closing to finalize scope, schedule, and costs for the value-add renovation plan |
| FF&E | Furniture, Fixtures, and Equipment; movable property in common areas (fitness equipment, pool furniture, etc.) that may need replacement |
| Environmental Report | A Phase I or Phase II environmental site assessment identifying contamination risks (asbestos, lead, mold, radon) on the property |
