## Summary
This document is a sample Contract of Sale for a 31-unit multifamily apartment complex (9 parcels) in Florida, with a purchase price of $5,050,000. It covers the full structure of a multifamily acquisition agreement including property definition, purchase price mechanics, earnest money deposits, loan assumption provisions, and inspection period rights. It is explicitly labeled as a sample requiring attorney review, making it a useful reference template for understanding the key contractual clauses Olive Tree will encounter in any acquisition.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Earnest Money is structured in two tranches** — an Initial Deposit ($50,000) due within 3 business days of the Effective Date, and a Second Deposit ($50,000) due at the expiration of the due diligence/inspection period. Both are fully refundable during the Inspection Period.
2. **The Inspection Period (30 calendar days)** gives the Purchaser absolute termination rights for any reason. Failure to send written notice of termination by the last day constitutes a waiver — this is a hard deadline that must be calendared immediately upon contract execution.
3. **Loan assumption clauses carry significant obligations on the Purchaser** — Purchaser pays all assumption expenses, must cooperate fully with the Lender (tax returns, credit, P&L), and must credit the Seller for any lender-held escrows at closing. Critically, the Seller retains the right to terminate if they are not fully released from the Note, Mortgage, and personal guarantees.
4. **The "and/or assigns" language in the Purchaser entity definition** allows Olive Tree to assign the contract to a new acquisition entity (e.g., a deal-specific LLC) without re-executing the contract — a standard syndication practice.
5. **Purchaser indemnifies Seller for all inspection-related damage**, including attorneys' fees and mechanic's liens. Any invasive testing beyond Phase I environmental and standard geotechnical requires Seller's written consent.
6. **The property definition is broad** — it includes land, improvements, tenant leases, security deposits, prepaid rents, personal property, warranties, licenses, permits, signage, phone numbers, URLs, and the property's trade name. Verify all of these transfer cleanly at closing.

## Action Items
- [ ] **Calendar the Inspection Period deadline immediately** upon signing any PSA. Set a reminder 5 days before expiration to force a go/no-go decision with buffer time.
- [ ] **Negotiate the two-tranche earnest money structure** on all Olive Tree LOIs — keep Initial Deposit small (e.g., $25K–$50K) and tie the hard Second Deposit to the end of the inspection period, not the Effective Date.
- [ ] **Confirm loan assumability before going under contract** on any deal with existing debt. Request the Note, Mortgage, and Lender estoppel as a pre-LOI diligence item where possible.
- [ ] **Always include "and/or assigns"** in the Purchaser name on all contracts to preserve flexibility for entity structuring and syndication.
- [ ] **Do not rely on verbal representations from Seller's inspection representative** — the contract explicitly states Purchaser shall not rely on any representation not set forth in the agreement. All material representations must be documented in writing.
- [ ] **Require Seller to be fully released from personal guarantees** before closing on any assumed loan — failure to confirm this gives Seller a termination right and creates a closing risk.
- [ ] **Catalog all "Other Property" items** (URLs, phone numbers, trade name, service contracts, warranties) during due diligence and confirm they are all included in the transfer or explicitly excluded with price adjustment.
- [ ] **Restore property to prior condition** if contract is terminated after physical inspections have been conducted — budget for this as a potential soft cost.
- [ ] **Give 48-hour advance notice** for all property inspections and coordinate with Seller's designated representative to remain compliant with the contract terms.
- [ ] **Have deal counsel review any PSA before signing** — this sample explicitly warns it must be drafted and reviewed by an attorney.

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Effective Date | The date the contract is fully executed by both parties; starts all contractual clocks (inspection period, earnest money deadlines, etc.) |
| Inspection Period | The due diligence window (30 days in this sample) during which Purchaser may terminate for any reason and receive a full earnest money refund |
| Earnest Money | Good-faith deposits made by Purchaser; credited toward Purchase Price at closing; forfeited if Purchaser defaults after the Inspection Period |
| Initial Earnest Money Deposit | First tranche of earnest money, due within 3 business days of the Effective Date |
| Second Earnest Money Deposit | Second tranche of earnest money, due at the expiration of the Inspection Period; marks the point at which funds go "hard" |
| Hard Money | Earnest money that is non-refundable; typically triggered by expiration of the Inspection Period without termination notice |
| Loan Assumption | Purchaser takes over the existing Seller mortgage, including its terms (rate, maturity, payment schedule), subject to Lender approval |
| Due on Sale Clause | A mortgage provision requiring full repayment upon property transfer; if present, Seller must obtain Lender consent to permit assumption |
| Lender Estoppel | A written certification from the Lender confirming the current principal balance, interest rate, maturity date, and loan status |
| Personal Guarantee Release | Requirement that Seller be removed from all personal liability on the assumed Note and Mortgage before closing is obligated to proceed |
| Tenant Leases | Existing leases between Seller (as landlord) and current tenants; transferred to Purchaser at closing along with security deposits and prepaid rents |
| Personal Property | Seller-owned tangible items on the property (appliances, amenities, equipment) included in the sale |
| Phase I Environmental Assessment | Non-invasive environmental due diligence study identifying potential contamination risk; permitted without Seller consent in this contract |
| Invasive Testing | Any testing beyond Phase I / standard geotechnical (e.g., soil borings, asbestos sampling); requires Seller's written consent |
| Purchaser's Representatives | Any third parties acting on Purchaser's behalf during inspections (engineers, inspectors, consultants); Purchaser is liable for their acts |
| Seller's Inspection Representative | A Seller-designated representative (e.g., property manager) who must be present during all physical inspections |
| And/Or Assigns | Contract language permitting Purchaser to assign the contract to a related entity (e.g., a deal-specific LLC) without Seller's re-approval |
| Closing | The final settlement event at which title transfers, funds are disbursed, and all contract obligations are fulfilled |
| Trade Name | The property's operating name (e.g., "NW 3rd Portfolio") which transfers to Purchaser as part of the sale |
