## Summary
This document is a comprehensive master checklist covering every phase of a multifamily syndication acquisition — from pre-close syndication and investor relations tasks through due diligence, insurance, title, property management onboarding, lender (KPI) tasks, and post-closing investor communications. It is designed to be used as a running task tracker across the full acquisition lifecycle, ensuring nothing is missed between PSA execution and asset stabilization handoff.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Due diligence is exhaustive and layered** — financial DD (T12, rent roll, tax bills, delinquency reports), legal DD (all leases, vendor contracts, warranties), and physical DD (PCA, environmental, ALTA survey, ADA compliance) must all run in parallel during the inspection period.
2. **Entity and legal structure must be established early** — EINs for the property entity, investor entity, and GP entity, plus the PPM, Form D filing, and DocuSign package for investors should be in place before capital is called.
3. **Post-closing handoff is a structured deliverable** — within one week of closing, the asset manager must conduct an on-site welcome meeting, set up weekly renovation calls, and send investors a full closing binder including the settlement statement, loan docs, title policies, third-party reports, and reno budget.
4. **Laundry lease review is flagged as most critical** among vendor contracts — must be cross-referenced with the title commitment Schedule B.
5. **Insurance must be bound at closing** with certificates naming both the lender and management company on all three Accord forms (25, 27, 28).
6. **Capital improvement budgeting is a pre-close lender requirement** — a contracted CapEx schedule and vendor bids must be submitted for lender approval prior to rate lock and commitment letter.

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Form D | SEC filing required to claim a Regulation D exemption for a private securities offering; must be filed within 15 days of the first sale. |
| PPM | Private Placement Memorandum — the legal disclosure document provided to prospective investors in a syndication, outlining the offering, risks, and terms. |
| EIN | Employer Identification Number — a federal tax ID required for each legal entity (property entity, GP entity, investor entity). |
| PSA | Purchase and Sale Agreement — the executed contract between buyer and seller that triggers the due diligence and closing timeline. |
| HAP Contract | Housing Assistance Payments contract — a HUD subsidy agreement tied to affordable/Section 8 units; must be reviewed and assumed at closing. |
| ALTA Survey | American Land Title Association survey — a detailed boundary and improvement survey required by lenders and title companies on commercial transactions. |
| PCA | Property Condition Assessment — a third-party physical inspection report evaluating the condition of all major building systems and estimating deferred maintenance. |
| ADA Compliance Survey | Assessment of the property's compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act; identifies required remediation items. |
| Accord 25 / 27 / 28 | Standard ACORD certificate forms: 25 = Certificate of Liability Insurance; 27 = Evidence of Property Insurance (mortgagees); 28 = Evidence of Commercial Property Insurance. |
| UCC Search | Uniform Commercial Code lien search — confirms whether any creditor has a security interest in the seller's personal property or assets tied to the deal. |
| Loss-Run History | A 5-year record of insurance claims filed on the property; used to underwrite new coverage and identify recurring physical issues. |
| OTP | Owner's Title Policy — title insurance protecting the buyer/ownership entity against defects in title discovered after closing. |
| Rate Lock | Agreement with the lender to fix the interest rate on the loan, typically tied to a good faith deposit and executed shortly before closing. |
| Commitment Letter | Formal lender document confirming loan approval, terms, and conditions precedent to closing. |
| AMP | Asset Management Plan — the post-closing operating and renovation plan executed between the GP and property management company. |
| Sources & Uses | A closing schedule itemizing all capital sources (equity, debt) and uses (purchase price, CapEx, reserves, closing costs, fees). |
| ACM O&M Plan | Asbestos-Containing Material Operations & Maintenance Plan — required when ACM is identified but not removed; governs safe handling procedures. |
| REO | Real Estate Owned — used here in the context of KP (Key Principal) lender reporting; refers to the sponsor's existing real estate portfolio disclosed to the lender. |
