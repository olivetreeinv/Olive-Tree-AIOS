## Summary
This document is the official Multifamily Schooled mentorship roadmap for acquiring a multifamily apartment building within 90 days. It breaks the acquisition journey into 10 sequential stages — from onboarding and business setup through market selection, broker outreach, deal underwriting, LOI submission, contract execution, due diligence, capital raising, and post-close asset management. Each stage includes a prioritized checklist of action items. The document also includes motivational tips and a frank benchmark: expect to review ~150 deals, underwrite ~15, submit ~5 LOIs, and close 1.

---

## Key Takeaways
1. **The funnel is steep — volume is non-negotiable.** The program explicitly benchmarks 150 deals reviewed → 15 underwritten → 5 LOIs → 1 close. Olive Tree must build and maintain a high-volume deal pipeline to generate one closed deal.
2. **Capital must be lined up before a deal is under LOI.** Stage 6 (Capital Foundation) precedes offers — soft commitments, a pitch deck, and lender relationships should all be in place *before* submitting LOIs, not after.
3. **Broker relationships are the primary deal source.** Stages 3, 4, and 7 all reinforce consistent broker outreach, rapport-building, and intelligence-gathering (seller motivation, competitive offer terms) as the core deal-flow engine.
4. **Underwriting cadence is 3–5 deals per week.** Speed and repetition build pattern recognition; slow underwriting is identified as a momentum killer.
5. **Due diligence requires physical presence and multi-trade bids.** Every unit (or a 100% sample), plus independent bids for roof, HVAC, plumbing, and electrical are required to validate CapEx assumptions before closing.
6. **Post-close management is defined by 4 weekly reports.** Unit availability, work orders, delinquencies, and lease expirations/renewals are the core KPIs to track from day one of ownership.
7. **"Manage the manager, not the property."** Ownership leverage comes from PM accountability, not operator involvement in day-to-day tasks.

## Action Items
*Things to apply directly to Olive Tree deals or process:*

- **Set up CRM** with all active broker contacts segmented by market; log every LOI sent and outcome per the Stage 7 protocol.
- **Build a 25-person soft-commitment investor list** now, before any deal is under contract; send a conversational update (not a pitch) to warm the list.
- **Create a standard LOI template** that includes price, earnest money amount, DD period, and closing timeline — confirm terms match norms in each Olive Tree target market.
- **Run 3–5 deals per week through the underwriting analyzer**; submit at least one per week to the team for review to build calibration.
- **Add broker intelligence questions to every broker call:** "What terms matter most to the seller?" / "Where do competitive offers land?" / "What's motivating the seller?"
- **Schedule market trips** for primary target markets (see [[markets/chattanooga-southside]], etc.) — include property tours, 2–3 broker meetings, 2–3 PM company meetings, and 1–3 lender meetings.
- **Identify 2–3 lenders per target market** and request LTV, DSCR, rates, and fee schedules; upload all to CRM.
- **Create a pitch deck using a mock deal** and circulate to the investor list for exposure (not selling) before a live deal is needed.
- **Build a due diligence checklist** pulling from Stage 9: request T12, rent roll, leases, service contracts, CapEx history, bank statements, utility bills, payroll, and delinquency reports on every deal under contract.
- **Establish 4 weekly PM reports** from day one of ownership: unit availability, work orders, delinquencies, lease expirations/renewals.
- **Track leasing velocity, renovation progress, expense control, and CapEx schedule** post-close to confirm alignment with underwriting assumptions.
- **Follow up within 24 hours of every LOI submission** — logged in CRM.

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| LOI (Letter of Intent) | Non-binding offer letter submitted to a seller outlining proposed price, earnest money, DD period, and closing timeline. |
| PSA (Purchase & Sale Agreement) | Legally binding contract converting a signed LOI into a formal purchase obligation; negotiated with a transaction attorney. |
| Due Diligence (DD) | The post-contract verification period during which the buyer inspects the property, audits financials, and confirms all underwriting assumptions. |
| Soft Commitment | An informal, non-binding pledge from a prospective investor indicating interest in funding a future deal — used to size a capital raise. |
| Pitch Deck | A concise investor presentation summarizing the deal thesis, projected returns, market overview, and team credentials. |
| T12 | Trailing 12-month income and expense statement — primary financial document used to verify in-place NOI. |
| Rent Roll | Current schedule of all tenant leases showing unit, tenant, lease dates, and monthly rent — used to verify occupancy and income. |
| CapEx (Capital Expenditure) | Budgeted spending on physical property improvements (roof, HVAC, unit renovations, etc.) — must be verified via contractor bids during DD. |
| DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) | NOI divided by annual debt service; lenders typically require ≥1.20x to qualify a loan. |
| Sensitivity Analysis | Stress-testing underwriting assumptions (exit cap rate, rent growth, vacancy) to assess downside risk and cash flow resilience. |
| PPM (Private Placement Memorandum) | Legal disclosure document required for syndication deals; prepared by a securities attorney and distributed to investors. |
| Syndication | A deal structure in which a sponsor (GP) raises equity from passive investors (LPs) to fund an acquisition. |
| JV (Joint Venture) | A deal structure involving two or more active co-investors sharing ownership, decision-making, and returns — distinct from a syndication. |
| Value-Add | An acquisition strategy targeting properties with below-market rents or deferred maintenance where renovations can force appreciation. |
| Earnest Money | A deposit paid by the buyer upon signing the PSA to demonstrate commitment; typically at risk if the buyer defaults. |
| PM (Property Manager) | Third-party management company responsible for leasing, maintenance, collections, and day-to-day operations on behalf of the owner. |
| Leasing Velocity | The rate at which vacant units are leased — a key post-close KPI for value-add execution. |
