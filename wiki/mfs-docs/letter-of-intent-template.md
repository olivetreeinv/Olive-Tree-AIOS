## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| LOI | Letter of Intent — a non-binding document submitted to a seller outlining proposed purchase price, terms, and conditions before a formal Purchase and Sale Agreement is executed. |
| Earnest Money Deposit (EMD) | Good-faith deposit submitted alongside or shortly after an accepted LOI; typically 1–2% of purchase price and may become hard at contract execution. |
| Due Diligence Period | Negotiated window (commonly 30–45 days) during which the buyer inspects financials, physical condition, and legal title before going hard on the contract. |
| Free Look Period | Portion of the due diligence period during which earnest money remains fully refundable. |
| Closing Timeline | Target date for funding and title transfer; typically 30–60 days post-expiration of due diligence. |
| Contingencies | Conditions that must be satisfied for the LOI/contract to remain binding — e.g., financing, inspection, zoning review. |
