## Summary
This guide walks through a structured framework for vetting and interviewing third-party property management companies during the acquisition process. It covers how to position yourself as an attractive client, what questions to ask across six key areas (company overview, market knowledge, leasing practices, maintenance, and fees), and how to interpret the answers. The document is written for out-of-state investors evaluating managers before or immediately after going under contract on a multifamily property.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Lead with your best property.** Your first call to a PM company is a sales pitch — present a clean, occupied, well-located asset to earn their interest before evaluating them. Avoid leading with a distressed or problem property.
2. **Sweet spot for PM portfolio size is 100–1,000 units.** Below 100 suggests under-resourced; above 1,000 may mean you get deprioritized. Look for a team-based structure with a dedicated point of contact per property.
3. **PM software (Appfolio, Buildium, Yardi, RentManager, Resman) is non-negotiable for out-of-state investors.** Require portal access to income/expense reports, invoices, and before-and-after maintenance photos as part of the management relationship.
4. **Vacancy turnaround benchmarks:** quality units should lease within 2–3 weeks; minor make-ready repairs (paint/flooring) should complete within 30 days; larger renovations within 30–60 days.
5. **Standard fee structure is ~10% of collected rent + 50% of first month's rent for new placements.** Companies offering significantly lower fees are a red flag — prioritize quality over cost.
6. **Start with a low-maintenance property.** Vet the PM on small repairs before trusting them with a value-add renovation scope. This de-risks the relationship early.
7. **If the PM company's owners also invest locally, that is a positive signal** — it indicates they have personal financial incentive to maintain high-quality systems and contractor relationships.

## Action Items
- **Before every acquisition call with a PM:** Frame the property favorably — highlight occupancy, tenant stability, condition, and rent level upfront.
- **Add PM vetting to the acquisition checklist** for every deal in the buy box. Confirm a quality PM exists in the submarket before going under contract.
- **Require portal access (Appfolio, Buildium, or equivalent)** as a contractual condition of any PM agreement — document this in the PM selection criteria.
- **Build a PM interview scorecard** using the six sections of this guide (company overview, market intel, leasing, maintenance, fees, owner-investor status) and score each candidate consistently.
- **Ask the paint-and-flooring ballpark question on every PM call** — a manager who can't give a rough range for a 2BR/900 sq. ft. unit lacks local vendor relationships or transparency.
- **Confirm nonpayment/eviction environment** during PM interviews in every active market — especially relevant in Nashville, Huntsville, and Birmingham where collections norms vary.
- **Do not negotiate PM fees down** unless bringing 3+ properties. Focus conversation on service quality and response time SLAs instead.

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Make-Ready | The process of preparing a vacant unit for a new tenant, typically including paint, flooring, cleaning, and minor repairs. |
| Leasing Fee | A one-time fee charged by the PM for placing a new tenant, typically equal to 50% of the first month's rent. |
| Management Fee | The ongoing monthly fee charged by the PM, typically ~10% of collected (not scheduled) rent. |
| Collected Rent Basis | Fee calculation method where management fees are charged only on rent actually received, not on the scheduled amount — preferable for the owner. |
| Portal | Software platform (e.g., Appfolio, Buildium, Yardi, RentManager, Resman) providing owner access to financials, maintenance logs, and lease documents. |
| Point of Contact (POC) | The specific staff member assigned to manage day-to-day communication for your property. |
| Vendor / Contractor Relationships | Pre-negotiated agreements with maintenance vendors that allow a PM to dispatch reliable workers at competitive rates. |
| Tenant Retention | The rate at which existing tenants renew leases; longer-term tenants reduce vacancy loss and turnover costs. |
| Nonpayment Rate | The frequency with which tenants in a given market or portfolio fail to pay rent on time; a proxy for market health and PM collections effectiveness. |
| Out-of-State Investor | An investor who does not live in the same market as the property; heavily reliant on PM transparency, software access, and photo/video documentation. |
