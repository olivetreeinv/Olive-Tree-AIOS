## Summary
This document is a full deal analyzer worksheet for 3814 Prospect, Cleveland, Ohio 44115 — a 20-unit, all 1+1 multifamily property built in 1966. The analyzer walks through a complete acquisition underwrite at a $1,400,000 offer price (vs. $2,400,000 ask), projecting a value-add renovation from $774/unit to $1,100/unit in rent. It models sources & uses, senior debt, a Year 3 refinance, a Year 6 sale, IRR, equity multiple, cash-on-cash, cap rates, and GP fee structure — making it a practical template for Olive Tree's own deal screening workflow.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Aggressive offer below ask is the move.** Asking price is $2,400,000 ($120K/unit); the model underwrites at $1,400,000 ($70K/unit) — a 42% discount — to make the deal pencil at a 16.24% IRR and 1.81x equity multiple. Chasing the ask destroys returns.
2. **Rent upside drives the entire thesis.** Current rents of $774/unit grow to $1,100/unit pro forma (+42%), lifting NOI from ~$85K to ~$120K and pushing the Year 6 cap rate to 9.84% against a 6.00% exit cap — that spread is where equity is made.
3. **Refinance in Year 3 is a critical liquidity event.** The model projects a cash-out refi returning $230,499 to investors at Year 3, reducing remaining investor capital to $430,101 before the Year 6 sale — this "return of capital early" structure significantly boosts IRR.
4. **Expense ratio discipline matters.** Both current and pro forma expense ratios hold at 51%, with per-unit expenses rising from $4,479 to $6,238 — the model flags that taxes nearly double post-stabilization ($24K → $45K), which is a common gotcha in value-add pro formas.
5. **The 1% rule is met at pro forma rents but not current.** Current monthly income of $15,480 on a $1,400,000 purchase = 1.11% (passes). Pro forma at $22,000 = 1.57% (strong). This confirms rent upside is essential to the thesis, not a bonus.
6. **GP fee stack must be modeled from day one.** The analyzer explicitly captures acquisition fee (2%), asset management (1% monthly), refi fee (1%), renovation fee (7%), and disposition fee (0% in this model) — totaling $51,798 in one-time fees. Leaving these out inflates investor returns.
7. **I/O period on the senior loan improves early cash flow.** The first two years use interest-only payments ($66,717/yr vs. $84,735 P&I), which is why Year 1 cash-on-cash of 2.77% (current) / 8.10% (pro forma) is serviceable before rents are fully pushed.

- **Always model a "deep discount" offer scenario** alongside the ask-price scenario. If the deal only works at 40%+ below ask, that's useful information for LOI strategy or a pass decision.
- **Build in a tax reassessment line item** on every value-add deal. This model shows taxes nearly doubling post-renovation ($24K → $45K); confirm local reassessment triggers and timelines for each target market.
- **Model the Year 3 refi explicitly** in Olive Tree's underwriting template. Leaving out the refi event understates IRR and misrepresents when investors get liquidity.
- **Verify the 1% rule at current rents, not pro forma,** as an initial filter. If current rents don't get you to 0.8%+ on your offer price, the rent-growth assumption is doing too much work.
- **Lock in GP fee structure before presenting deals to investors.** Use this model's fee schedule (2% AQ, 1% AM, 1% refi, 7% reno, 0–5% disposition) as a benchmark and document deviations explicitly.
- **Cross-check expense ratio against T-12.** The 51% expense ratio used here is a model assumption — always validate against actual trailing financials before proceeding past LOI.

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Purchase CAP | Cap rate based on current NOI divided by the purchase/offer price — reflects what you're buying today, not what you're projecting. |
| Yr 1 Proforma CAP | Cap rate based on stabilized (pro forma) NOI divided by the offer price — reflects the upside scenario if rent and expense assumptions are achieved. |
| EGI (Effective Gross Income) | Gross scheduled income minus vacancy/credit loss plus other income — the actual revenue the property is expected to collect. |
| NOI (Net Operating Income) | EGI minus all operating expenses (before debt service) — the core profitability metric used to value the property. |
| DCR / DSCR (Debt Coverage Ratio) | NOI divided by annual debt service — lenders require ≥1.20x; this model shows 1.27x current and 1.80x pro forma. |
| Cash-on-Cash Return (CoC) | Annual cash flow after debt service divided by total equity invested — measures the annual yield on cash deployed. |
| Equity Multiple | Total cash returned to investors (including sale proceeds) divided by total equity invested — 1.81x means $1 invested returns $1.81. |
| IRR (Internal Rate of Return) | Time-weighted annualized return on equity, accounting for the timing of all cash flows (contributions, distributions, refi proceeds, sale) — 16.24% here. |
| LTV (Loan-to-Value) | Loan amount divided by property value — senior loan here is 70% LTV at acquisition, refinanced to 60% LTV in Year 3. |
| Interest Only (I/O) | A loan structure where only interest is paid for a set period (2 years here), reducing early debt service and improving initial cash flow. |
| Sources & Uses | A table showing where acquisition capital comes from (loan + equity) and how it is spent (purchase price + renovation + closing costs). |
| Refinance Cash-Out | Proceeds from a new loan that exceed the payoff of the existing loan, returned to investors as a mid-hold liquidity event. |
| Expense Ratio | Total operating expenses divided by gross scheduled income — used as a quick sanity check on expense assumptions; 51% is the model's stabilized assumption. |
| Return on Cost (ROC) | The incremental NOI generated by the renovation divided by the renovation budget — 47.42% here means every $1 of reno spend returns $0.47/yr in NOI. |
| 1% Rule | A quick filter: monthly gross rent divided by purchase price ≥ 1.0% suggests adequate rent-to-price ratio for cash flow viability. |
| APOD (Annual Property Operating Data) | A standardized one-page income and expense summary used to evaluate a property's current and projected annual performance. |
| Disposition Fee | A GP fee charged at sale, calculated as a percentage of the sale price — modeled at 0% in this deal but standard range is 1–2%. |
| Asset Management Fee (AM Fee) | A recurring monthly fee paid to the GP for managing the asset and investor relations — modeled at 1% of monthly rents here. |
| Acquisition Fee (AQ Fee) | A one-time GP fee charged at closing, typically 1–2% of purchase price — modeled at 2% ($28,000) here. |
| Renovation Fee | A GP fee for overseeing the capital improvement program, typically 5–10% of the renovation budget — modeled at 7% ($11,550) here. |
| Price Per Unit (PPU) | Total purchase price divided by number of units — $70,000/unit at offer; $114,743/unit at projected Year 6 sale
