## Summary
This document is a draft Limited Liability Company Operating Agreement for BPMF Fund 1 LLC, a Wyoming LLC formed in March 2021. It establishes the legal and governance framework for a multifamily investment fund, defining the two-class membership unit structure (Class A Preferred Non-Voting and Class B Voting Common units), manager authority, capital account mechanics, and key definitions governing member rights, distributions, and voting. It is intended to accompany a Confidential Offering Memorandum and is structured for a syndication or fund vehicle targeting real estate acquisitions.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Two-Class Unit Structure:** Class A Preferred Non-Voting Common Membership Units carry no voting rights and are typically issued to passive investors; Class B Voting Common Membership Units retain governance control and are typically held by the GP/Manager. Olive Tree should structure any fund vehicle similarly to protect operational control.
2. **Manager Authority is Broad:** The Manager controls Available Cash determinations, Management Fee terms, officer appointments, and Majority Vote outcomes — effectively giving the GP full operational discretion without investor interference on day-to-day decisions.
3. **Memorandum Governs on Conflict:** In any conflict between the Operating Agreement and the Confidential Offering Memorandum (PPM), the Memorandum controls. This means the PPM is the primary disclosure and governing document — it must be drafted with extreme care before investor closing.
4. **Wyoming LLC Formation:** Wyoming is a favorable jurisdiction for LLC formation due to strong asset protection, privacy, and flexible operating agreement statutes. This is a common choice for fund sponsors.
5. **Ownership Percentage vs. Percentage Voting Interest are distinct:** Ownership Percentage covers all classes (economic rights); Percentage Voting Interest covers only Voting Securities (governance rights). These can and do diverge — LPs may own a large economic share but zero voting rights.
6. **Unregistered Securities Disclaimer:** Units are not registered under the 1933 Act or state securities laws. Transfer restrictions apply. Any Olive Tree fund offering must include proper private placement exemption compliance (Reg D, Rule 506(b) or 506(c)).

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Class A Preferred Non-Voting Common Membership Unit | Membership interest with economic rights (distributions, capital return) but zero voting rights. Typically issued to passive LP investors. |
| Class B Voting Common Membership Unit (Voting Common Unit) | Membership interest with full voting rights. Typically held by the GP/Manager to retain operational control. |
| Available Cash | All cash on hand legally available for distribution, determined in good faith by the Manager. Forms the basis for investor distributions. |
| Capital Account | Each member's running ledger of contributions, allocated income/loss, and distributions — governs tax and liquidation economics. |
| Capital Contribution | Any cash or property contribution made by a member to the company. |
| Majority Interest | More than 50% of all issued and outstanding voting ownership percentages — threshold for member-level decisions. |
| Majority Vote | (i) Members: affirmative vote of holders of Majority Interest; (ii) Manager: vote or written consent of the Manager alone. |
| Manager | The controlling Person of the LLC — not required to be a Member. Holds broad operational authority. Equivalent to GP role. |
| Management Fee | Fee payable to the Manager for operating the fund/company, defined in Section 2.5.3. Amount not specified in this excerpt. |
| Membership Interest / Membership Unit | A member's entire equity interest in the company, regardless of class. Economic and/or voting rights depend on unit class. |
| Officer | Individual appointed by the Manager with delegated responsibilities (CEO, President, etc.). Serves at Manager's pleasure. |
| Ownership Percentage | Each member's pro-rata share of all issued units across all classes — governs economic (not voting) rights. |
| Percentage Voting Interest | A member's share of Voting Securities only — governs governance rights. Distinct from Ownership Percentage. |
| Voting Securities | All membership interests designated with voting rights, including Class B units and any other class listed in Schedule B. |
| Memorandum (PPM) | Confidential Offering Memorandum — the primary disclosure document accompanying the Operating Agreement. Governs on conflict. |
| Wyoming LLC Act | Governing statute for the company's formation and operations; chosen for its flexibility and asset protection features. |
| Treasury Regulations | IRS regulations under the Internal Revenue Code governing tax treatment, including Capital Account maintenance rules. |
| Unregistered Securities | Units not registered under the Securities Act of 1933 or state law — subject to transfer restrictions and private placement rules. |
