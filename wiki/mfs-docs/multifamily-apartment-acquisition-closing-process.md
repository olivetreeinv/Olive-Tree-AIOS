## Summary
This document outlines the end-to-end closing process for a multifamily apartment acquisition, broken into seven sequential phases: Pre-Contract, Entity & Syndication Setup, Due Diligence, Capital & Financing, Closing Preparation, Closing, and Post-Closing Transition. It serves as a high-level operational checklist covering legal, financial, physical, and investor-relations workstreams from offer acceptance through the first days of asset management.

## Key Takeaways
1. **The PSA kicks off every parallel workstream** — entity formation, capital raise, due diligence, and lender engagement must all launch immediately after execution to avoid compressing timelines later.
2. **Due diligence is three-pronged** — financial (rent roll, T12, leases, tax records), physical (inspections, PCR, environmental, survey), and legal/title must all be completed concurrently, not sequentially.
3. **Capital and financing must be finalized before closing prep** — investor commitments collected and loan terms locked (rate lock + good faith deposit) are hard prerequisites to receiving a lender commitment letter.
4. **Closing is a coordination event, not a decision** — by closing day, all decisions are made; the work is executing loan docs, wiring funds, and recording the deed correctly.
5. **Post-closing transition is a distinct operational phase** — property management handoff, investor binder delivery, insurance binding, and reno plan launch are not optional follow-ups; they are structured deliverables.

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| PSA (Purchase & Sale Agreement) | The legally binding contract between buyer and seller that governs the terms of the acquisition, including price, deposit, due diligence period, and closing date. |
| Initial Deposit / Earnest Money | Funds delivered to escrow upon PSA execution as a show of good faith; typically at risk if buyer defaults outside of contingency periods. |
| EIN (Employer Identification Number) | Federal tax ID issued by the IRS; required for each newly formed legal entity before opening bank accounts or receiving investor funds. |
| PPM (Private Placement Memorandum) | Legal disclosure document required for syndication that outlines investment risks, structure, and terms for prospective investors under SEC Regulation D. |
| Offering Memorandum (OM) | Marketing document describing the investment opportunity, the property, the business plan, and projected returns; distributed to prospective investors. |
| Subscription Agreement | Legal document signed by each investor to formally commit capital and confirm they meet investor qualification standards (e.g., accredited investor status). |
| T12 | Trailing 12-month operating statement showing actual income and expenses; a primary financial due diligence document. |
| Rent Roll | Current schedule of all units, tenants, lease terms, and rents; used to verify occupancy and income at the time of acquisition. |
| PCR (Property Condition Report) | Third-party engineering report assessing the physical condition of the property and estimating near-term and long-term capital needs. |
| Phase I Environmental | Third-party environmental site assessment required by most lenders to identify recognized environmental conditions (RECs) on or near the property. |
| Rate Lock | Agreement with the lender to hold a specified interest rate for a defined period; typically requires a non-refundable deposit. |
| Good Faith Deposit (Lender) | Deposit paid to the lender — separate from the earnest money deposit — to cover third-party report costs and signal serious intent to close. |
| Commitment Letter | Formal letter from the lender confirming loan approval and stating final terms; issued after underwriting and third-party reports are accepted. |
| Settlement Statement (HUD-1 / ALTA) | Itemized accounting of all funds flowing at closing — purchase price, prorations, loan proceeds, fees, and net proceeds to seller. |
| Closing Binder | Post-closing package delivered to investors and partners containing executed loan docs, deed, settlement statement, and entity documents. |
| Asset Management | Ongoing oversight of the property post-closing, including financial reporting, renovation execution, leasing performance, and investor communications. |
