## Summary
This document is a redline draft of a Confidential Private Placement Memorandum (PPM) dated August 26, 2021, prepared by Weingold Law Group on behalf of TBD LLC (sponsored by The Brennan Pohle Group, LLC), a California LLC structured for a $3,250,000 equity raise. The offering consists of 65 Class A Preferred Non-Voting Membership Units at $50,000 per unit, sold on a best-efforts basis to accredited investors only. The PPM covers the legal structure, offering terms, investor suitability requirements, risk factors, and securities law disclosures applicable to a private real estate syndication. It serves as a template and reference for structuring a compliant equity raise under SEC Regulation D exemptions.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Best-efforts raise with no minimum close:** The offering has no minimum fundraising threshold — capital is accepted and deployed as it comes in. This means the deal can close underfunded relative to the target, which creates execution risk on the business plan.
2. **Class A = Preferred + Non-Voting:** Passive investors receive a preferred return and economic rights but surrender governance/voting rights to the sponsor/manager. This is standard syndication structure — the sponsor (Class B / managing member) retains full operational control.
3. **Fractionalized units are permitted:** The Company can sell less than one full unit ($50,000 minimum), giving the sponsor flexibility to accept smaller checks and fill the raise.
4. **No placement agent required, but one may be used:** Managers and officers may sell units directly without compensation. A FINRA-registered broker-dealer may be engaged for placement, in which case commissions (including warrants or other securities) are a deal expense.
5. **Offering expenses estimated up to $25,000:** These are deducted from gross proceeds before deployment, reducing effective capital available for the deal.
6. **No secondary market exists or is expected:** Units are illiquid. Investors must be prepared for a complete loss and no liquidity event unless the sponsor creates one (refinance, sale, etc.).
7. **Securities are unregistered under the 1933 Act:** The raise relies on a Reg D exemption (likely 506(b) or 506(c)). Resale restrictions apply — units cannot be freely transferred without registration or a valid exemption.
8. **Sponsors must not make unauthorized representations:** All investor communications must be consistent with the PPM. Side deals, verbal promises, or projections outside the PPM create legal exposure.
9. **Financial projections are inherently uncertain:** The PPM explicitly disclaims accuracy of pro forma projections. Investors must conduct independent due diligence.
10. **Confidentiality obligations on offerees:** Recipients of the PPM are contractually bound to keep its contents confidential and return it if they do not invest.

## Action Items
Things to apply directly to Olive Tree deals or process:
- **Build a PPM template** for Olive Tree raises using this document as a structural reference — retain counsel (securities attorney) to draft the final version; never self-draft a PPM.
- **Decide on offering unit size and minimum investment** for each deal raise before going to market — $50,000/unit is a common floor; evaluate whether Olive Tree's LP base supports this or requires a lower minimum.
- **Define Class A vs. Class B (or equivalent) structure** explicitly in the Operating Agreement: Class A = preferred return + capital priority + no voting; Class B (sponsor) = promoted interest + management authority.
- **Document all investor communications** and ensure nothing said verbally or in email contradicts or supplements the PPM without a formal amendment — this is a securities law compliance requirement.
- **Calculate offering expenses** (legal, PPM drafting, filing fees, placement agent if used) as a line item in every deal's sources and uses — do not assume gross raise = net deployment capital.
- **Confirm accredited investor status** for every LP before accepting a subscription — collect signed Subscription Agreements and supporting documentation (income/net worth verification or third-party confirmation).
- **Use a "best-efforts" structure cautiously** — if the raise closes short of the equity needed, have a contingency plan (bridge loan, reduce CapEx scope, bring in a co-GP) documented in advance.
- **Add a PPM/legal checklist** to Olive Tree's deal pipeline so the capital-raise process is initiated early enough to allow counsel drafting time (typically 3–6 weeks minimum).
- **Review state Blue Sky laws** for each state where LPs reside — Reg D federal exemption does not automatically exempt from state-level securities registration requirements; file Form D and any required state notices promptly.

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Private Placement Memorandum (PPM) | A confidential legal disclosure document provided to prospective investors in a private securities offering. Describes the company, investment terms, risk factors, and use of proceeds. Equivalent to a prospectus for private deals. |
| Class A Preferred Non-Voting Membership Units | Equity interests in an LLC that carry a preferred economic return (paid before other classes) and priority in distributions/liquidation, but grant no voting or management rights to holders. |
| Accredited Investor | An individual or entity meeting SEC-defined wealth/income thresholds (e.g., $1M net worth ex-primary residence, or $200K/$300K annual income). Only accredited investors may participate in certain Reg D offerings. |
| Best-Efforts Offering | A fundraising structure in which the issuer (or placement agent) sells as many units as possible with no guarantee that the full offering amount will be raised and no minimum threshold required to close. |
| Regulation D (Reg D) | SEC rules providing exemptions from the registration requirements of the 1933 Securities Act for private placements. Rule 506(b) allows up to 35 non-accredited sophisticated investors; Rule 506(c) allows general solicitation but requires all investors to be verified accredited. |
| Placement Agent | A FINRA-registered broker-dealer engaged to market and sell securities on behalf of the issuer in exchange for a commission (cash and/or warrants). |
| Blue Sky Laws | State-level securities laws that may require separate registration or notice filings even when a federal Reg D exemption applies. Each state has its own rules. |
| Form D | SEC filing required within 15 days of the first sale of securities under a Reg D exemption. Publicly filed at SEC.gov. |
| Subscription Agreement | The legal contract signed by an investor to purchase units in the offering. Includes investor representations, suitability certifications, and accredited investor status confirmation. |
| Promoted Interest (Promote) | The sponsor's disproportionate share of profits above a preferred return hurdle, earned as compensation for sourcing, managing, and executing the deal. Not explicitly defined here but implied by the Class B / manager structure. |
| Operating Agreement | The governing LLC document that defines member rights, manager authority, distribution waterfalls, voting rights, and transfer restrictions. The PPM references but does not replace the Operating Agreement. |
| Preferred Return | A minimum annualized return (e.g., 7–8%) paid to Class A investors from cash flow or sale proceeds before the sponsor receives any profit share. |
| Fractionalized Units | Units sold in increments smaller than the stated minimum unit size, at the Company's discretion, to accommodate smaller investors or fill out a raise. |
| Securities Act of 1933 | Federal law requiring registration of securities offerings with the SEC unless a valid exemption (such as Reg D) applies. |
| Illiquidity | The condition of an investment that cannot be easily sold or converted to cash. PPM units have no secondary market; investors must hold until a liquidity event (sale, refi, or fund wind-down). |
