## Summary
This document is a draft subscription agreement (Appendix A) for TBD LLC, a California LLC raising up to $3,250,000 through the sale of 65 Class A Preferred Non-Voting Membership Units at $50,000 per unit, offered exclusively to accredited investors under Rule 506(c) of Regulation D. It establishes the legal framework governing how investors subscribe for units, the representations and warranties investors must make, transfer restrictions on the securities, and the conditions under which the offering is accepted or rejected. While the entity and deal details are placeholders ("TBD"), the document serves as a reusable template illustrating standard private placement subscription agreement language applicable to multifamily syndications.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Best-efforts offering structure** — No minimum raise threshold is required to close the offering. Proceeds become available to the company upon receipt and clearance, meaning capital can be deployed on a rolling basis rather than waiting for a full raise.
2. **Rule 506(c) exemption** — The offering relies on the general solicitation exemption, which requires that ALL investors be verified accredited investors. This is a higher compliance bar than 506(b), which allows up to 35 non-accredited sophisticated investors and prohibits general solicitation.
3. **Irrevocability of subscription** — Once submitted, the subscription is irrevocable by the investor (subject to state securities law exceptions), but the Company retains sole discretion to accept, reject, or modify any subscription — giving the sponsor significant control over the investor roster.
4. **Transfer restrictions are strict** — Units cannot be transferred without prior written approval from Company counsel, and stop-transfer instructions will be issued. Investors must understand these are highly illiquid securities with no guaranteed resale market.
5. **Fractionalized units are permitted** — The Company reserves the right to accept investments below the $50,000 minimum by selling fractional units, giving flexibility to accommodate smaller check writers.
6. **Insiders may co-invest** — Officers, directors, advisors, employees, existing interest holders, and their affiliates may purchase units on the same terms as outside investors. This should be disclosed clearly in the PPM.
7. **Accredited investor self-certification + verification required** — Under 506(c), passive reliance on investor self-certification is insufficient; the sponsor must take reasonable steps to verify accredited investor status (tax returns, W-2s, CPA/attorney letter, etc.).

## Action Items
- **Build a 506(c) vs. 506(b) decision framework** for each Olive Tree raise — default to 506(b) if no general solicitation is planned, to avoid mandatory accredited-investor verification burden.
- **Draft or obtain a standard subscription agreement template** reviewed by securities counsel before the first Olive Tree LP/LLC offering. Use this document as a structural reference, not a final form.
- **Create an investor onboarding checklist** that includes: (1) subscription agreement execution, (2) accredited investor verification (if 506(c)), (3) wire transfer instructions, (4) countersignature by sponsor, and (5) delivery of fully executed agreement to investor.
- **Add transfer-restriction language to Operating Agreement** for all Olive Tree LLC entities — units are not freely transferable; require manager written approval consistent with this template.
- **Do not rely on investor representations alone under 506(c)** — establish a third-party verification process (e.g., Parallel Markets, VerifyInvestor) before launching any general solicitation campaign.
- **Note fractionalization option** — when closing a raise that is slightly short of a full unit increment, the ability to accept fractional units prevents leaving small amounts of committed capital on the table.
- **Ensure PPM and subscription agreement are consistent** — representations in the sub agreement (especially risk tolerance and accreditation) must mirror disclosures in the accompanying Memorandum.

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Subscription Agreement | Legal contract by which an investor offers to purchase securities in a private company; becomes binding when countersigned by the issuer |
| Class A Preferred Non-Voting Membership Units | Equity interest in an LLC that carries economic rights (distributions, preferred returns) but no voting or governance rights |
| Accredited Investor | Per SEC Rule 501 of Reg D: individual with $200K+ income ($300K joint) or $1M+ net worth excluding primary residence, or certain entities |
| Rule 506(c) | SEC exemption under Regulation D permitting general solicitation of securities offerings, provided all purchasers are verified accredited investors |
| Rule 506(b) | SEC exemption permitting sales to up to 35 non-accredited sophisticated investors and unlimited accredited investors, but prohibiting general solicitation |
| Best-Efforts Offering | Offering structure with no minimum raise required to close; proceeds are available to the issuer as received, without an escrow release trigger |
| Regulation D | SEC regulation providing safe-harbor exemptions from Securities Act registration for private placements |
| Rule 144 | SEC rule governing resale of restricted and control securities; sets holding periods and volume limits for routine secondary sales |
| Offering Memorandum (OM) / PPM | Confidential Private Placement Memorandum disclosing risks, terms, and business plan of a private securities offering |
| Stop-Transfer Instruction | Direction from issuer to its transfer agent (or recorded in company books) to block unauthorized transfers of restricted securities |
| Irrevocable Subscription | Investor's commitment that cannot be withdrawn after submission, unless state law provides an exception |
| Fractionalized Units | Units sold at less than the stated minimum denomination; requires explicit authorization in the offering documents |
| General Solicitation | Broadly advertising a securities offering to the public (e.g., social media, websites, seminars); only permissible under 506(c) with verified accredited investors |
