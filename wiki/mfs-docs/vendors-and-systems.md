## Summary
This document provides a curated reference list of software systems and vendor contacts recommended for running a multifamily syndication business. It covers tools for accounting, investor CRM, website, design, document management, and deal sourcing, alongside a vetted directory of attorneys (transaction, securities, business), CPAs, bookkeepers, cost segregation specialists, lender brokers, and insurance brokers — each with direct contact information.

## Key Takeaways
1. A lean syndication tech stack (QuickBooks + Syndication Pro + Canva + Zapier) can handle most back-office and investor management needs without custom software.
2. You need at minimum three types of attorneys: a transaction attorney for closings, a securities attorney for PPM/offering docs, and a business attorney for entity structuring — these are distinct roles and should not be consolidated carelessly.
3. Cost segregation studies are a standard tool to accelerate depreciation on acquisitions; vendors are pre-vetted and ready to engage at the deal level.
4. Multiple lender broker relationships (Hannah Capital, Walker & Dunlop, MBP Capital, Groves Capital) give optionality for sourcing agency debt, bridge, and creative financing structures.
5. Crexi is listed as the preferred deal sourcing platform with a named contact, making it the primary inbound deal flow channel to prioritize.

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Syndication Pro | CRM and investor portal platform purpose-built for real estate syndicators; manages investor contacts, distributions, and offering documents. |
| PPM (Private Placement Memorandum) | Legal disclosure document prepared by a securities attorney required when raising capital from outside investors under SEC exemptions (e.g., Reg D 506(b) or 506(c)). |
| Cost Segregation | An IRS-approved engineering study that reclassifies components of a real property into shorter depreciation schedules (5, 7, or 15 years), accelerating tax deductions for the owner. |
| Registered Agent | A designated individual or entity authorized to receive legal and government documents on behalf of an LLC; required in every state where the LLC is registered. |
| Lender Broker | A mortgage broker specializing in commercial real estate debt who shops loan terms across multiple lenders (banks, agencies, debt funds) on behalf of the borrower. |
| Soft Quote | A preliminary, non-binding loan estimate provided by a lender broker used for early-stage underwriting before a full loan application is submitted. |
| Agent of Service | The entity or person designated to accept legal process (lawsuits, subpoenas) on behalf of a business entity; often the same as the registered agent. |
| Zapier | A no-code automation tool that connects software applications via triggers and actions, allowing data to flow between platforms without manual entry. |
| Crexi | Commercial Real Estate Exchange — an online marketplace and broker platform for listing and sourcing commercial properties including multifamily. |
| Anderson Advisors | A business and tax advisory firm commonly used by real estate investors for entity structuring and asset protection strategy. |
