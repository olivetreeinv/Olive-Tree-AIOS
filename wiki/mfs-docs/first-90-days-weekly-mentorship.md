## Summary
This document is a structured 13-week (plus bonus week) onboarding guide for new multifamily investors enrolled in a mentorship program. It walks through a week-by-week action plan covering market selection, deal underwriting practice, broker outreach, LOI submission, syndication setup, due diligence, and closing — with motivational tips and common mistake warnings woven throughout. The guide is designed to take a new investor from zero to a closed first deal within 90 days by enforcing consistent deal volume, relationship building, and parallel business infrastructure setup.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Deal funnel math is non-negotiable:** On average, you must explore ~150 opportunities, analyze ~15, submit ~5 LOIs, and close just 1 deal. Olive Tree should expect and budget for that attrition rate in its pipeline cadence.
2. **Velocity over perfection:** The guide mandates analyzing 3 new deals per week from Week 1 onward. Consistent deal review — even simulated — builds underwriting instinct faster than any single deep dive.
3. **Broker relationships are the primary deal source:** The program front-loads broker tours, follow-ups for off-market deals, and CRM population. Brokers are treated as the #1 pipeline source, not listing sites.
4. **Business infrastructure runs in parallel, not before:** LLC setup, website, logo, and legal docs are built *during* active deal pursuit — not as prerequisites. This prevents analysis paralysis.
5. **Escrow/due diligence window is 60–90 days:** This is the operational runway to complete inspections, finalize syndication docs, secure lending, and raise investor capital simultaneously.
6. **80/20 rule applies:** Most time and energy should concentrate on the highest-leverage activities — broker calls, deal analysis, investor conversations — not administrative tasks.
7. **Motivation is a practiced skill:** The guide treats motivation as a system (goals, visualization, environment, community) rather than a personality trait, which is relevant for a small acquisitions team under deal drought pressure.

## Action Items
Things to apply directly to Olive Tree deals or process:

- **Set up Crexi.com** as a primary deal sourcing feed for all 10 buy-box markets; filter to 15–50 units.
- **Establish a weekly deal review cadence** — target analyzing at least 3 deals per week across active markets using the pro forma template.
- **Build and maintain a broker CRM** — add 10+ broker contacts per active market; log every touch in [[brokers/]] pages; pursue off-market deal requests proactively on every broker follow-up call.
- **Schedule property tours immediately upon identifying a target** — do not wait for full underwriting before making broker contact.
- **Prepare LOI templates in advance** so LOIs can be submitted within the same week a deal clears initial underwriting screens; reference [[mfs-docs/]] for LOI best practices when available.
- **Track pipeline attrition explicitly:** Log all prospects in `_log.md` so Olive Tree can measure its own funnel ratio (prospects → analyzed → LOI → under contract → closed) against the 150:15:5:1 benchmark.
- **Prepare a 1-page tear sheet and pitch deck** for any deal approaching LOI stage to accelerate investor conversations in parallel with due diligence.
- **Use Loom or similar video tools** to communicate live deal updates to prospective investors during the escrow period.
- **Open a dedicated property LLC and bank account** (BofA recommended per guide) at the start of the escrow period — do not wait until close.
- **Engage a syndication attorney early** — at or before LOI acceptance, not after the purchase contract is signed.
- **Attend at least 2–3 local REI meetups per month** and add every contact to the CRM with a follow-up drip.
- **Resist rushing into a bad deal** to hit an arbitrary timeline — the guide explicitly warns against this as the #1 common mistake alongside inaction.

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Crexi.com | Commercial real estate listing marketplace used for deal sourcing; primary recommended platform in the guide for finding multifamily listings |
| Pro Forma | Forward-looking financial model projecting income, expenses, NOI, and returns for a prospective deal; used to simulate underwriting during practice weeks |
| LOI (Letter of Intent) | Non-binding written offer submitted to a broker or seller outlining proposed price and key terms before a formal purchase contract |
| Deal Analyzer | Underwriting spreadsheet tool used to assess deal viability; referenced throughout the guide for weekly practice |
| Pitch Deck | Investor-facing presentation summarizing a deal opportunity, including financials, market data, business plan, and projected returns |
| Tear Sheet (1-pg) | Single-page deal summary distributed to prospective investors or partners as an initial teaser before the full pitch deck |
| Escrow | The period between an executed purchase contract and closing during which due diligence, financing, and syndication are finalized; typically 60–90 days |
| Due Diligence (DD) | The investigative process during escrow covering physical inspections, financial review, legal review, and third-party reports |
| Syndication | The pooling of investor capital under a legal structure (typically an LLC or LP) to acquire a property; requires SEC-compliant documentation prepared by a syndication attorney |
| CRM | Customer Relationship Management system; used to track broker contacts, investor leads, and deal follow-ups (Wix CRM and SyndicationPro referenced) |
| SyndicationPro | Software platform for managing investor relations, capital raises, and syndication workflows |
| Property Management (PM) | Third-party operator hired to manage day-to-day operations of a multifamily asset; guide recommends sourcing PM referrals directly from brokers |
| LLC (Wyoming/Delaware) | Recommended legal entity formation states for real estate holding companies due to favorable privacy laws (WY) and legal structure flexibility (DE) |
| Account Manager (AM) | Mentorship program support contact who reviews underwriting submissions, guides the escrow process, and helps troubleshoot deal issues |
| 80/20 Rule | Pareto principle applied to real estate: 80% of results come from 20% of activities — broker calls, deal analysis, and investor conversations |
| Deal Funnel | The sequential pipeline from initial prospect identification through analysis, LOI, contract, and close; guide benchmarks at 150 → 15 → 5 → 1 |
