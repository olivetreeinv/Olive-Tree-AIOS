Where Capital Is Flowing

→ What It Signals for 2026

In private CRE, the most honest data isn't what investors say they believe. It's where they actually put money.

A new Agora analysis — 1,000+ investment firms, 150,000+ investors, $300 billion in AUM — reveals where capital moved in 2025.

The Southeast pulled away

The region's share of total returns jumped from 28.57% in 2024 to 40.51% in 2025. A 12-percentage-point swing in one year.

The Southeast led capital raised at 42.06%. The Northeast? Just 21.38% of returns — half the Southeast's share.

Florida and Texas are money

Florida: 18.14% of all capital contributions, 10.72% of total returns (highest of any state).

Texas: 11.32% of contributions.

The top 10 states captured 64.36% of total investments and 66.92% of total returns.

Multifamily dominated, industrial surged

Multifamily: 48.61% of capital raised, 40.31% of total returns.

Industrial (including self-storage): 22.44% of total returns despite just 2.94% of capital raised. The sharpest return-to-capital ratio in the dataset.

Industrial's share of quarterly returns climbed from 8.05% in Q1 to 35.98% in Q4 — nearly matching multifamily by year-end.

The 2026 outlook adds momentum

74% of investors plan to buy more CRE assets than last year. 55% are increasing capital allocations (up from 48% in 2025).

Debt funds now account for 37% of closings. Multifamily rent growth projected at 2%, with higher gains in Florida and Texas.

The bottom line:

The data shows where capital has already decided to go. Conviction capital is concentrating in fewer places and around fewer asset classes.

For GPs raising in 2026, these are the benchmarks.

🏠 What Makes an Offer Stand Out in Today's Market

→ Today's market may be balancing out, but strong offers still matter during busy spring season.

Here's what separates winners from the rest:

Pre-approval strength matters

Not all pre-approvals are equal. Fully underwritten pre-approvals (income verified, credit pulled, documentation reviewed) win deals. Automated pre-qualifications get ignored.

Earnest money shows commitment

Standard is 1-2% of purchase price. In competitive situations, 3-5% earnest money signals serious intent — especially paired with strong pre-approval.

Flexible closing timelines

Ask your agent what the seller needs. Match their timeline. Flexibility is strategic positioning, not weakness.

Clean contingencies reduce risk

Don't waive protections recklessly. Structure them strategically:

  • Shorten inspection period from 14 days to 7 (shows urgency, keeps protection)

  • Offer to cover small appraisal gaps ($5K-$10K) to reduce seller risk

Work with a local agent

Every market is different. A local agent knows what wins deals in your area — comps, seller priorities, competitive strategies.

You don't need the highest price. You need the strongest package.
