Spring Sellers Have an Edge. Here's Why.

→ Why Rising Buyer Demand, Stronger Offers, and Faster Sales Make This the Most Powerful Season to List Your Home

Homeowners looking to sell want three things: buyers, strong offers, and speed.

Spring delivers all three.

1. More buyers are looking

Spring is peak moving season. ShowingTime data proves buyer activity peaks every year in spring.

This year? Mortgage rates are near 3-year lows.

More buyers + improving affordability = more eyes on your house.

Redfin confirms: "Homebuying demand is improving, and mortgage-purchase applications are near their highest level in three years."

More activity = more opportunity to close.

2. You'll get more offers

NAR data from the last three years shows sellers in spring consistently receive more offers than any other season.

Realtor.com: "Spring brings out buyers ready to move before summer. Listings see more views, showings, and offers."

That's good for your bottom line.

3. Homes sell 20 days faster

On average, homes sell 20 days faster in spring vs. winter. Almost three weeks shaved off your timeline.

Since homes have been taking longer to sell lately, listing during peak season sets you up to move quickly.

The reality:

Spring doesn't guarantee a sale. Strategy still matters.

But this season gives you momentum: more buyers, more activity, more opportunity.

If you're selling this year, why not do it when the odds are in your favor?

🏠 Multifamily Investor Confidence Is Rising Again

→ Buyer Sentiment Strengthened in Late 2025 as Stable Cap Rates and Improving Financing Brought Investors Back

For most of 2023-2024, multifamily investors stayed on the sidelines. Cap rates compressed. Financing costs doubled. Deal flow froze.

Late 2025 changed that.

Investor confidence started climbing. Not because rates dropped dramatically—but because uncertainty cleared.

What shifted:

Cap rates found a floor – After two years of repricing, cap rates landed at 5.5-6.5% and held. Pricing clarity emerged. Deal flow returned.

Financing became accessible – Agency debt stabilized. HUD/FHA multifamily rates dropped to 5.18-5.67%—the lowest in years. Deals penciled again.

Institutional capital re-entered – After sitting on billions, institutional investors started deploying in Q4 2025. Private investors followed.

What this means for 2026:

Confidence is rising. Deal flow is accelerating. Capital is moving.

Winners are executing on:

  • Value-add properties with operational upside

  • Secondary markets with population growth

  • Stabilized assets in supply-constrained areas

They're not betting on appreciation. They're betting on cash flow at current rates.

The strategic advantage:

Early 2026 is the window before everyone else catches on.

Investor confidence is rising. But it hasn't peaked yet.

That gap—between rising confidence and peak competition—is your opportunity.

Spring won't have less competition. Q2 won't have better pricing.

The move is now.
