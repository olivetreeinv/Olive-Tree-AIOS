July 2026 — Multifamily

July brings the clearest split in multifamily in years — and which side of it you're on depends entirely on which zip code you're buying.

Here's what's happening: CoStar just revised year-end national vacancy up to 8.8%. Rent growth nationwide sits at 0.5% for Q2. The Sun Belt markets that dominated the last decade are now the worst performers. Austin inventory is up 32% since 2023. Phoenix, Dallas, and Orlando are right behind it. GlobeSt is reporting 20–30% price resets on workforce and Class C assets in the most oversupplied markets.

Here's what the market isn't telling you: Atlanta is forecast at 4.1% rent growth for 2026 — second-highest of any major metro in the country. 19,000 new jobs are coming to metro Atlanta this year, fourth-highest nationally. New construction is dropping by 8,400 units versus 2025. Vacancy sits at 6.4%.

Translation: Jobs coming in. Supply pulling back. That equation only runs in one direction — rents go up.

The "Sun Belt is strong" thesis drove a decade of capital. It's over as a blanket statement. The new game is surgical. A well-located Atlanta asset is outperforming its Sun Belt neighbors by 3–4 percentage points of rent growth annually right now. That gap compounds fast in a 4-to-6-year hold.

At Olive Tree, we've always underwritten by submarket, not by metro. Specific zip codes in Georgia, Tennessee, and Alabama — workforce housing where job growth is real and supply hasn't caught up. The markets we operate in don't make the national headlines. That's exactly why the deals still make sense.

GlobeSt said it plainly this month: micro-market underwriting is "the difference between outperforming and quietly bleeding." Five miles apart, two buildings — one raising rents 4%, the other giving away two months free. We're buying the one raising rents.

The window where Atlanta stays under the institutional radar won't stay open. Position now.

July 2026 — Single Family

July brings a reality check for buyers who've been waiting for rates to fall.

Here's what's happening: The Fed held rates at 3.50–3.75% on June 17. The real news wasn't the hold — it was the dot plot. The committee erased its last projected cut for 2026 and pushed reductions to 2027 at the earliest. The 30-year fixed is sitting at 6.5%. It's staying there.

Here's what the market isn't telling you: every buyer waiting for rates to drop just lost their thesis. July is one of the lowest-competition windows of the year. Spring traffic has cleared out. The pool of competing buyers just shrank.

Translation: The best time to buy isn't when rates are low — it's when everyone else is convinced they should wait. That's right now.

The math on waiting is worse than most buyers think. Every month on the sideline is another month of rent paid with zero equity building. And when rates eventually move, every buyer currently sitting out jumps back in at the same time.

If you qualify today and the payment works today, July is the right time to move. Less competition. More negotiating room. Sellers who are actually motivated.

The buyers who get the best deals buy when everyone else is hesitating.
