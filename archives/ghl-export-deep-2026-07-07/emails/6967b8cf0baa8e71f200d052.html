More Buyers Are Planning To Move in 2026. Here's How To Get Ready

→ Practical Strategies for Preparing Your Home or Finding Your Next One as Buyer Demand Rises

Momentum is building.

New NerdWallet data shows 17% of Americans plan to buy a home in the next 12 months—up from 15% last year. In a market that's been cooling for years, that 2% jump signals a shift.

What this means: Early movers win. Late movers compete.

Planning to move in early 2026? Do these 4 things:

1. Get pre-approved – Understand your buying power at today's rates. Just know: pre-approvals expire in 30-90 days, so time this right.

2. Run the numbers – Calculate your actual budget. Factor in all expenses and your future mortgage payment. Don't stretch too far.

3. Define your non-negotiables – Location, commute, layout, school district, lifestyle needs. Get clear now, decide faster later.

4. Choose your agent early – Find one you trust who understands pricing, competition, timing, and strategy—not just someone who shows homes.

Buying later in 2026? Prepare now.

  • Work on your credit – Pay down debt, make payments on time. Your score impacts your loan terms and rate.

  • Automate your savings – Set up automatic transfers. Remove the temptation to spend it elsewhere.

  • Lean into side hustles – Part-time work, freelance gigs—anything that boosts savings faster.

  • Put windfalls to work – Tax refunds, bonuses, gifts? Straight to your house fund.

The bottom line:

This is the largest year-over-year increase in buyer intent since rates spiked.

The market is waking up. Position yourself now, or compete with everyone else in spring.

🏠 How Multifamily Survived and Built Momentum in 2025

→ Key Trends, Deals, and Strategies That Kept Multifamily Resilient Through a High-Rate Environment

2025 threw everything at multifamily. The sector absorbed 506,000+ new apartments—the most since the 1980s—yet emerged resilient.

🌊 Supply met demand

Over half the new units landed in the South, Texas cities led construction. Despite historic supply, the rental market stayed competitive: Rental Competitiveness Index hit 75.2, up from 74.4 in 2024.

Miami topped the nation with 19 renters competing per vacant apartment—double the national average.

Translation: Supply came. Demand absorbed it. No collapse.

📈 Rent growth recovered

National rents hit $1,755 in March, up 1.0% year-over-year after early-year declines.

New York: +5.5%. Chicago and Kansas City: +3.7%. Columbus: +3.5%.

Renewal rates climbed to 63% nationwide—renters stayed put, keeping competition tight.

🗺️ Geographic split widened

Sun Belt metros (Austin, Phoenix, Tampa) felt pressure from peak deliveries.

Supply-constrained markets saw pricing power return. Washington, D.C. added 23,000 downtown apartmentssince 2020 and topped livability rankings.

💰 Capital surged back

Office-to-apartment conversions broke records: 70,700+ units created from former offices in 2025. D.C. led with 6,533 units in the pipeline.

Transaction volume rebounded—Q4 2025 saw deal volume surge 51% year-over-year as buyers and sellers agreed on valuations.

The bottom line:

Multifamily enters 2026 with momentum.

For investors:

  • Cap rates: 5.5-6.5% (real returns)

  • Conservative underwriting (protects downside)

  • Weak operators exited (less competition)

  • Institutional capital returned (deal flow is back)

The market absorbed massive supply and still performed. That's resilience.
