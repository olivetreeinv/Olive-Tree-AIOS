Bonus Depreciation Returns

→ Why this tax change might be the most profitable update of the year

The new House tax bill has revived 100% bonus depreciation, retroactive to January 1, 2023, and extended through 2025. If you own or plan to acquire capital-heavy properties, this changes everything.

Here’s why this matters:

✅ You can now deduct the entire cost of qualifying assets—from HVAC systems to parking lot improvements—in the year they’re placed in service.
✅ Cost segregation studies just became dramatically more valuable. Breaking out short-lived assets in your property can unlock six- or seven-figure deductions.
✅ It’s retroactive, so if you made qualifying investments in 2023, you may be able to amend your return and get a refund.

But there’s a deadline. The 100% rate drops again in 2026, and unless extended, this opportunity phases out completely by 2027.

Investor takeaway:
Use this window wisely. Review your 2023 acquisitions, plan upcoming projects, and talk to your CPA or tax strategist about maximizing accelerated depreciation while it lasts.

Getting a Mortgage in 2025

→ What single-family buyers need to know about lending this summer

Yes, interest rates are still elevated—but that’s not the whole story. If you're thinking about buying, refinancing, or holding off, here's what really matters right now:

What lenders are looking for:

  • DTI (Debt-to-Income): Most lenders want it under 43%.

  • Credit Score: Aim for 740+ to access top-tier terms.

  • Documentation: Self-employed or investor? Prepare two years of returns, bank statements, and a clear financial picture.

What smart buyers are doing:

  • Exploring ARM options: A 5/1 ARM may offer lower upfront rates if you plan to refinance later.

  • Negotiating closing costs or rate buydowns with motivated sellers.

  • Locking based on affordability, not fear. Waiting for rates to drop isn’t always a winning bet—especially if prices rise in the meantime.

Bottom line:
Don’t let rates alone make your decision. If the home fits, and the payment makes sense, your future self will thank you.
