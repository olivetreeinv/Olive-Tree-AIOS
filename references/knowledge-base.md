## Quick Reference — Key Numbers

*Read this section first. Covers 80% of what skills need. Read deeper sections only for full checklists or edge cases.*

**Deal thresholds (acquisition screening floors — minimums, not targets):**
- Cash-on-cash: ≥6% by year 3–4 | Property IRR: ≥16% | DSCR: ≥1.25 | Equity multiple: ≥1.8x | LP IRR: ≥14%
- 75% Rule: all-in cost < 75% of stabilized value
- 10x NOI Rule: purchase price ≤ 10× NOI

**Deal structure (standard):** 6% pref → 70/30 LP/GP. Target: 18.21% annual ROI, 2.09× equity multiple.

**Buy box:** 15–50 units, $1M–$3M, value-add only. Active markets → `references/buy-box.md`.

**Stage 4 cadence:** Underwrite 3–5 deals/week → submit 1–3 LOIs/week. Funnel: 150 reviewed → 15 deep-dives → 5 LOIs → 1 deal.

**Market filters:** Pop growth ≥1%/yr | Job growth ≥2%/yr | Rent growth ≥3%/yr | MHI ≥$56K.

**CapEx rule:** $1k reno → $200–$300/mo rent increase to pencil. Bridge loan: non-recourse, 36-mo I/O, 1% lender fee.

## The 10-Stage Deal Pipeline

The full acquisition process from first contact to ownership. Each stage has a checklist in the sections below.

| Stage | Name | Core Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Onboarding | Get plugged in — systems, calls, community |
| 2 | Business-in-a-Box | Become a legal operator — LLC, CRM, branding |
| 3 | Market Selection | Choose 2–3 markets, lock in 3 broker relationships |
| 4 | Brokers & Deal Flow | Underwrite 3–5 deals/week, submit 1–3 LOIs/week |
| 5 | Market Trip | 3-day in-person trip — tours, brokers, PMs, lenders |
| 6 | Capital Foundation | 25 soft-commit investors, pitch deck sent, lender intros |
| 7 | Offers & Negotiation | LOI submitted, terms negotiated, LOI signed |
| 8 | Under Contract | PSA executed, legal looped in, DD timeline set |
| 9 | Due Diligence & Capital Raise | Physical + financial DD, investor outreach, lender lock |
| 10 | Managing & Ownership | Closed — transition, PM setup, reno launch, asset management |

**The numbers:** On average, expect to explore 150 opportunities → deep-dive 15 → submit 5 LOIs → close 1 deal. One deal can yield hundreds of thousands. It's a treasure hunt.

## Stage 3 — Market Selection

**Goal:** Choose your investment territory and begin deal flow.

- Use AI for market selection (see `references/market-research-prompt.docx`)
- Choose 2–3 markets to explore — use Crexi to identify active brokers
- Make contact through active listings → build relationships → progress to off-market
- Lock in 3 broker relationships per market before moving forward
- Select your primary market

## Stage 4 — Brokers & Deal Flow

**Goal:** Build deal analysis skill and broker momentum.

### Weekly cadence:
- Underwrite 3–5 deals/week using the Deal Analyzer
- Send deals to team/mentor for review
- Submit 1–3 LOIs/week
- Build ongoing rapport with brokers (even when LOIs lose)

### Broker strategy:
1. **Crexi** — Use on-market listings to identify brokers in the market, then convert to off-market
2. **LinkedIn** — Search "commercial real estate broker [city/state]"
3. **Conferences** — Buy VIP/premium tickets to get in front row and VIP dinners (where real deals happen)
4. **offerd.com** — Direct-to-seller marketing, off-market deal sourcing
5. **thebrokerlist.com** — Directory of commercial RE brokers
6. **Equity/debt brokers** — Money finders and loan officers are sources of off-market deals
7. **Direct-to-seller** — Guerrilla marketing; requires consistency and small team
8. **Investor partnerships** — Other investors bring deals; find them at meetups and conferences
9. **Acquisitions analyst** — Hire someone (LinkedIn, networking events) to find deals nationally

### Key broker questions to ask:
- *"What terms matter most to the seller?"*
- *"Where do competitive offers usually land?"*
- *"What's motivating the seller?"*

## Stage 5 — Market Trip (3-Day Format)

**Goal:** Validate the market in person before committing capital.

- Schedule 3 property tours
- Meet 2–3 brokers in person
- Meet 2–3 property management companies
- Meet 1–3 local lenders
- Create social proof / story content from the trip

## Stage 6 — Capital Foundation

**Goal:** Build the financial engine so your deal can be funded when you get one under LOI.

### Investor pipeline checklist:
- [ ] Build list of 25 soft-commitment investors
- [ ] Reach out with conversational update about your investing plans
- [ ] Hold 3–5 short investor intro calls
- [ ] Create simple pitch deck (mock deal is fine)
- [ ] Send pitch deck to list of 25 (no selling — just exposure)

### Lender pipeline checklist:
- [ ] Identify 2–3 local lenders in chosen market
- [ ] Introduce yourself (email + call)
- [ ] Request loan programs and terms (LTV, DSCR, rates, fees)
- [ ] Upload lenders into CRM
- [ ] Build 2–3 ongoing lender relationships
- [ ] (Optional) Secure syndication attorney for future deal docs

### Content:
- [ ] Publish 2–3 pieces of social proof content — "touring deals" style

## Stage 7 — Offers & Negotiation

**Goal:** Analyze deals quickly and submit LOIs that get attention.

### Underwriting checklist:
- [ ] Underwrite 3–5 deals/week
- [ ] Identify rent comps and verify renovation assumptions
- [ ] Run sensitivity analysis (DSCR, cash flow, exit cap)

### LOI submission checklist:
- [ ] Create personal LOI template (see `references/` for sample)
- [ ] Submit 1–3 LOIs/week
- [ ] Confirm LOI terms match market norms (DD period, earnest money, closing time)
- [ ] Ask broker about seller motivation and competitive offers
- [ ] Follow up within 24 hours of sending each LOI
- [ ] Log all offers in CRM

### Negotiation targets:
- Price
- Earnest money (amount + hard/soft schedule)
- Due diligence period length
- Closing timeline

## Stage 8 — Under Contract

**Goal:** Convert signed LOI into a legally binding PSA and prep for due diligence.

### Legal checklist:
- [ ] Begin PSA negotiations
- [ ] Loop in transaction attorney
- [ ] Review and negotiate: inspection timelines, reps & warranties, closing conditions, earnest money terms
- [ ] Finalize deal structure (JV, syndication, or hybrid)

### Capital raise kickoff:
- [ ] Prepare pitch deck for the real deal
- [ ] Record deal summary video (optional but powerful)
- [ ] Set up investor landing page

### Document requests from seller:
- [ ] T12 (last 12 months P&L)
- [ ] Rent roll
- [ ] Leases
- [ ] Service contracts
- [ ] CapEx history

### Closing infrastructure:
- [ ] Coordinate with lender for term sheet and document requirements
- [ ] Build due diligence timeline
- [ ] Confirm closing attorney + title company

### Entity & syndication setup (run concurrently):
- [ ] Form legal entities (Property LLC, GP, LP/investor entities)
- [ ] Obtain EINs for each entity
- [ ] Open bank accounts
- [ ] File Form D (SEC)
- [ ] Create PPM
- [ ] Create operating agreement
- [ ] Create subscription agreement
- [ ] Create DocuSign version for investors
- [ ] Create wire instructions

## Stage 9 — Due Diligence & Capital Raise

**Goal:** Verify the numbers, the property, and raise the equity needed to close.

### Physical due diligence (2–5 days on site)

**Inspection team to schedule:**
- Property inspector (generalist + plumber + electrician)
- Roofer
- HVAC contractor
- Contractor for rehab/renovation bids
- Termite inspector
- Pool inspector (if applicable)
- Landscaper

**Walk the property:**
- [ ] Walk every unit (or 100% sample)
- [ ] Pay attention to down/vacant units — verify count
- [ ] Document all deferred maintenance
- [ ] Have pre-construction meeting prior to close — plan and costs finalized before closing

**Systems to inspect:**
- [ ] Scope sewer lines
- [ ] HVAC units (age, condition)
- [ ] Water heaters
- [ ] Boilers / furnaces
- [ ] Roof (age, last replacement, leaks in last 5 years?)
- [ ] Windows
- [ ] Siding, stucco, paint
- [ ] Termite
- [ ] Foundation and drainage
- [ ] Electrical panels (flag Federal Pacific or Stab-Loc — must replace)
- [ ] Wiring type (aluminum vs. copper)
- [ ] Plumbing type (galvanized vs. copper)
- [ ] Fire sprinkler system (last serviced, any code violations)
- [ ] Pool, play yard, common areas, FF&E
- [ ] Landscape, entrance, gates

**Third-party reports to order:**
- [ ] Environmental assessment (Phase I, Phase II if needed)
- [ ] Zoning compliance report
- [ ] Asbestos survey + ACM O&M Plan if needed
- [ ] Property condition assessment

**4 things to assess on initial tour (before contract):**
1. Location — how does it feel?
2. Personnel — staff, tenants, property manager
3. Processes — what software and systems are they using?
4. Pricing to market — who is the competition?

### Financial due diligence

- [ ] T-12 (profit/loss for last 12 months)
- [ ] All 12 months of utility bills
- [ ] Copies of all vendor contracts
- [ ] Copies of all leases
- [ ] Verification of down units, loss to lease, bad debt, non-paying tenants
- [ ] Verification of rent deposits and other income (bank statement match)
- [ ] Verification of RUBS (utility reimbursables)
- [ ] Verification of CapEx expenditures and repair expenses
- [ ] All current work orders + last 24 months of work orders
- [ ] Updated bank statements
- [ ] Utility bills (full history)
- [ ] Payroll
- [ ] Delinquency reports

**Key questions for seller:**
- Any major plumbing/sewer repairs in last 5 years?
- Is electrical to code? Any city code violations?
- Age of roof — when replaced? Full or partial? Any leaks in last 5 years?
- How many Section 8 / housing assistance units?
- Age/replacement of water heaters, boilers, furnaces, condensers?
- Aluminum or copper wiring?
- Federal Pacific or Stab-Loc panels?
- Galvanized or copper plumbing?
- Fire sprinkler: last serviced? Any code violations?
- Fire extinguishers: last serviced?
- Radon or lead paint?
- Any foundation issues, retaining wall, subterranean water, waterproofing issues in last 5 years?

### Capital raise checklist:
- [ ] Begin active investor outreach
- [ ] Send pitch deck + summary video
- [ ] Host 1–2 investor calls / Q&A sessions
- [ ] Track soft-commits and verify allocation amounts
- [ ] Send weekly updates to investors
- [ ] Prepare all legal docs (PPM, subscription docs)
- [ ] Coordinate signatures and capital raise timeline
- [ ] Ensure all funds scheduled to wire before closing

### Financing checklist:
- [ ] Work with lender on appraisal + underwriting
- [ ] Lock rate + finalize lender terms
- [ ] Ensure all funds scheduled to wire before closing

## Stage 10 — Closing & Asset Management

**Goal:** Take control of the asset and execute the business plan.

### Closing day:
- [ ] Final walk-through
- [ ] Review closing statement line-by-line
- [ ] Execute loan docs
- [ ] Title transfer & deed recorded
- [ ] Wire funds
- [ ] Close the deal

### Post-closing (first 90 days):
- [ ] Notify investors with closing binder + settlement docs
- [ ] Schedule weekly calls with property manager
- [ ] Get access to PM software owner portal
- [ ] Request 4 weekly reports: unit availability, work orders, delinquencies, lease expirations/renewals
- [ ] Begin 90-day business plan execution
- [ ] Kick off renovations (if value-add)
- [ ] Establish monthly lender reporting
- [ ] Visit property once a month for first 90 days → quarterly after stabilization
- [ ] Bind insurance + update lender/partners

### Manage the manager — not the property. Track weekly:
- Leasing velocity
- Renovation progress
- Expense control
- CapEx schedule

### KPIs to verify against underwriting:
- Occupancy rate vs. underwritten occupancy
- Actual rents vs. projected rents
- Expenses vs. budget
- Renovation timeline vs. plan

## Financial Underwriting Metrics

These are the hard thresholds Brian uses to evaluate deals. A deal that doesn't hit these metrics is a pass.

### Market-level filters (before underwriting the deal):
| Metric | Minimum |
|---|---|
| Population growth | 1%+ per year |
| Job growth | 2%+ per year (diverse providers) |
| Rent growth | 3%+ per year |
| Median household income | $56,000+ |
| Employer base | Diverse — no single-employer towns |

### Deal-level filters:
| Metric | Threshold | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cash-on-cash return | ≥6% by year 3–4 | Screening floor (after reno + stabilized); target higher |
| Property IRR | ≥16% | Levered, 5–6 yr hold |
| Equity multiple | ≥1.8x | Screening floor; target 2.09x |
| LP IRR | ≥14% | Needs LP waterfall (6% pref → 70/30) — not yet modeled |
| 75% Rule | All-in cost < 75% of stabilized value | Hard filter |
| 1% Rule | Avg monthly rent/unit > 1% of purchase price/unit | Quick sanity check |
| 10x NOI Rule | Purchase price ≤ 10x NOI | Implies ≥10% cap rate on purchase |
| DSCR | ≥1.25 | Debt service coverage ratio |

**20:1 NOI ratio:** For every $1 in NOI = $20 in value (assumes 5% cap rate). Use to estimate value.

**CapEx return rule:** For every $1 spent on renovations, target a $1.20–$1.30/mo rent bump (20–30% monthly rent increase from the capital invested). If the cap rate is 5%, $1 in monthly rent = $12/yr NOI = $240 in value. So $1k in reno should produce $200–$300/mo in rent increase to justify.

### Deal structure — Olive Tree standard:
| Type | Pref Return | Split | GP Equity | LP Equity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Value-add | 6% | 70/30 (LP/GP) | 10% | 90% |
| Stabilized acquisition | 6% | 70/30 (LP/GP) | 10% | 90% |
| Ground-up development | 3–6% | 50/50 | 10% | 90% |
| Higher-upside value-add | 8% | 70/30 (LP/GP) | 10% | 90% |

Post-hurdle: 15% IRR hurdle → then 50/50 split.

### Fee schedule:
| Fee | Amount |
|---|---|
| Property management | 3–5% of gross revenue |
| Asset management | 1–2% of gross income |
| Acquisition fee | 1–3% of purchase price |
| Capital raise fee | 3% |
| Construction/reno management | 5–8% of hard costs |
| Builder fee | 5–6% of hard costs |
| General conditions (construction projects) | 7–8% of hard costs |
| Developer fee (entitlement projects) | 3% of total project cost |
| Lender fee | 1% |
| Loan broker fee | 0.5% |
| Total closing costs (typical) | 3–4% of purchase price (includes lender fees, title, escrow, inspections, survey, environmental, legal, cost seg, acquisition fee, working capital) |
| Working capital reserve | 1–2 months gross revenue |

### CapEx sequencing:
1. Deferred maintenance first
2. Cosmetic upgrades second (beautify the property)
3. Vacant units first for renovations
4. Then set calendar for unit turns

### Debt financing (value-add):
**Bridge loan (preferred for value-add):** Acquire → renovate → stabilize → refinance within 3–4 years into agency debt (Freddie Mac or Fannie Mae).

Typical bridge terms: Non-recourse, 36-month I/O, 1% lender fee, 0.5% broker fee, 6-month extension option at 0.5%.

## Glossary — Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Absorption Rate** | % of newly completed units that are leased over a given period |
| **Cap Rate** | NOI ÷ Property Value — unlevered % return on a property. Lower = more expensive. |
| **Capital Stack** | All debt + equity sources combined to fund an acquisition |
| **Cash-on-Cash Return** | Annual pre-tax cash flow ÷ total cash invested |
| **Concession** | Incentive given to a tenant (e.g., 1 month free rent) to lease or renew |
| **DSCR** | Debt Service Coverage Ratio = NOI ÷ Annual Debt Service. Must be >1.20 for most lenders |
| **Equity Multiple** | (Total net profit + equity invested) ÷ equity invested. Brian's target: 2.09x |
| **Fair Market Rent** | 40th percentile rent for a given market area (HUD calculation) |
| **GPR (Gross Potential Rent)** | Total rent collected if 100% occupied at market rates |
| **IRR** | Internal Rate of Return — discount rate that makes NPV = 0. Brian's target: 15–20%+ |
| **Loss to Lease** | Difference between market rent and actual contract rent for leased units |
| **NOI** | Net Operating Income = Gross Revenue − Operating Expenses (before debt service) |
| **Preferred Return** | LP investors get this % return on invested capital before any GP profit share |
| **RUBS** | Ratio Utility Billing System — method for billing tenants for shared utilities |
| **T-12** | Trailing 12 months of income/expense history — the first document you request in DD |
| **Value-Add** | Strategy of buying underperforming asset, renovating, raising rents, and refinancing/selling at a higher valuation |
| **Vacancy Rate** | % of units that are unoccupied at a given time |
| **Bridge Loan** | Short-term financing (2–3 years) used during value-add renovation before refinancing into permanent debt |
| **Agency Debt** | Long-term permanent financing from Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac — obtained after stabilization |
| **Basis Points** | 1/100th of a percentage point. 100 bps = 1% |
| **LIHTC** | Low Income Housing Tax Credit — government subsidy for affordable housing |
| **Mezzanine Debt** | Subordinate debt that sits between senior debt and equity in the capital stack — higher cost, higher risk |
