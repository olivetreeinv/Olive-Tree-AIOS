## Source Stack

### Tier 1 — Daily Read (Always Pull First)

| Source | URL | What to use it for |
|---|---|---|
| **CRE Daily** | credaily.com | Morning CRE briefing — multifamily, industrial, lending, proptech. Best single source for deal-relevant headlines. Also covers market reports and CoStar data summaries. |
| **GlobeSt** | globest.com/sectors/multifamily | Multifamily-specific coverage — vacancy trends, rent growth, regional market shifts, investor sentiment. Strong on Southeast and Sun Belt stories. |

### Tier 2 — Pull When Touching Underwriting or Markets

| Source | URL | What to use it for |
|---|---|---|
| **Reuters** | reuters.com | Macro headlines — Fed decisions, credit markets, inflation, jobs, geopolitical events. **Always pull** when a major world event is breaking — wars, sanctions, trade disputes, and energy shocks all ripple into construction costs, cap rates, and debt markets. |
| **WSJ (Real Estate + Economy)** | wsj.com/real-estate + wsj.com/economy | Institutional-grade coverage of CRE trends, capital markets, and policy. Also covers major government moves (tariffs, tax policy, housing legislation). Good for LP-facing content that needs credibility. |
| **Bloomberg** | bloomberg.com | Rate and credit depth — Treasury moves, SOFR, spread changes, CMBS market. Use any time a blog touches debt, refinancing risk, or cap rate compression/expansion. Also best source for tracking how geopolitical events transmit into bond markets and borrowing costs. |
| **AP News** | apnews.com | Breaking news on major government and world events — wars, legislation, executive orders, policy shifts. Use when a story is too big to miss and Reuters hasn't fully fleshed it out yet. Fast and unbiased. |

### How Big Events Connect to Multifamily Underwriting

When a major macro or geopolitical event breaks, evaluate it through these lenses before deciding whether to write about it:

| Event Type | CRE Impact to Flag |
|---|---|
| **War / geopolitical conflict** | Energy prices → utility costs for operators; supply chain disruption → construction/renovation cost increases; flight to safety → Treasury yields drop → potential cap rate compression |
| **Tariffs (steel, lumber, appliances)** | Renovation budgets increase; basis per unit rises; value-add deals get tighter; new construction slows (less competition for existing stock) |
| **Fed decisions / rate moves** | Direct impact on agency debt (Fannie/Freddie), bridge loan rates, refinance risk, cap rate expectations — always worth a blog angle |
| **Immigration / labor policy** | Construction labor supply and cost; property management staffing costs in value-add markets |
| **Tax legislation** | Depreciation rules, 1031 exchanges, Opportunity Zones, carried interest — LP and GP economics both affected |
| **HUD / FHFA policy** | Agency loan caps, underwriting standards, MIP changes on FHA/HUD loans — directly affects available debt for 15–50 unit deals |
| **Housing bills / zoning reform** | Supply pipeline changes; rent control expansion risk in target markets |
